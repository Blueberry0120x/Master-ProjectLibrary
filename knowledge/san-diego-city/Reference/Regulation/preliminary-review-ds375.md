# DS-375 / IB-513 — Preliminary Review (City of San Diego)

**Scope:** How to prepare, audit, and submit a City of San Diego **Preliminary Review (PR)** application (DS-375) without burning a cycle. Includes a mandatory pre-submittal audit pattern derived from real-project findings (PRJ-1155895).

> **Why this file exists.** A prior submittal we helped prepare came back with format-compliance, discipline-tag, and numerical-consistency issues that would have triggered a "review may be delayed" bounce or silent non-response to ~30% of the questions. This file is the institutional fix — run the audit below on every DS-375 before it leaves the office.

---

## 1. What DS-375 Is

- **Form:** DS-375 "Preliminary Review Questionnaire"
- **Policy document:** Information Bulletin **IB-513** "Preliminary Review" (procedural rules)
- **Purpose:** Get early, non-binding written direction from DSD disciplines on **specific project questions** before paying full submittal fees. Not a substitute for entitlements.
- **Fee basis:** Charged per discipline selected. Each discipline has ~4 hours budgeted to research and respond.
- **Time:** 4–6 weeks typical turnaround. No guaranteed deadline.
- **Output:** A written Preliminary Review Letter (PRL) from each selected discipline.
- **Not binding:** Answers reflect current code interpretation; they do **not** vest rights unless paired with a separate vesting instrument (SB 330, vesting tentative map, development agreement).

## 2. IB-513 Hard Rules (the format reviewer will bounce on)

| Rule | Requirement | Common failure |
|---|---|---|
| Question cap | **Max 10 questions** total across all disciplines. Cap is on **questions, not disciplines** — identify as many disciplines as the project plausibly needs. | Conflating "10 questions" with "10 disciplines," or sneaking 11–12 questions as sub-parts |
| Discipline tag per question | Each question must be tagged with **one or more** disciplines that should respond. Tag multiple when a question requires joint input — e.g., `[Structural / Fire Plan Review]` for an alteration needing both fire-resistive construction and suppression-system guidance. Reviewer's preferred format: "Planning/Engineering for Question #1; Mapping for Question #2." | Tagging a discipline you never identified on the form — the PM can only route to disciplines the applicant identified |
| Disciplines identified | Identify **every** discipline that could own any part of any question. **After submittal, the assigned Project Manager selects the applicable disciplines from what you identified.** You cannot amend the DS-375 after it is submitted. | Under-identifying: any un-listed discipline has no owner and the corresponding question is dropped |
| No post-submittal edits | Once submitted, you cannot add/remove disciplines or revise questions. Fixing material errors requires a **new** PR submittal (new fee, new clock). | Realizing during review that Water/Sewer or Transportation Development should have been listed |
| Construction data (Section 7) | Complete for every building scope: sprinklers Y/N, egress, Type of Construction, Occupancy (existing / proposed), SF existing / proposed, grading Y/N, earthwork CY, ROW improvements | Left blank — Engineering and Structural cannot answer |
| Planning data (Section 8) | Community Plan area, base zone, 45-year structure Y/N, MHPA Y/N, stormwater flag Y/N, parking ratio, deviations / dev. incentives | Left blank — Planning will mark "incomplete" |
| SB 330 pathway | If selected, a **separate SB 330 Preliminary Application (DS-3032)** with its own Sections A–Q must be attached; otherwise vesting is **not preserved** | Checking the SB 330 box but failing to attach DS-3032 |
| Cover sheet = questionnaire | Numbers on the cover must match the questionnaire exactly | Cover says FAR 1.4, Q4 says 0.89 — instant completeness bounce |

## 3. Disciplines You Can Identify (and what they actually cover)

> **Rule of thumb: identify broadly, tag precisely.** The DS-375 discipline picker has ~15 options. Identify **every** discipline that could own any part of any of your 10 questions — the PM will select the applicable ones after submittal. Under-identifying is the costlier error: the PM cannot engage a discipline you never listed, and you cannot edit the form post-submittal.
>
> **Per-question tagging:** a single question may be tagged to one **or more** disciplines when the answer requires joint input. Reviewer-preferred format:
> - `Q1 [DSD-Planning Review / DSD-Engineering]`
> - `Q5 [DSD-Structural / DSD-Fire Plan Review]` — building alteration touching both fire-resistive construction and suppression
> - `Q7 [DSD-Map Check]` — single-owner question
>
> Fees are assessed based on which disciplines the **PM engages**, not merely those listed. Thorough identification does not automatically stack fees; it simply preserves your ability to get an answer.

| Discipline on DS-375 | What it owns |
|---|---|
| **DSD-Combined** | Building (structural, mechanical, plumbing, electrical) + basic water/sewer **for SFR and room additions only**. For anything ≥ 3 DU or commercial, add DSD-Water/Sewer separately. |
| **DSD-Water and Sewer** | Multi-unit, commercial, tenant improvement water/sewer capacity, sewer loading, water meter sizing, fire flow demand review |
| **DSD-Engineering** | Grading, drainage, stormwater, ROW, dedication, public improvement plans, erosion control, floodplain |
| **DSD-Transportation Development** | Driveways, curb cuts, sidewalk, traffic study scope, **parking count review including AB 2097 transit exemption**, bike parking layout |
| **DSD-Planning Review** | Zoning compliance, setbacks, height, FAR, density, use permissibility, SDMC 142/143 regulations (incl. **SDMC 142.0525 bike parking**), Community Plan consistency, Design Review eligibility, overlays (Coastal, CPIOZ, Airport, MHPA) |
| **DSD-Fire Plan Review (SDFD)** | Fire access, hydrants/flow, sprinkler threshold, aerial apparatus access, standpipe, FDC, smoke control, brush management zones |
| **DSD-Geology** | Slope stability, fault proximity, liquefaction, bluff setback, geologic hazard; triggers IB-515 preliminary geotech |
| **DSD-Map Check / Subdivision** | Tentative parcel/tract maps, condo conversion maps, lot line adjustments, lot consolidations — including **SB 9 urban lot split map** |
| **DSD-Historic** | 45-year review (IB-580), local/state/national listing, Mills Act, HRB referral; **photographic survey requirement lives here** |
| **Planning-Facilities Financing** | DIF (Development Impact Fee), TIF, RTCIP (SANDAG), FBA (Facilities Benefit Assessment), Park Impact (PDIF), school fees coordination, affordable housing in-lieu fee estimate |
| **Planning-Environmental Analysis (EAS)** | CEQA scoping: CE finding feasibility, MND/EIR path, noise, air, biology, cultural resources |
| **Coastal** | Coastal Development Permit review (if in Coastal Overlay) — height limits, public access, blueline |
| **Airport Authority (SDCRAA) referral** | ALUCP compatibility (Review Area 1 / 2), noise attenuation, FAA Part 77 Form 7460-1 screening |
| **Public Utilities — Recycled Water** | Purple-pipe requirement for irrigation ≥ threshold acreage in designated area |

### Routing anti-patterns (PRJ-1155895 audit)
- **AB 2097 parking / bike parking** → route to **Planning Review** (parking policy) + **Transportation Development** (layout). *Not* DSD-Combined.
- **Water/sewer capacity for ≥ 3 DU** → **DSD-Water and Sewer**. *Not* DSD-Combined.
- **DIF / HTF / RTCIP / FBA fee estimate** → **Planning-Facilities Financing**. *Not* DSD-Combined or Engineering.
- **45-year / historic / photo survey (IB-580)** → **DSD-Historic**. Always confirmed in Section 8.

## 4. SB 330 Pathway (critical for housing projects)

- **Governing instrument:** the separate **DS-3032 SB 330 Preliminary Application**, not DS-375.
- **What it vests:** the ordinances, policies, fees, and standards **in effect the day DS-3032 is deemed complete**, for **≥ 30 months** while the project moves through entitlement/permit.
- **Required attachments** (SB 330 Sections A–Q): project site info, adjacent land uses, existing units, proposed units (incl. DU breakdown by BR count), density, affordable units by level, height, square footage, parking, community plan area, APN, tract/lot, existing structure demo list, tree removals, vehicle trips, and more. A missing letter = not vested.
- **DS-375 + SB 330** is the normal combo: use DS-375 to ask the 10 hardest questions, attach DS-3032 so answers are anchored to today's code.
- **Hearing cap:** SB 330 caps entitlement hearings at **5**. Applies regardless of DS-375.
- **Rezoning:** SB 330 does **not** authorize rezoning; you must still follow the zone's process. But no city may reduce density or downzone during the vesting period.

## 5. Pre-Submittal Audit Pattern (run this on every DS-375)

This is the institutional checklist. A single failure in any row triggers revise-before-submit.

### 5.1 Format compliance

- [ ] Exactly **10 questions** or fewer. (11+ = rejection.)
- [ ] Each question tagged `[Discipline-Name]` and the discipline is **selected** on the application.
- [ ] Section 7 construction data fully populated (not blank).
- [ ] Section 8 planning data fully populated (not blank).
- [ ] SB 330 box consistent with DS-3032 attachment (either both present or both absent).

### 5.2 Discipline-tag cross-check

For every question `Qn [Discipline-X / Discipline-Y / ...]`:
- [ ] **Every** discipline tagged on the question is **identified on page 1 of DS-375**. (The PM can only engage disciplines you identified.)
- [ ] Each tagged discipline actually owns a piece of the subject matter (see §3 table).
- [ ] Joint-discipline questions use the `[X / Y]` format (slash or "and"), e.g., `[Structural / Fire Plan Review]` — not a single merged label.
- [ ] Section 1 discipline identification is **inclusive** — add every discipline that any question touches, even obliquely. It is safer to identify a discipline the PM declines to engage than to miss one.
- [ ] Reviewer-format example present somewhere in the cover letter, e.g., "Planning/Engineering for Question #1; Mapping for Question #2; Structural/Fire for Question #5."
- [ ] **Last-chance sanity:** you cannot edit after submittal. Re-read all 10 questions and ask "is every named discipline on my Section 1 list?"

### 5.3 Numerical consistency (cover sheet ↔ questionnaire ↔ exhibits)

Flag any of the following — fix before submittal, not after:
- [ ] **Lot area** is identical on cover sheet, Q-list, development standards table, and matches ZAPP / Assessor (cite source).
- [ ] **FAR**: proposed value identical across cover and questions; **max allowed** matches SDMC Table 131-05B / 131-04B for the cited zone (e.g., CC-3-9 base max = 2.0 per Table 131-05B, *not* 3.0).
- [ ] **Building SF**: existing and proposed identical across cover narrative, Q-list, and any exhibit footprint dimension (40'×42' = 1,680 SF cannot coexist with a 6,576 SF existing building).
- [ ] **Setbacks**: required vs proposed vs existing — if proposed deviates, mark **"existing nonconforming, pending confirmation"** in the standards table — do **not** pre-claim "COMPLIES" unless code section literally permits it.
- [ ] **Lot coverage**: cell labeled "max" (not "min"); proposed value consistent; compared to zone max per SDMC table.
- [ ] **Height**: consistent across cover + Q-list + elevations; structural vs architectural projection flagged per SDMC 131.0461.
- [ ] **Unit count / density**: proposed DU count is identical everywhere; density check uses correct base formula (e.g., 1 DU / 400 SF in CC-3-9 × lot area).
- [ ] **Parking**: required count uses current SDMC + AB 2097 exemption if applicable; proposed count matches site plan.

### 5.4 Overlay / permit triggers that are commonly missed

Flag and ask a question (or get a written non-applicability letter) for each that the site hits:

| Overlay / trigger | Source | Add a question if… |
|---|---|---|
| **ALUCP Airport Influence Area / Review Area 1 or 2** | SDCRAA ALUCP (SAN, Montgomery, Brown, Gillespie, Palomar, Ramona) | Site is inside any AIA; R-2 or sensitive use → noise attenuation design mandatory |
| **FAA Part 77 notification surface** | 14 CFR 77.9 | Top of structure elevation is within ~200 ft of a Part 77 surface; need Form 7460-1 or written finding |
| **Inclusionary Housing Ordinance** | SDMC 142.13 | Any project creating **≥ 2 net new dwelling units** (verify current threshold) — six-figure in-lieu fee or on-site path |
| **Slope ≥ 25% / Sensitive Biology** | SDMC 143, ESL regs | Geographic data flags slope = 1; triggers IB-515 preliminary geotech via DSD-Geology |
| **Stormwater Priority Development Project (PDP)** | SDMC 142.0154, Storm Water Standards Manual | Adding ≥ 10,000 SF new/replaced impervious; 4+ DU residential subdivision; also Standard Project category in 2,500–9,999 SF band |
| **ROW dedication / public improvements** | SDMC 142.05, City Council Policy 600-33 | Fronting arterial (e.g., El Cajon Blvd); almost always triggers curb/gutter/sidewalk/ADA ramp/driveway upgrade and sometimes dedication |
| **Change of occupancy under CEBC Ch. 10** | CEBC §1012 | Conversion between groups (S-1 → R-2, M → R-3, etc.); ask whether CEBC Ch. 10 prescriptive path is accepted and what upgrades are mandatory |
| **CBC 11A / 11B Accessibility** | CBC Ch. 11A (covered multifamily) + 11B (common areas, leasing, ROW) | ≥ 3 newly created R-2 units (11A applies); always applies to leasing/common/parking (11B) |
| **Condo / tentative parcel map intent** | SDMC 125, 144 + SMA | Any intent to sell units separately or split lots; ask Map Check early (concurrent processing potential) |
| **Legal existing structure / Certificate of Occupancy** | SDMC 129.0202; CEBC | Adaptive reuse or "legal nonconforming" claim — confirm CofO on record or the nonconforming premise collapses |
| **Coastal Overlay** | SDMC 126.0700 | Site in Coastal Zone — CDP process, 30 ft (or 26 ft beach) height cap, public view corridors |
| **MHPA / ESL** | SDMC 143 | MSCP Multi-Habitat Planning Area on or adjacent to site; biology survey required |
| **Historic Resource** | SDMC 123; IB-580 | Any structure ≥ 45 years old — IB-580 photographic survey + potential HRB referral |

### 5.5 Question-quality check

Reject any question that:
- [ ] **Self-answers from ZAPP or SDMC Tables** (e.g., "Is R-2 permitted in CC-3-9?" — read Table 131-05A instead).
- [ ] **Mixes two scopes** that cannot both be true ("remodel" vs "vertical addition" — pick a lane and match the exhibits).
- [ ] **Pre-claims compliance** the reviewer is supposed to grant ("the wall is legal nonconforming and therefore…" — let the reviewer make the finding).
- [ ] **Has no discipline owner** on your selected list.

## 6. The Golden Swap (when you're at 10 and still need critical coverage)

Rank every question by **"cost of wrong answer at DSD intake"** descending. Keep the top 10. Kill anything ZAPP/SDMC already answers.

High-value questions that usually belong on the list for a 4-unit R-2 infill in Greater North Park / Mid-City:
1. Historic (IB-580) — confirmed non-historic or HRB path
2. Change of occupancy — CEBC Ch. 10 acceptability + required upgrades
3. Fire — sprinkler basis (NFPA 13R vs 13), aerial apparatus access, FDC
4. Utilities — sewer EDU capacity, water meter size, fire flow
5. Parking — AB 2097 exemption documentation + bike parking (SDMC 142.0525)
6. DIF / HTF / RTCIP / FBA estimate — from Facilities Financing
7. **ALUCP** compatibility (Review Area 1 or 2) + noise attenuation
8. **Inclusionary Housing** pathway (in-lieu fee vs on-site)
9. **Stormwater** — Standard Project or PDP classification
10. **ROW** — dedication + public improvement scope on fronting arterial

(Map Check, 11A/B accessibility, and geotech/IB-515 replace any of the above if the project specifics make them more material. Pick one; don't stack.)

## 7. Post-Submittal Protocol

- Save the assigned **PR case number** and each discipline's assigned reviewer.
- Log the 4-hr discipline budget clock mentally — if one reviewer is silent at week 3, politely email to confirm receipt.
- PRL responses: treat each answer as **direction, not approval**. Incorporate into the full submittal narrative and cite the PRL in the cover letter.
- If SB 330 / DS-3032 was filed concurrently, confirm **SB 330 Determination Letter** issued separately.
