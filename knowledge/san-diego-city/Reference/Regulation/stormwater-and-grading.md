# Stormwater & Grading — City of San Diego

Authority: **Transportation & Stormwater Department — Stormwater Division**
(MS4 permit administration) + **DSD Grading/SWQ reviewers** (project
compliance). Regulatory backbone: **Regional Water Quality Control Board
Order R9-2013-0001 (as amended)** — San Diego Region MS4 Permit.

## Controlling documents

- **Storm Water Standards Manual** — current edition **2018**; amendments
  rolled in thereafter. Lives on DSD Land Development Manual webpage.
  - Part 1: **BMP Design Manual** (design + sizing)
  - Part 2: Construction BMP Standards
- **SDMC Chapter 14 Article 2 Division 2 — Grading Regulations**

## Project categorization by impervious surface

| Category | New / replaced impervious | Requirements |
|----------|---------------------------|--------------|
| **Standard Project** | < 2,500 sf (not in ESA) | Site-design + source-control BMPs; **WPCP** (construction) |
| **Standard Project — middle tier** | 2,500 – 9,999 sf | Adds Trash Capture; source + site design; construction WPCP |
| **Priority Development Project (PDP)** | **≥ 10,000 sf** new or replaced impervious (or specific land uses — see below) | Structural treatment control BMPs + hydromodification BMPs + full SWQMP + operation & maintenance agreement + SWPPP |

### PDP trigger categories (Manual §1.4) — a project is PDP if ANY apply:

1. New/replaced impervious ≥ 10,000 sf.
2. Detached SFD with ≥ 10,000 sf impervious (rare — big estates only).
3. Restaurants.
4. Hillside (slope ≥ 25%) with ≥ 5,000 sf impervious.
5. Parking lots ≥ 5,000 sf or ≥ 15 stalls.
6. Streets/roads ≥ 5,000 sf contiguous.
7. Retail gasoline outlets.
8. Automotive repair shops.
9. Projects discharging to ESA (Environmentally Sensitive Area) with ≥ 2,500 sf.

**Redevelopment rule:** If ≥ 50% of existing impervious is replaced/altered
and total new+replaced ≥ 10,000 sf, the whole site becomes PDP. Under 50%,
only the altered footprint triggers PDP.

## BMP hierarchy

All projects must apply in this order (Manual §5):

1. **Site design BMPs** — preserve drainage, minimize impervious, disperse
   runoff, self-retaining areas (SD-1 through SD-8).
2. **Source control BMPs** — covered trash, landscape-drainage separation,
   storm drain stenciling (SC-1 through SC-11).
3. **Pollutant Control BMPs (Treatment Control)** — biofiltration is the
   **default** treatment for PDPs (PR-1 biofiltration; INF-1/2/3 infiltration
   where feasible). Proprietary treatment (HDS, cartridge filters) allowed
   when biofiltration is infeasible.
4. **Hydromodification Management BMPs (HMP)** — flow-duration / volume
   control for PDPs in HMP-applicable watersheds (most of the City).
   Exempt: directly to tidal waters, hardened conveyance to ocean.

## Watershed Management Areas (WMA)

| WMA | Reach |
|-----|-------|
| San Diego River | Mission Valley, Santee, El Cajon |
| Mission Bay / La Jolla | PB, OB, La Jolla |
| San Dieguito | North County coastal (not all in City) |
| Peñasquitos | Rancho Peñasquitos, Mira Mesa, Sorrento Valley |
| Pueblo San Diego | Downtown, OB, Point Loma, Mid-City |
| Sweetwater | South City (Encanto, Paradise Hills) |
| Otay | far south |
| Tijuana | border communities |

Each WMA has a **WQIP** (Water Quality Improvement Plan) listing priority
pollutants — project selection of treatment BMP must address those pollutants
(see §5.2 of Manual).

**Regional WQ equivalency / alternative compliance**: a project may buy
into an offsite retrofit within the same WMA where on-site compliance is
infeasible (§1.6).

**5552 Redwood / 92841** — Mid-City, drains to **Pueblo San Diego WMA**
(Chollas Creek sub-watershed is primary). Detached 1,200 sf ADU on
typical SFR lot is very unlikely to hit 10,000 sf impervious → Standard
Project, requires WPCP and site-design BMPs only.

## WPCP vs SWPPP

| Document | When required | Author |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| **WPCP** (Water Pollution Control Plan) | Small projects < 1 acre disturbance; Standard Project scale | Applicant / contractor; City template OK |
| **SWPPP** (Storm Water Pollution Prevention Plan) | Disturbance ≥ 1 acre — triggers State Construction General Permit (CGP). Filed with SWRCB SMARTS. | QSD/QSP |
| **SWQMP** (Storm Water Quality Management Plan) | PDPs — post-construction permanent BMPs + O&M covenant | Civil engineer |

## Grading permits (SDMC Ch 14 Art 2 Div 2)

| Earthwork / slope | Permit |
|-------------------|--------|
| < 200 cy AND no slope > 5 ft AND no fill in drainage | Usually **exempt** from stand-alone grading permit (still covered under building) |
| 200 – 5,000 cy | **Grading permit** — IB-201 requirements; soil report often required |
| > 5,000 cy OR fill > 10 ft OR cut > 10 ft OR affecting ESA | Grading permit + geotech report + specialty review |
| In Hillside Review Overlay / Steep Slopes | Triggers slope analysis, height restrictions, MHPA review |

## Geotechnical report (IB-515)

Required when ANY:
- Slope ≥ 1:5 (20%) on or within 15 ft of building footprint.
- Expansive soils (visual or mapped).
- Fill supporting structures or > 1 ft below footings.
- Two or more stories with variable / unknown bearing.
- Retaining wall > 4 ft (bottom of footing to top of wall) or any retaining
  wall supporting surcharge.
- Cuts > 5 ft or slopes steeper than 2:1.
- Areas of known geologic hazard (landslide, liquefaction, active fault).

Presumed bearing values (**IB-141**) allow skipping the soils report for
1–2 story SFR/duplex/ADU on **level lots** in known stable soil with no
expansive conditions — most flat Mid-City ADU lots can qualify.

## Low Impact Development (LID) — site design essentials

- Disperse roof drainage to pervious (self-retaining) areas.
- Preserve existing native vegetation where possible.
- Limit soil compaction outside building footprint.
- Vegetated swales between driveway and street.
- Minimize driveway / impervious walk widths.
- Treat at source — avoid conveying all roof to single downspout → street.

## Priority pollutants (typical residential)

Sediment, trash, pathogens (pet waste), hydrocarbons (driveway), nutrients
(landscape fertilizer).

## Construction BMP essentials (WPCP minimum)

- Stabilized construction entrance (rock pad).
- Silt fence / fiber rolls at downslope edge.
- Inlet protection on all affected storm drain inlets.
- Covered material storage.
- Concrete washout area (off paving, lined).
- Stockpile covers during wet season (Oct 1 – Apr 30).
- Portable sanitation staked and away from inlets.
