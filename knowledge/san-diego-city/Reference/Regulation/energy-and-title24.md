# Energy & Title 24 — City of San Diego

Baseline: **2022 California Energy Code (Title 24 Part 6)** + **CALGreen
(Title 24 Part 11)**. 2025 code cycle effective **January 1, 2026** —
projects submitted on/after that date must comply with 2025 Part 6.

City of San Diego has adopted a **reach code** amending Title 24 Part 6 and
CALGreen that goes beyond the state floor. The reach code implements the
**Climate Action Plan (CAP) 2022 Update** goals and the
**Decarbonization of the Built Environment** strategy.

## San Diego reach code — highlights

| Measure | City requirement (reach) | State baseline |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Space heating** | **All-electric / heat pump** for new residential; new gas connections prohibited for most new construction | Gas permitted |
| **Water heating** | **Heat-pump water heater (HPWH)** — either ducted or unducted, sized per CF1R | Gas permitted |
| **Cooking** | Electric-ready infrastructure required even where gas used (pre-wired 240V) | Electric-ready only at multifamily |
| **Clothes drying** | Electric-ready infrastructure required | Electric-ready only |
| **Space cooling** | Heat-pump HVAC (same unit as heating) | Mixed |
| **PV (single-family)** | Per state §150.1(c)14 (solar + battery ready as applicable) | Same — PV required by state since 2020 |
| **PV (multifamily)** | Per state 2022/2025 update (PV + battery storage) | State 2022/2025 |
| **EV-ready receptacles** | Per CALGreen + City — Level 2 receptacle or raceway at each dwelling (new SFD); increased % for MF | CALGreen baseline |

The reach code ordinance (often cited in project context as **O-21450** or
its successor) was adopted in connection with the CAP update, with
effective dates tied to the 2022 code cycle (most provisions took effect in
2023 for permits submitted after the ordinance effective date).

## Title 24 Part 6 compliance track

Two compliance paths:

1. **Prescriptive** — follow the package; simplest for SFR; select "HERS
   registered" where required.
2. **Performance** — model via CBECC-Res/Com approved software, produce
   CF1R-PRF-01; allows trade-offs.

For an ADU ≤ 1,200 sf, the **Alterations path** or a prescriptive dwelling
package is typical. CF1R must be HERS-registered and show all-electric
configuration.

### HERS rater verifications (typical on SFR/ADU)

- Duct leakage test (when central forced air used).
- Refrigerant charge / fan-watt-draw.
- Whole-house ventilation airflow.
- Quality Insulation Installation (QII) when claimed.
- Building envelope air leakage (blower door) for alterations path.

Rater must be enrolled with a HERS Provider (CalCERTS, CHEERS).

## PV + battery storage (state + City)

| Building type | 2022 code | 2025 code (Jan 1 2026) |
|---------------|-----------|------------------------|
| New low-rise SFD | PV sized to meet TDV energy budget | PV + **battery storage** (ESS) per prescriptive; extended solar-ready |
| New low-rise MF | PV required + **battery-ready** (panel space, wiring) | PV + **battery storage** installed |
| Non-residential (office, retail, warehouse) | PV + battery | Expanded coverage |

**Solar-ready** exceptions (where PV not installed now):
- Insufficient solar access (shading > 30% of effective area).
- Roof too small (< 80 sf available solar zone).
- Alternative compliance via PCF (community shared solar) where allowed.

### ADU PV

- **Attached** ADU — included with primary under state PV rule.
- **Detached new** ADU — subject to state §150.1(c)14; City interprets
  PV as required unless exception applies (small roof, shading). HPWH
  and all-electric requirements always apply under reach code.
- **ADU < 100 sf increase** to existing structure — may trigger only
  alteration package.

## Heat-pump water heater (HPWH) specifics

- Indoor installation: must have ≥ 700 cu ft air volume or louvered
  closet; condensate drain.
- Outdoor/garage installation permitted (verify ambient temp rating).
- 240V / 30A circuit typical.
- Listed for California / NEEA Advanced Tier where claimed for rebate.

## Envelope highlights (SFR, Climate Zone 7 — most of San Diego city)

| Assembly | Prescriptive U/R |
|----------|-------------------|
| Roof attic — insulated ceiling | R-38 batts + R-6 continuous or R-49 batts |
| Roof — cathedral / unvented | R-30 + R-5 ci |
| Wall — 2×4 | R-15 cavity + R-4 ci (or R-21/R-19 batts per package) |
| Wall — 2×6 | R-21 cavity |
| Slab | No perimeter insulation required in CZ 7 |
| Window U | ≤ 0.30 |
| Window SHGC | ≤ 0.23 |

**Climate Zone 7** covers most of San Diego city (coastal + mesa). Inland
community plan areas (Rancho Peñasquitos, Scripps Ranch, Mira Mesa
eastern edge) may be **CZ 10** — verify with address lookup on CEC map.

## Ventilation & IAQ (§150.0(o))

- Mechanical ventilation required in every dwelling.
- SFD: continuous exhaust or balanced HRV/ERV; rate per ASHRAE 62.2.
- Kitchen range hood: ≥ 100 cfm vented to exterior (recirculating not
  allowed in new construction after 2022).
- Bathroom exhaust: ≥ 50 cfm intermittent or 20 cfm continuous.

## Lighting (§150.0(k))

- 100% high-efficacy (LED equivalent) interior and exterior.
- Automatic controls per space type (occupancy / daylight).

| Form | When |

## 2025 code cycle key deltas (Jan 1 2026 forward)

- Expanded heat-pump space-heating prescriptive package for all climate
  zones.
- PV + battery storage required for most new low-rise residential and a
  broader set of non-residential.
- EV-ready: higher % of stalls Level 2 (from 10% to 25%+ for MF).
- CALGreen Part 11 raises baseline indoor air quality and water efficiency.
- Embodied carbon reporting for large non-residential added.
- Submittals on/after 1/1/2026 subject to 2025 package; City reach code
  layers on top — confirm with CAP desk for current form.
