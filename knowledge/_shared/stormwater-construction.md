# Stormwater — Construction & Post-Construction

**Scope:** California Construction General Permit (CGP, Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ), SWPPP requirements, QSD/QSP roles, risk levels, MS4 (Phase I/II) permits, post-construction BMPs (LID, hydromodification), Priority Development Project (PDP) thresholds, erosion/sediment controls, and 401/404 triggers.

## 1. Regulatory Framework

Three overlapping systems govern stormwater for construction projects:

| Level | Permit | Regulator | Triggered by |
|-------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Federal → State** | Construction General Permit (**CGP**) | State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) | ≥ **1 acre** of land disturbance (or part of larger common plan) |
| **Local** | Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System (**MS4**) permit | Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB); implemented by city/county | Any construction within permittee jurisdiction — BMPs scoped to project size |
| **Post-construction** | Baked into MS4 for Priority Development Projects (**PDPs**) | City/county | Thresholds: usually ≥10,000 sf of impervious surface (San Diego), varies |

## 2. Construction General Permit — CGP 2022-0057-DWQ

**Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ** (NPDES No. CAS000002), adopted **September 8, 2022**, **effective September 1, 2023** — replaces the 2009/2012 CGP. Applies statewide to construction sites disturbing:

- **≥ 1 acre** of total land area, OR
- **< 1 acre** if part of a **common plan of development** that cumulatively disturbs ≥ 1 acre (e.g., phased subdivision)

### Coverage Mechanism
Projects obtain coverage by filing electronically in **SMARTS** (Stormwater Multiple Application Report Tracking System):
1. File **Permit Registration Documents (PRDs)** before construction begins
2. Pay annual fee (varies by risk level & acreage)
3. SWPPP must be on-site and posted before ground disturbance
4. File **Notice of Termination (NOT)** when site is fully stabilized (≥70% perennial vegetation or equivalent)

### Key Roles

| Role | Definition | Required certification |
|------|------------|-----------------------|
| **LRP** — Legally Responsible Person | Landowner or authorized delegate who signs PRDs and certifies compliance | None (must have legal authority) |
| **Approved Signatory** | Person authorized to sign on behalf of LRP | Written delegation |
| **QSD** — Qualified SWPPP Developer | Develops the SWPPP; responsible for design | **QSD certification** (CASQA, CPESC, CPSWQ, or PE + training) |
| **QSP** — Qualified SWPPP Practitioner | Implements SWPPP on site; inspects, signs reports | **QSP certification** |
| **Trained Contractor Personnel** | Daily site workers trained in SWPPP basics | QSP-supervised training |

QSD/QSP certifications administered by **California Stormwater Quality Association (CASQA)**.

## 3. Risk Levels (CGP Attachment A)

CGP uses a **risk-based** framework. Risk = Sediment Risk × Receiving Water Risk.

| Risk Level | Sediment risk | Receiving water risk | Requirements |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **RL 1** | Low | Low | Basic BMPs; standard inspections |
| **RL 2** | Medium–high sediment OR medium–high receiving | Mid-tier | Stricter BMPs; turbidity monitoring NAL (Numeric Action Level); ATS may be required |
| **RL 3** | High sediment AND high receiving | Both | Active Treatment Systems (ATS) may be required; monitoring NALs; stricter sampling |

Computed in the **Project Risk Calculator** (RUSLE2-based sediment calc + receiving water impairment). RL 1 is typical for most residential infill <5 acres. RL 3 is reserved for large earthwork adjacent to impaired waters.

## 4. SWPPP Contents (CGP Attachment D)

Required elements:

1. **Narrative** — project description, phasing, acreage, receiving waters
2. **Site maps** — existing and proposed, drainage patterns, BMP locations, concrete washout, vehicle access, stockpiles
3. **BMPs** — erosion controls (EC), sediment controls (SC), good housekeeping / waste management (WM), non-stormwater controls (NS)
4. **Training records**
5. **Inspection schedule** — weekly + before/during/after qualifying rain events (0.5" for RL 1, 0.25" for RL 2/3)
6. **Rain event action plan (REAP)** — 48-hr advance preparation
7. **Monitoring program** — pH, turbidity sampling at RL 2/3
8. **Amendments log**

## 6. MS4 Permits (Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System)

Administered by **9 Regional Water Quality Control Boards**. Two phases based on population:

| Phase | Population | Permit type |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Phase I** | > 100,000 | Individual permits to large urbanized areas (e.g., San Diego MS4 Order R9-2013-0001) |
| **Phase II** | ≤ 100,000 or non-traditional (state universities, Caltrans facilities) | General Phase II permit (Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ coordinates) |

MS4 permits require cities/counties to:
1. Implement a **Stormwater Program** (outreach, illicit discharge detection, construction BMP enforcement)
2. Require **post-construction BMPs** for development projects
3. Issue **local enforcement** (via municipal code or grading ordinance)

### Local Manuals
Each MS4 region publishes a **BMP Design Manual**:
- **San Diego Region** — "BMP Design Manual" (2016, updated 2020) — city and county adopt their own appendices
- **Los Angeles Region** — Low Impact Development (LID) Ordinance handbooks
- **SF Bay Region** — C.3 Provisions

## 7. Post-Construction BMPs & Priority Development Projects (PDPs)

When a project exceeds an impervious-surface or land-disturbance threshold, it becomes a **PDP** (sometimes called "Regulated Project" or "C.3 project" in Bay Area). PDPs must include **permanent** stormwater control BMPs sized to capture and treat runoff.

### PDP thresholds (San Diego Region example)
A project is a PDP if it meets any of:
- **New development** creating ≥ **10,000 sf of impervious surface**
- **Redevelopment** creating ≥ 5,000 sf of impervious surface on a PDP-created existing site
- **Restaurants** ≥ 5,000 sf impervious
- **Parking lots** ≥ 5,000 sf impervious
- **Commercial/industrial** ≥ 5,000 sf impervious
- **Streets/roads** ≥ 10,000 sf of impervious surface
- **Hillside development** ≥ 10,000 sf on slopes ≥25%
- **Retail gas stations**, **auto repair**, **car dealerships**
- **Projects tributary to an ESA** (environmentally sensitive area) — ≥2,500 sf threshold

(Other regions have different numeric thresholds; always verify with the applicable BMP Manual.)

### BMP Design Hierarchy (per San Diego BMP Design Manual Ch 5)

Pollutant control BMPs are selected in this priority order:
1. **Retention** (infiltration, evapotranspiration, capture+reuse) — preferred
2. **Biofiltration** (engineered soil media with underdrain) — second choice
3. **Flow-thru treatment** (proprietary media filters, hydrodynamic separators) — only when 1 & 2 infeasible

### Sizing — Design Capture Volume (DCV)
`DCV = 0.85 × 3600 × d × A × C`  (English units), where:
- d = 85th percentile 24-hour rainfall depth (in), region-specific (SD coast ≈ 0.6"; Inland ≈ 0.75")
- A = tributary area (acres)
- C = runoff coefficient

### Typical LID BMPs

| BMP | Function | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Bioretention (rain garden) | Retention + biofiltration | Engineered media depth ≥18" |
| Permeable pavement | Infiltration | Requires infiltration-capable subgrade |
| Green roof | Retention + evapotranspiration | Structural + waterproof design |
| Infiltration trench/basin | Retention | Needs geotech feasibility; depth-to-groundwater ≥10 ft typ. |
| Cistern / rainwater harvest | Retention (capture + reuse) | Storage sized to DCV |
| Underground detention (with treatment) | Flow-thru | Only when LID infeasible |

## 8. Hydromodification Management (HMP)

When the project is a PDP **and** discharges to an unlined natural or soft-bottom receiving water, it triggers **Hydromodification Management** — flow control sized to protect the receiving stream from erosion.

Performance standard (San Diego and others):
- Control post-project **peak flows** to match **pre-development** (natural) conditions for the **10%-of-2-yr through 10-yr** storm range
- Control **flow durations** (not just peaks) via detention + controlled release

Exemptions:
- Tributary to lined channels, tidal waters, or large reservoirs
- Lot-scale dispersed infiltration on very pervious soils

## 9. 401 / 404 — Federal & State Water Permits

| Permit | Jurisdiction | Triggered by |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **CWA §404 (ACOE)** | US Army Corps of Engineers | Discharge of dredged/fill material into **waters of the U.S.** (WOTUS) — includes wetlands, streams, lakes |
| **CWA §401 Water Quality Certification** | RWQCB | Any federal permit that may result in discharge (required alongside §404) |
| **CDFW §1600 Streambed Alteration** | California Dept of Fish & Wildlife | Any project altering bed/bank/channel of a river, stream, lake — state counterpart to federal §404 |
| **RWQCB Waste Discharge Requirements (WDRs)** | RWQCB | Discharges to non-WOTUS state waters (e.g., isolated wetlands after *Sackett*) |

**Triggers to watch for on plan review:**
- Any grading within 100 ft of a creek, ditch, or waterway
- Any fill of a depression/wetland
- Channel crossings (culverts, bridges)
- Bank stabilization work

A **Pre-Construction Notification (PCN)** to ACOE is required for most fills, even under Nationwide Permits. Timeline: 45 days.

## 10. Quick Plan-Check Cheat Sheet (Construction Stormwater)

- [ ] Land disturbance ≥1 acre? → File CGP via SMARTS; SWPPP signed by QSD
- [ ] If <1 ac but common plan of development ≥1 ac → still CGP
- [ ] Local grading permit → MS4 BMPs per local manual (even <1 ac)
- [ ] Impervious increase ≥ PDP threshold (10,000 sf new; 5,000 sf SD specific categories) → LID BMPs sized to DCV
- [ ] Hydromodification tributary check → natural stream? → HMP BMPs
- [ ] Waters of U.S. / creek / wetland on site? → 404 + 401; CDFW 1602
- [ ] Construction BMPs on plans: TR-1, SC-1 or SC-5, SC-10, WM-8
- [ ] Inspection frequency per risk level documented
- [ ] NOT filed at project completion
