# California Statewide ADU & JADU Law

**Scope:** Government Code §65852.2 (ADU) and §65852.22 (JADU) as amended through 2024. Covers the cumulative body of state law (AB 68, AB 881, SB 13, AB 670, AB 671, AB 3182, AB 2221, AB 1033, SB 477, AB 976, AB 1332) and the minimum standards that local jurisdictions may **not** override. Interaction with SB 9 and fee rules.

## 1. Why State Law Matters

California ADU law is **state preemptive**. Gov Code §65852.2(a)(4) and subsequent amendments explicitly preempt conflicting local zoning, and HCD has expanded enforcement authority (AB 976 / AB 1033 era). A local ordinance that is more restrictive than state law is **void to the extent of the conflict** (Gov Code §65852.2(a)(4)). HCD can compel corrections.

Official HCD landing: **hcd.ca.gov/policy-research/accessorydwellingunits.shtml**

## 3. Minimum State Standards (Cannot Be Overridden)

These are the **floor** — a local agency may be more permissive but never more restrictive:

### Unit Types Per Single-Family Lot

On any lot with an existing or proposed single-family dwelling, a local agency must allow at least **one ADU + one JADU** ministerially:
- **ADU**: up to 1,200 sf detached, or 50% of primary (or 800 sf, whichever greater) for attached
- **JADU**: up to 500 sf within the footprint of the primary SFR

On a lot zoned for single-family with an SB 9 lot split, ADUs and JADUs are limited separately (see §9 below).

On multifamily lots: up to **two detached ADUs** + converted ADUs (up to 25% of existing units, or 1, whichever greater) (§65852.2(e)(1)(D)).

### Size Limits (§65852.2(c))

| Type | Max size that local agency must allow |
|------|--------------------------------------|
| **Detached ADU (1 bd or studio)** | **800 sf** regardless of local FAR, lot coverage, open-space rules |
| **Detached ADU (2+ bd)** | **1,000 sf** minimum allowance (local may allow up to 1,200 sf) |
| **Attached ADU** | **800 sf** OR 50% of primary dwelling, whichever is larger |
| **Conversion (within existing SFR or accessory structure)** | No size limit — whatever fits the existing envelope; 150 sf expansion for ingress/egress permitted |
| **JADU** | **500 sf**, within the walls of the existing or proposed SFR |

### Setbacks (§65852.2(a)(1)(D)(vii))

| Condition | Max setback local agency can require |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| **Side & rear** (new construction ADU) | **4 ft** |
| **Conversion** of legally existing structure | **0 ft** (even up to lot line) |
| **Front setback** | Local standard may apply — BUT if the ADU ≤ 800 sf and front setback prevents construction, the front setback **must** be reduced to allow it |
| **Primary dwelling setback** | Local zoning (unaffected by ADU law) |

### Height Limits (§65852.2(a)(1)(D)(i))

| Condition | Maximum height local agency can require |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| **Detached ADU baseline** | **16 ft** min allowance statewide |
| **Detached ADU within ½ mi walking of major transit stop OR high-quality transit corridor** | **18 ft** — plus 2-ft additional if needed to match primary dwelling roof pitch |
| **Detached ADU on a multifamily lot with ≥2-story primary** | **18 ft** |
| **Attached ADU** | **25 ft** OR primary dwelling height limit, whichever is **lower** |
| **ADU above existing accessory structure** | Typically 16 ft but coordinate with 18 ft rules |

### Parking (§65852.2(d))

Local agency may require **1 space per ADU or per bedroom** — but **must waive** parking if any one of the following applies:
- ADU is within **½ mile walking distance** of public transit (this includes a bus stop)
- ADU is in an architecturally & historically significant historic district
- ADU is part of the proposed or existing primary residence OR an accessory structure (conversion)
- Permit parking is required on-street and the ADU has no parking permit
- Car-share vehicle is located within one block
- When a permit is requested with a new SFR — the replacement ADU parking when a garage is converted cannot be required in the setback

### Ministerial Review & Timeline (§65852.2(b))

- Review is **ministerial** (over-the-counter) — no CEQA, no discretionary conditions, no neighbor notice required.
- Local agency must approve or deny within **60 days** of complete application (was 120 days pre-AB 68).
- Pre-approved plans (AB 1332) must be approved within **30 days** — effective Jan 1, 2025 mandate.
- Denial must cite specific section(s) and permit a **cure/re-submittal** (AB 2221 strengthens).

### Sprinkler & Fire (§65852.2(a)(1)(D)(vii))

**State preemption rule:** An ADU **cannot** be required to install fire sprinklers solely because the primary dwelling is not required to have them. This is a hard preemption — a local ordinance that requires ADU sprinklers when the primary is unsprinklered is void. (Gov Code §65852.2(a)(1)(D)(vii); reinforced by SB 897, eff. Jan 1 2023.)

**SB 897 (2023) addition:** ADU construction also cannot *trigger* a retroactive sprinkler retrofit on the existing primary dwelling. The primary's sprinkler status is evaluated at the time of ADU permitting and is not escalated by the ADU project.

#### Waiver decision tree

```
Is primary dwelling required to have / does it have sprinklers?
  NO → ADU sprinklers are NOT required (state preemption)
       UNLESS: site is in VHFHSZ (see below) OR it is a Bonus/Affordable ADU
  YES → ADU must have sprinklers; tie-in to primary system or separate 13D
```

#### Waiver conditions summary

| Scenario | Sprinklers required? |
|---|---|
| Detached ADU ≤ 1,200 sf, primary unsprinklered | **NO — waived** |
| Attached ADU, primary unsprinklered | **NO — waived** |
| JADU (≤ 500 sf inside primary), primary unsprinklered | **NO — waived** |
| Any ADU, primary sprinklered (post-2011 CRC R313 home) | **YES — tie-in required** |
| ADU in VHFHSZ — local FD may override | **YES — confirm with AHJ** |
| Bonus ADU / Affordable ADU (San Diego) | **YES — required by program** |

#### What does NOT waive sprinklers

- **Construction type (V-A vs V-B)** — completely irrelevant to the ADU sprinkler waiver. Type V-A 1-hr rated construction addresses structural fire resistance of the framing, not fire suppression. CRC R313 sprinkler requirements are triggered by dwelling type and primary-dwelling status, not by construction type. An R-3 ADU built to V-A standard still needs sprinklers if the primary is sprinklered.
- **Building height or size** — the waiver threshold is the primary's sprinkler status, not ADU size (though local fire codes may have additional thresholds in VHFHSZ areas).

#### CRC R313 baseline (new construction)

CRC R313 requires NFPA 13D throughout all **new** one- and two-family dwellings and townhouses in California (effective statewide since 2011). This means any primary SFD permitted after ~2011 has sprinklers → any ADU on that lot also requires sprinklers (no waiver available).

Pre-2011 primaries and older un-permitted conversions are typically unsprinklered → ADU waiver applies.

- If primary was built after CRC R313 took effect (2011), primary has sprinklers → ADU must also (CRC R313).
- Local fire marshal may still require other fire measures (access, water supply) but cannot ban the ADU outright.

### Owner-Occupancy (post-AB 976 / AB 1033)

- **ADU** — no owner-occupancy requirement allowed, permanently (AB 976 made this permanent after the original 2025 sunset).
- **JADU** — owner-occupancy **is required** (still; §65852.22(a)(2)).

### Short-Term Rentals
A local agency may prohibit renting an ADU for <30 days (short-term rental). Most jurisdictions do.

## 4. Junior ADU (§65852.22)

| Rule | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Max size** | 500 sf |
| **Location** | Within the walls of an existing or proposed single-family residence (the "primary") — includes attached garage converted |
| **Sanitation** | May share bathroom with primary OR have its own |
| **Kitchen** | Efficiency kitchen required (sink, cooking facility with max 120V appliance, counter, storage) |
| **Separate entrance** | Required (to outside) |
| **Parking** | No parking required (regardless of transit distance) |
| **Owner-occupancy** | Required — owner lives in either the primary OR the JADU |
| **Sold separately?** | No — JADU cannot be sold apart from primary |
| **Deed restriction** | Required, recorded against title limiting size/owner-occ/no-separate-sale |
| **Impact fees** | **Fully exempt** (Gov Code §65852.22(g)) |

## 5. Pre-Approved Plans (AB 1332 — effective Jan 1, 2025)

Every city and county must:
1. Adopt a **pre-approval program** for stock ADU plans by Jan 1, 2025.
2. Publish the list (drawings, specifications) on the local website.
3. Approve permit applications using a pre-approved plan within **30 days** of complete submittal.
4. Allow minor modifications (e.g., siding, color) without losing pre-approval status.

HCD provides model templates. Some cities (SD City, SF, San Jose) offered ad hoc programs pre-2025 and have transitioned.

## 6. Impact Fees (Gov Code §65852.2(f) / §66000 series)

| ADU size | Impact fee rule |
|----------|-----------------|
| **< 750 sf** | **Fully exempt** from local agency, special district, and water-corporation impact fees (including capacity/connection fees). School fees also exempt for <750 sf JADUs always. |
| **≥ 750 sf** | Fees must be **proportional** to primary dwelling based on square footage (example: 1,200 sf ADU / 2,400 sf primary → 50% of the primary's fee). |
| **JADU any size** | **Fully exempt** from all impact fees |
| **Utility connection** | ADU created from existing space is **not** a new residential use for capacity charges unless built concurrently with a new SFR |

School district fees may still apply for ADUs ≥ 750 sf at the discretion of the school district (Education Code 17620).

## 7. HOAs (AB 670 / AB 3182)

HOAs **cannot** outright prohibit ADUs or JADUs on properties where state law permits them. HOAs **may** impose reasonable architectural guidelines on exterior design, colors, and materials (consistent with the HOA aesthetic) but not rules that have the effect of making construction infeasible.

## 8. AB 1033 — Separately Saleable ADU Condos (effective Jan 1, 2024)

AB 1033 **authorizes but does not require** local agencies to adopt an ordinance allowing ADUs to be conveyed **separately** from the primary residence as a **condominium**. Conditions:
- Local agency must opt in via ordinance.
- Must comply with the Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act (a mini-HOA forms).
- Existing lender consent required.
- Recordation as a condo plan.

As of 2025, only a handful of cities (including San Jose and some Bay Area cities) have opted in. **San Diego has not yet opted in** — verify per project.

## 9. Interaction with SB 9 (Gov Code §65852.21, §66411.7)

**SB 9** (2022) allows on single-family-zoned lots:
1. **Two-unit development** (duplex or two detached units) on a lot by ministerial permit; OR
2. **Urban lot split** (subdivide into two lots of ≥1,200 sf each, each with up to 2 units).

### Unit Counts with Both Laws
- **No lot split, existing SFR**: 1 primary + 1 ADU + 1 JADU = 3 units. Under SB 9 the owner may also add a second "primary" unit (duplex) → 1+1+1+1 = up to 4 units on the lot.
- **With SB 9 lot split**: each resulting lot gets up to **2 units total** (any combination of primary, ADU, JADU). The state law does not require more than **4 units** across both lots.

### Key constraints
- SB 9 requires owner to sign a 3-year owner-occupancy affidavit after a lot split.
- SB 9 preempts rental restrictions on resulting units <30 days.
- Historic districts, VHFHSZ, earthquake fault zones, prime farmland exempt.

## 10. Enforcement & Appeals

- HCD has authority to **notify** a non-compliant city and, if unresolved, refer the ordinance to the Attorney General.
- Applicants may appeal a denial to the local agency's appeal body and then file with HCD if the denial contradicts state law.
- AB 976 / AB 1033 expanded HCD to issue **letters of enforcement** with compliance deadlines.

## 11. Quick-Reference Checklist for an ADU Plan Check

- [ ] Is the ADU proposed on a lot with an existing/proposed SFR or MF? (Must be residential zone.)
- [ ] Detached / attached / conversion / JADU? (Determines size + setback rules.)
- [ ] Size ≤ 800 sf (guaranteed) or 1,000–1,200 sf (local detail)?
- [ ] Height ≤ 16 ft baseline / 18 ft transit or MF / 25 ft attached?
- [ ] Setbacks ≥ 4 ft side/rear (new) or 0 ft (conversion)?
- [ ] Parking waived? (Transit ½-mi; conversion; historic; permit zone; car-share.)
- [ ] Primary has sprinklers? If no → ADU may waive R313 per state preemption.
- [ ] Impact fees: <750 sf exempt; ≥750 sf prorated to primary.
- [ ] JADU only: owner-occupancy affidavit + deed restriction recorded.
- [ ] Pre-approved plan (AB 1332) used? → 30-day review.
- [ ] Short-term rental prohibition acknowledged (if local).
