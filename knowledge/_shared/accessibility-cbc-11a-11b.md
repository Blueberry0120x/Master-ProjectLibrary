# Accessibility — CBC Chapters 11A & 11B

**Scope:** 2022 California Building Code Chapters 11A (Housing Accessibility — privately funded multifamily) and 11B (Public Accommodations, Commercial, State/Public Housing). Scope triggers, adaptable vs accessible, path-of-travel, ADA federal overlay, and exemptions for SFR/duplex/ADU.

## 1. The Two-Chapter System

California splits accessibility into two chapters that apply in parallel depending on the project:

| Chapter | Applies to | Adopting agency |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **11A** | **Privately funded covered multifamily dwellings** (≥3 dwelling units in one structure, or 4+ units on a site with common use) — "covered" per FHA | **HCD** |
| **11B** | **Public buildings** (state/local gov), **public accommodations** (open to public), **commercial facilities**, **publicly funded housing** | **DSA-AC** |

A single project can trigger **both** (e.g., a privately-funded apartment building with a leasing office open to the public: 11A for dwelling units + 11B for leasing office/common areas).

**Federal overlay:** The federal **Fair Housing Act (FHA)** and **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)** always apply. California (11A/11B) is generally equal to or stricter than federal minimums; compliance with CBC Chapters 11A/11B typically satisfies FHA and ADA Title II/III, but design professionals should cross-check (e.g., the 2010 ADA Standards).

## 2. Exemptions — What is NOT Covered

### Chapter 11A does NOT apply to
- **Single-family dwellings** (R-3 with 1 unit)
- **Duplex / two-family dwellings** (R-3 with 2 units)
- **Townhouses** with separate exterior entrance where each unit is on a separate lot
- **Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)** — an ADU is a dwelling unit but not a "covered multifamily dwelling" in the FHA/11A sense. ADUs have **no 11A requirement**.
- **JADUs** — treated as part of the primary SFR → not covered.

### Chapter 11B does NOT apply to
- Private residential units not open to the public
- Religious facilities (federally exempt from ADA Title III)
- Private clubs (ADA federal exemption)

**But 11B does apply to:**
- Common use areas of multifamily (leasing, mailroom, rec rooms)
- Building permit work in existing public/commercial (triggering **path-of-travel** upgrades)

## 3. Chapter 11A — Covered Multifamily Housing

### "Covered Multifamily Dwellings" definition (CBC 1102A)
- **3 or more units in one structure** (R-2), OR
- **4 or more units on a single site** with common use facilities

### Key concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Adaptable units** | Units with structure + rough-in designed so they can be easily modified (e.g., blocking in walls for grab bars, lever handles, usable kitchen/bath dimensions). Not fully accessible from day one. |
| **Accessible units** | Fully compliant with 11A mobility & communication features at completion (typically only required for public housing in 11B or when state/local requires). |
| **Public-use and common-use areas** | Must be on an **accessible route** (1117A): leasing, mailbox, trash, rec, pool, pathways, parking. |
| **Mobility features** | Wheelchair maneuvering clearances, usable kitchen/bath fixtures, reachable controls, accessible routes within units. |
| **Communication features** | Visual alarms, captioned devices (where phones provided), assistive listening (in common areas). |

### Scoping — How Many Adaptable Units?

| Building type | # of adaptable units required |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| **Ground-floor units in buildings without elevator** (non-elevator buildings) | **All ground-floor units** must be adaptable + on accessible route |
| **All units in elevator buildings** | **All units** on floors served by elevator must be adaptable |
| **Multi-story units (townhouse/condo style)** | Primary entry level must be adaptable |

Plus: **mobility features on ground floor** for non-elevator buildings; **communication features** per 1127A for 10% of units minimum (typically 4% in CA-adopted 11A, but California amends to higher % than federal FHA).

### Unit Design Requirements (summary, 11A-1132A)

- **Doorways** ≥ 32" clear width
- **Routes** ≥ 36" wide, no steps
- **Bathrooms** — usable clear floor space; reinforced walls for future grab bars; at least one bathroom with accessible fixtures
- **Kitchens** — 30" × 48" clear space at sink, ≥60" turning radius OR 30" × 48" at each major appliance; work surfaces; accessible controls
- **Electrical outlets / switches** — reachable height (15"–48")
- **Thermostats / environmental controls** — reachable
- **Window sills & latches** — reachable
- **Laundry** — accessible if provided within unit

## 4. Chapter 11B — Public Accommodations & Commercial

### Scope (CBC 1101B)
- All **publicly funded** buildings (state, county, city, school district, UC/CSU, special districts)
- **Public accommodations** open to the public (retail, restaurants, hotels, medical offices, banks, theaters)
- **Commercial facilities** (offices, warehouses, factories) that are privately funded but non-residential
- **Transient lodging** (hotels, motels, R-1)
- **Publicly funded housing** (public housing, HUD-assisted)

### Key Systems (CBC 11B-202 through 11B-813)

| Feature | Standard | Section |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Parking** | 1 van-accessible per 6 accessible; ratios per Table 11B-208.2 | 11B-208 |
| **Accessible route** | ≥36" min clear, 60" passing or turnarounds every 200 ft | 11B-403 |
| **Doors** | 32" clear, max 5-lb force, closer delay | 11B-404 |
| **Ramps** | 1:12 max slope, 36" clear, handrails | 11B-405 |
| **Stairs** | Handrails both sides, 34"–38" ht; stair nosings contrasting | 11B-504 |
| **Elevators** | 11B-407; required in buildings >3 stories, ≥3,000 sf/floor (per 11B-206.2.3 exceptions) |
| **Restrooms** | Accessible stall, mounting heights, pipe wrap under lav | 11B-603–11B-604 |
| **Drinking fountains** | 2 required — 1 low (wheelchair) + 1 standing | 11B-602 |
| **Signage** | Tactile/Braille at room IDs, high-contrast, pictograms | 11B-703 |
| **Assistive listening** | Assembly areas ≥50 occupants or amplified | 11B-706 |
| **Counter heights** | 36" max service counter accessible section ≥36" long | 11B-904 |
| **Hotel guest rooms** | Ratios: 1 accessible + 1 with comm features per range | Table 11B-224 |

### Path of Travel (CBC 11B-202.4) — The "20% Rule"

When doing any alteration, addition, or change of use of an **existing** public/commercial building, the permit triggers accessibility upgrades to the **path of travel** from the public right-of-way to the altered area, **including** the primary entrance, restrooms, drinking fountains, signage, and public phones serving the altered area.

**Valuation threshold:** When the cost of path-of-travel upgrades exceeds **20% of the total adjusted construction cost of the alteration**, the path-of-travel upgrades may be limited (pro-rated) to 20% of the project valuation — the "unreasonable hardship" exception allowing phased compliance. Threshold updated annually for CPI (2026 threshold approximately $200,740 per CBSAS notice — confirm current year).

### Path-of-Travel Priorities (descending)
1. Accessible entrance
2. Accessible route to the altered area
3. At least one accessible restroom for each gender (or one single-user family)
4. Accessible telephones
5. Accessible drinking fountains
6. When possible: accessible signage, alarms, parking

## 5. Adaptable vs Accessible — Quick Comparison

| Feature | **Adaptable (11A typical)** | **Accessible (11B typical)** |
|---------|----------------------------|------------------------------|
| Where required | Private multifamily unit interiors | Public accommodations, commercial |
| When usable | Can be modified by future resident | Usable on Day 1 |
| Grab bars | Reinforced walls; installed later | Installed |
| Base cabinets | Standard; removable at sink/ADA | Knee clearance or removable at sink |
| Wheelchair routes | Provided | Provided |
| Cost impact | Lower (structural prep only) | Higher (fixtures, finishes) |

## 6. Housing — Special Situations

### Single-Family Detached
- No Chapter 11A or 11B requirements for the dwelling itself.
- But federally funded (HUD, HOME, CDBG) SFRs may trigger FHA and/or Section 504.

### Duplex
- If privately funded: exempt from 11A (<3 units) and no 11B (private).
- If publicly funded: 11B Chapter 11B-233 may trigger accessible unit requirements.

### Townhouse
- **Separate exterior entry + separate lot (CRC townhouse)**: exempt from 11A. Still subject to CRC R311/R315.
- **Common entry/hall** (R-2 occupancy): covered by 11A.

### ADU / JADU
- **No 11A requirement** — state ADU law preempts; HCD confirms ADUs are not "covered multifamily" under 11A.
- Local agency **may** voluntarily require accessibility features (rare).

### Condos
- 4+ units in a building (R-2 condo): 11A applies to dwelling units; 11B applies to any common areas open to the public (sales office).

### Public/HUD Housing
- Must comply with both 11A (for dwellings) and 11B (as publicly funded housing — especially 11B-233 scoping and 11B-809 residential dwelling unit provisions).

## 7. Common-Area Requirements at Multifamily (11A-1127A)

Even when only 11A applies (private multifamily), **common-use areas** must be accessible:
- Parking (11A-1109A): 2% of spaces, min 1
- Mailboxes at accessible height
- Trash enclosures on accessible route
- Laundry rooms / rec rooms
- Swimming pools — lift or accessible entry
- Leasing/management office (if open to public → 11B overlay)

## 8. ADA Coordination

Federal ADA (applied via 2010 Standards) imposes Title II (public entities), Title III (public accommodations/commercial). CBC 11B is substantially equivalent and in many cases **stricter**. Design to **the more stringent** of CBC 11B vs 2010 ADA Standards. Common CA-stricter items:
- More restrictive parking ratios
- Detectable warnings at curb ramps (CA has always required)
- Slopes: CA cross-slope max 1:50 (2%)
- Accessible guest-room bathing facilities scoped higher

**CASp (Certified Access Specialist) program** (CA-only, Civil Code 55.53) allows trained inspectors to provide a "CASp inspection" report granting some litigation protection. Many tenants & landlords voluntarily obtain.

## 9. Quick-Reference Scoping Table

| Project | 11A | 11B | FHA | ADA |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| SFR new construction | No | No | No | No |
| Duplex private | No | No | No | No |
| Triplex+ private multifamily | **Yes** (covered units) | Common areas | Yes | Title III for any public area |
| Mixed-use: retail + multifamily (R-2 + M) | Units 11A | Retail 11B | Yes | Title III |
| Apartment with leasing office | Yes | Leasing office yes | Yes | Yes |
| Publicly funded housing | Yes | Yes | Yes | Title II |
| ADU | No | No | No | No |
| Office, retail, hotel | No | **Yes** | — | **Yes** |
| School, government | No (typ.) | Yes | — | Title II |
