# CBC Fire-Resistance & Exterior Materials

**Scope:** 2022 CBC Chapters 7 (Fire & Smoke Protection), 7A (WUI), 14 (Exterior Walls), with CRC R302 and R337 companion references. Assemblies, opening protectives, draftstopping/fireblocking, and a cheat-sheet of common 1-hr rated walls and roofs.

## 1. Fire-Resistance Rating Basics (CBC Chapter 7)

Ratings express how long an assembly holds up under standard fire test (ASTM E119 / UL 263):

| Rating | Typical use |
|--------|-------------|
| **0-hr** | No rating required — V-B, II-B, III-B baseline |
| **1-hr** | V-A, III-A, II-A; corridors; exit access; single-story R-2 separations |
| **2-hr** | Shafts ≥4 stories, exit enclosures ≥4 stories, Type II-B/I-B column protection; townhouse common walls (CRC R302.2) |
| **3-hr** | Occupancy separations (high-hazard), horizontal separation in podium (510.2), area separation walls |
| **4-hr** | Firewalls (CBC 706), extreme separation |

**Three ways to document a rating:**
1. **Prescriptive** — follow CBC Tables 721.1(1)–(3) (masonry, concrete, wood/steel) or Section 722 calculated.
2. **Tested** — cite a UL Directory, GA File, or Intertek listing (UL U-series, V-series, GA WP-series).
3. **Engineered** — per Chapter 17 alternate materials/methods with signed engineering.

## 2. Common 1-Hour Wall Assemblies (UL / GA Cheat-Sheet)

| Listing | Framing | Gypsum | Notes |
|---------|---------|--------|-------|
| **UL U305** | 2×4 or 2×6 wood studs @ 16" or 24" o.c. | 5/8" Type X each face | Most-used residential 1-hr; insulation optional; STC≈35–53 depending on resilient channel |
| **UL U309** | 2×4 wood studs @ 16" o.c. | 5/8" Type X each face | Similar to U305, tight stud spacing |
| **UL U419** | 3-5/8" steel studs @ 24" o.c. | 5/8" Type X each face | Common commercial 1-hr partition |
| **UL U423** | 2×4 wood @ 16" o.c. | 1 layer 5/8" + face layer | Loadbearing 1-hr |
| **GA File WP 3605** | 2×4 wood @ 16" o.c. | 5/8" Type X each face | GA equivalent to U305 |
| **GA File WP 1410** | 3-5/8" steel studs | 5/8" Type X each face | GA equivalent to U419 |

**Pro tip:** UL listings specify a **minimum** stud size and a **maximum** spacing. You may upsize the stud and tighten the spacing without losing the listing. Insulation is *not* required for U305 unless noted.

## 3. Opening Protectives (CBC Table 716.1(1)–(3))

Required opening-protective ratings based on wall rating:

| Wall rating | Fire door | Fire window (sidelight/transom) |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------------------|
| 4-hr wall (firewall) | 3-hr | Not permitted |
| 3-hr wall | 3-hr | Not permitted |
| 2-hr wall (shaft, exit) | 1½-hr | Permitted if ≤25% wall area, wired/ceramic; limited to fire-protective glazing w/ area limits |
| 1-hr wall (corridor) | 20-min (45-min corridor) | 45-min fire-protective |
| Smoke partition | 20-min, no hose stream | — |

**Fire-protective vs fire-resistance-rated glazing** (CBC 716.1):
- **Fire-protective** — wired glass, ceramic; limited to 25% of wall area; tested to UL 9/NFPA 252.
- **Fire-resistance-rated** — tested to ASTM E119; no area limit; usable where wall rating ≥1-hr and opening exceeds 25%.

## 4. Draftstopping & Fireblocking (CBC 718 / CRC R302.11, R302.12)

**Fireblocking** — blocks concealed **vertical** and **horizontal** draft openings at:
- Stud walls: vertically every 10 ft; horizontally at the ceiling and floor levels
- Concealed soffits, drop ceilings, cove ceilings
- Interconnections of floor, wall, ceiling, stair stringer spaces
- Openings around vents, pipes, wires, cables, ducts

**Allowed fireblocking materials** (CBC 718.2.1 / CRC R302.11.1):
- 2× nominal lumber
- Two thicknesses of 1× nominal lumber with broken lap joints
- 23/32" wood structural panel with joints backed by 23/32" panel
- 3/4" particleboard with backed joints
- ½" gypsum board
- ¼" cement-based millboard
- Batts or blankets of mineral wool or glass fiber securely retained
- Cellulose insulation in specific circumstances (vertical only)

**Draftstopping** — subdivides concealed spaces to limit flame/smoke spread:

| Location | Max area between draftstops |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Floor/ceiling assemblies (CBC 718.3) | 1,000 sf (R-2/R-3 exempted when sprinklered per 718.3.2) |
| Attic spaces (CBC 718.4 / CRC R302.12) | 3,000 sf, or within 60 lf max dimension |

Allowed draftstopping materials: 1× lumber, 3/8" wood structural panel, ½" gypsum, or batt insulation securely retained.

**Exceptions:**
- R-2 with fewer than 4 dwelling units or R-2 ≤4 stories with NFPA 13 + 13R — floor/ceiling draftstopping not required.
- R-3 with <3 dwelling units — floor/ceiling draftstopping not required.

## 5. Exterior Wall Materials & Weather Resistance (CBC Chapter 14)

Every exterior wall must provide a **weather-resistant exterior wall envelope** (CBC 1402.2) consisting of:

1. **Water-resistive barrier (WRB)** behind the cladding, minimum **one layer** (CBC 1403.2). Acceptable:
   - No. 15 asphalt felt (ASTM D226 Type 1)
   - ASTM E2556 Type I/II housewrap (e.g., Tyvek, Typar)
   - Fluid-applied WRB with ICC-ES report
2. **Flashing** at all penetrations, windows, doors, intersections with roofs (CBC 1404.4 / CRC R703.4).
3. **Drainage** — gap/cavity behind cladding, weeps at base. For stucco lath: **two layers** of WRB required in some jurisdictions (CA has adopted two-layer requirement via CBC 2510.6 for stucco over wood-based sheathing).

### Combustibility by Construction Type (CBC 603 / 1404)

| Construction | Exterior wall material |
|--------------|------------------------|
| I, II | Noncombustible: brick, concrete, metal panel, fiber-cement (rated noncombustible), stucco on wire over noncombustible sheathing |
| III | Noncombustible exterior walls (fire-retardant-treated wood framing, **FRTW**, permitted in exterior walls) |
| IV | Mass timber / noncombustible |
| V | Any permitted — wood framing, vinyl, wood siding, stucco, fiber-cement, composite |

**FRTW** (fire-retardant-treated wood): IBC 2303.2. Required when a wood element must be in a noncombustible construction type's exterior wall.

## 6. Roofing (CBC Chapter 15, CRC R902)

**Roof classifications** (fire exposure, ASTM E108 / UL 790):

| Class | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **A** | Highest — effective against severe fire test exposure (≥2 hr). Required in WUI and most Type I–III buildings. |
| **B** | Moderate — effective against moderate exposure (~1 hr). |
| **C** | Light — resists light exposure (~20 min). |

**Typical roofing by class:**
- Class A: slate, clay tile, concrete tile, metal panel (most), asphalt composition shingle listed Class A (fiberglass-based shingles with Class A rating by assembly).
- Class B: some wood shake with assembly, certain asphalt roll.
- Class C: un-reinforced asphalt shingle, untreated wood shake.

**CBC 1505** assigns minimum class by construction type and occupancy. CRC R902 mirrors for 1- and 2-family dwellings.

## 7. Chapter 7A / CRC R337 — Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI)

Applies in **State Responsibility Areas (SRA)** or **Local Responsibility Area Very-High Fire Hazard Severity Zones (VHFHSZ)**. Adopted by State Fire Marshal; CRC R337 mirrors for dwellings.

### WUI material requirements (summary)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Roof covering** | Class A (CRC R902.1.4; CBC 705A.3) |
| **Attic / underfloor / foundation vents** | WUI-listed (ASTM E2886 flame + ember) OR approved by SFM; **1/16" to 1/8" corrosion-resistant noncombustible mesh** required as baseline |
| **Eaves / soffits** | Noncombustible, ignition-resistant, or 1-hr rated. Exposed rafter tails must be enclosed or ignition-resistant |
| **Exterior walls** | Noncombustible OR ignition-resistant (SFM 12-7A-1 test); OR log wall; OR 1-hr rated assembly |
| **Exterior windows & glazing** | **Dual-pane insulating glass with at least one tempered pane**, OR 20-min fire-rated glazing, OR glass block |
| **Exterior doors** | Noncombustible, solid-core ≥1-3/4", or 20-min fire-rated |
| **Decking** | SFM-listed Section 704A-compliant decking, or ignition-resistant, or 1-hr rated, or noncombustible |
| **Gutters/downspouts** | Noncombustible |

**Local overlays** — Many jurisdictions extend WUI requirements to all new construction regardless of zone (e.g., some mountain and canyon neighborhoods in San Diego County). Verify VHFHSZ/SRA mapping via **CAL FIRE FHSZ Viewer**.

## 8. Firewalls vs Fire Barriers vs Fire Partitions (terminology)

| Element | CBC § | Rating | Continuity | Typical use |
|---------|-------|--------|------------|-------------|
| Firewall | 706 | 2–4 hr | Foundation to above roof | Area separation; creates "separate buildings" |
| Fire barrier | 707 | 1–4 hr | Floor to floor/roof above | Shafts, exit enclosures, occupancy separations |
| Fire partition | 708 | ½ – 1 hr | To underside of rated floor/ceiling above | Corridors, tenant separations, dwelling separations in R-2 |
| Smoke barrier | 709 | 1 hr | Floor to floor/roof | Smoke control boundaries |
| Smoke partition | 710 | No rating | To deck or lay-in ceiling | Smoke separation (no fire resistance needed) |
| Horizontal assembly | 711 | Per Table 601/508 | Horizontal | Floor separations, podium 3-hr slab |

## 9. CRC Townhouse Common Walls (CRC R302.2)

Townhouses: **two 1-hr rated fire-resistive walls** separated by a structural gap, OR a single **2-hr rated wall** (R302.2.2) — with structural independence of each unit. Openings are **not permitted** through the common wall assembly.
