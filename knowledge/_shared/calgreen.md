# CALGreen — California Green Building Standards Code (Title 24 Part 11)

**Scope:** 2022 CALGreen (with 2024 Supplement, effective July 1, 2024 for Tier 2 measures). Covers mandatory measures (Tier 0), voluntary Tier 1 and Tier 2, construction waste management, water efficiency, indoor air quality, EV charging, cool roof / heat-island. Notes 2025 changes effective Jan 1, 2026.

## 1. Structure of CALGreen

| Chapter | Topic |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | Administration |
| 2 | Definitions |
| 3 | Green Building (applicability scope) |
| **4** | **Residential Mandatory Measures** (low-rise res — SFR, duplex, townhome ≤3 stories, low-rise multifamily) |
| **5** | **Nonresidential Mandatory Measures** (commercial, industrial, high-rise multifamily) |
| 6 | Referenced Organizations & Standards |
| **A4 / A5** | **Voluntary Tier 1 & Tier 2** (residential / nonresidential) |

**Tiers:**
- **Tier 0** = Chapter 4 & 5 mandatory statewide (everyone meets this).
- **Tier 1 / Tier 2** = voluntary unless a local jurisdiction **reach code** adopts them (e.g., Santa Monica Tier 1; San Francisco Tier 2 in some zones).

**2024 CALGreen Supplement:** added mandatory embodied-carbon measures (for nonresidential ≥100,000 sf new schools, offices, retail) effective July 1, 2024. Residential was not affected.

## 3. Construction Waste Management (§4.408 / §5.408)

**Mandatory:** Recycle and/or salvage for reuse a **minimum of 65%** of non-hazardous construction and demolition (C&D) debris generated at the site.

**Four compliance paths** (§4.408.2 through §4.408.4):

1. **Local waste ordinance** — comply with a more-stringent local C&D plan (many CA jurisdictions require 65% + deposit system, e.g., San Diego WMF).
2. **Waste Management Plan (§4.408.2)** — written plan identifying materials, diversion facilities, estimated volumes. Submit receipts at final.
3. **Waste management company (§4.408.3)** — contract with a service that self-hauls to a certified facility.
4. **Waste stream reduction alternative (§4.408.4)** — for residential low-rise new construction, demonstrate disposal ≤ **3.4 lbs/sf** (≡ 65% diversion baseline).

**Flag items** for plan check:
- Mixed debris recycling facilities: provide facility name + permit number
- Hazardous waste (asbestos, lead paint, treated wood) **excluded** from the calculation
- Aggregate/dirt/land-clearing debris may be excluded or included per local rule

## 4. Water Efficiency (§4.303–§4.305)

### Indoor Fixture Maximum Flow Rates (§4.303.1)

| Fixture | Max flow / flush |
|---------|------------------|
| **Water closet (toilet)** | **1.28 gpf** (gallons per flush) |
| **Urinal** (non-residential) | 0.125 gpf (wall-mount) / 0.5 gpf (floor) |
| **Showerhead** (single) | **1.8 gpm** @ 80 psi |
| **Multiple showerheads per stall** | Combined ≤ 1.8 gpm OR one operating at a time |
| **Lavatory faucet** (residential) | **1.2 gpm** @ 60 psi |
| **Lavatory faucet** (non-residential public) | 0.5 gpm @ 60 psi (metered: 0.25 gal/cycle) |
| **Kitchen faucet** | **1.8 gpm** @ 60 psi (may be 2.2 gpm momentarily) |

### Outdoor Water — MWELO Tie-In (§4.304)

California **Model Water Efficient Landscape Ordinance (MWELO)**, DWR Title 23 Div. 2 Ch. 2.7, is referenced:

| Project type | MWELO threshold |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **New construction** with landscape | Landscape area ≥ **500 sf** (any permit-requiring) |
| **Rehabilitated landscape** | ≥ **2,500 sf** |
| **Homeowner-installed** (new SFR) | ≥ **5,000 sf** or per local ordinance |

MWELO requires: weather-based irrigation controller, hydrozoning, Maximum Applied Water Allowance (MAWA) calculation, soil management report for sites >2,500 sf.

## 5. Indoor Air Quality / Low-Emission Materials (§4.504)

| Category | Limit | Reference |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Adhesives, sealants, caulks** | VOC ≤ SCAQMD Rule 1168 (various by type) | Table 4.504.1 |
| **Paints & coatings** | VOC ≤ limits per CARB SCM 2007 (Table 4.504.3) |
| **Aerosol paints** | CARB 2003 regulation — Title 17 CCR 94507–94517 |
| **Carpet** | CRI Green Label Plus OR CA Sect 01350 emissions |
| **Resilient flooring** | 80% must meet Resilient Floor Covering Institute FloorScore, CRI Green Label Plus, or CDPH Standard Method v1.2 |
| **Composite wood products** (hardwood plywood, particleboard, MDF) | **CARB Phase 2** formaldehyde emission limits (ATCM, 17 CCR 93120) |

### Ventilation for IAQ (§4.506)
Cite Title 24 Part 6 §150.0(o) — ASHRAE 62.2 whole-house ventilation (see title24-energy.md).

## 6. EV Charging (§4.106.4 / §5.106.5.3)

### Single-Family Residential (§4.106.4.1)
For new SFRs with attached/detached garage or dedicated parking:
- **One EV-capable** parking space per unit. EV-capable = raceway (conduit) from electrical panel to the space, panel space reserved, and a dedicated **208/240 V 40 A (minimum)** branch circuit identified.
- 2022: upgraded so the Level 2 **40 A** circuit is the standard.

### Multifamily Low-Rise (≤3 stories) (§4.106.4.2)

| Spaces provided | EV-Capable | EV-Ready | EVSE installed |
|-----------------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Per unit (1 space) | 10% of total | — | — (voluntary higher) |

(The 2024 CALGreen Supplement and many local reach codes require **EV-Ready** or installed EVSE at higher percentages.)

### Non-Residential (§5.106.5.3)

| Total spaces | EV-Capable / Ready / Installed |
|--------------|--------------------------------|
| 0–9 | 0 required (voluntary) |
| 10–25 | 4 EV-capable + 1 EV-ready + 0 EVSE |
| 26–50 | 8 EV-capable + 2 EV-ready + 0 EVSE |
| 51–75 | 13 EV-capable + 3 EV-ready + 0 EVSE |
| 76–100 | 17 EV-capable + 4 EV-ready + 0 EVSE |
| 101–150 | 25 EV-capable + 6 EV-ready + 0 EVSE |
| 151–200 | 35 EV-capable + 9 EV-ready + 1 EVSE |
| 201+ | 20% EV-capable + 5% EV-ready + small # installed |

Accessible EV space ratios: 1 per parking facility (or per ADA CBC 11B-812 for larger facilities).

## 7. Bicycle Parking (§5.106.4 — nonres) + CALGreen 4.106.9 (multifamily ≥3 units)

| Building | Short-term (visitor) | Long-term (employee/resident) |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Nonres (≥10 TV) | 5% of TV min, 2 space min | 5% of TV, locker/secure |
| Multifamily new (Ch 4) | 5% of dwelling units | 15% of dwelling units |

## 8. Heat-Island / Cool Roof & Paving (§5.106 / A5.106)

### Cool Roof (§A5.106.11 voluntary / §5.106.11 for nonres Tier 0 low-slope ≥2,000 sf)

| Roof slope | Solar Reflectance min (3-yr aged) | Thermal Emittance min | SRI min (alt path) |
|------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Low-slope (≤2:12) | 0.63 | 0.75 | 75 |
| Steep-slope (>2:12) | 0.20 | 0.75 | 16 |

Mandatory for:
- Nonresidential new construction
- **Multifamily ≥ 4 stories** (high-rise multifamily, R-2)

Cool roof requirements coordinate with Title 24 Part 6 §110.8; roofing products must be rated by the **Cool Roof Rating Council (CRRC)**.

### Cool Paving (voluntary A5.106.11.2)
Site paving with SR ≥0.30 (3-yr aged) for 50% of hardscape.

## 9. Commissioning & Testing (§5.410)

**Nonresidential ≥10,000 sf** requires:
- OPR (Owner's Project Requirements)
- Basis of Design (BOD)
- Commissioning Plan — HVAC, lighting, domestic hot water, renewable energy systems
- Functional performance testing + verification reports

**Testing & adjusting balancing (TAB)** required on all HVAC systems.

## 10. CALGreen Checklist (The Form)

Most CA jurisdictions require a **CALGreen Mandatory Measures Checklist** signed by the designer + installer submitted with permit. HCD provides the state template:

- **HCD-SHL-620** — Residential Mandatory Measures Checklist (2022)
- **HCD-SHL-621** — Nonresidential Mandatory Measures Checklist (2022)
- **HCD-SHL-625** — Residential Tier 1/2 Checklist (when adopted locally)

## 11. Tier 1 and Tier 2 (Voluntary) — Key Reach Topics

When a jurisdiction adopts Tier 1 or Tier 2 via reach code:

| Category | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Water savings | 30% reduction | 35–40% reduction |
| Envelope/Energy | 10% more efficient than Part 6 | 15% more efficient than Part 6 |
| Construction waste | 65% diversion (same as mandatory) | 80% diversion |
| Recycled materials | 10% | 15% |
| Bicycle parking | Higher ratios | Higher |
| Pollution control | HEPA filters during construction | Stricter |

## 12. 2025 CALGreen Preview (effective Jan 1, 2026)

- **Expanded EV-Ready** in multifamily (ramping up from 10% capable toward ~40% ready).
- **Embodied carbon** extends from nonres ≥100,000 sf in 2024 toward broader applicability in 2025 cycle.
- **Graywater / rainwater** stubs mandatory in more project types.
- **Battery storage** coordination with Part 6.
