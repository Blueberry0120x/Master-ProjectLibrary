# CBC Occupancy Classifications & Construction Types

**Scope:** CBC (2022) Chapters 3 (Use/Occupancy) and 6 (Types of Construction), with cross-references to Chapter 5 (heights/areas), Chapter 7 (fire-resistance), and CRC R313 (residential sprinklers). Focus on R-series occupancies and Type V/III construction used for infill and ADU work.

## 1. Residential Occupancy Groups (CBC 310)

| Group | What it is | Key trigger |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **R-1** | Transient boarding — hotels, motels, short-term (<30 days) | Transiency |
| **R-2** | Non-transient with **3 or more** dwelling units OR **more than 2 sleeping units** — apartments, condos, dormitories, congregate, monasteries | ≥3 dwellings |
| **R-2.1** | Residential care/assisted-living — ambulatory, non-medical 7+ clients | State-specific (CA) |
| **R-3** | 1 or 2 dwelling units not classified R-2, R-4; also care ≤6 clients; congregate ≤16 | 1–2 dwellings |
| **R-3.1** | Small residential facility (6 or fewer ambulatory) | State-specific |
| **R-4** | Residential care/assisted-living ≥6 to ≤16, non-ambulatory restricted | State-specific |

### ADUs and the Occupancy Question
An ADU is a **dwelling unit** (CBC Ch 2 definition — independent living with sleeping, cooking, sanitation). For code enforcement:
- **Detached ADU on a single-family lot** = primary + ADU = 2 dwellings → **R-3** (CRC applies).
- **Third unit (JADU)** on same lot still within a single-family residence = generally still R-3/CRC scope, but 3+ dwelling units on one lot can trigger R-2/CBC at some jurisdictions. Confirm with local interpretation.
- **Multiple detached ADUs on multifamily lot** → remains R-2 (CBC).

### CRC Applicability (CRC R101.2)
The **California Residential Code (Part 2.5)** applies to:
- Detached one- and two-family dwellings
- **Townhouses not more than three stories above grade plane**, each unit with its own means of egress
- Accessory structures

Everything else (R-1, R-2, R-4, mixed-use with R over B/M, buildings >3 stories) falls under the **CBC (Part 2)**.

## 2. Non-Residential Groups (cheat-sheet)

| Group | Use |
|-------|-----|
| A-1 to A-5 | Assembly (theater, church, nightclub, arena) |
| **B** | Business — office, bank, clinic (outpatient), education >12th grade |
| E | Educational K–12 |
| F-1/F-2 | Factory (moderate/low hazard) |
| H-1..H-5 | Hazardous |
| I-1 to I-4 | Institutional (assisted-living, hospital, prison, day care) |
| **M** | Mercantile — retail |
| S-1/S-2 | Storage (moderate/low hazard) |
| U | Utility/Miscellaneous (garages, sheds, fences) |

**Mixed-use M over R-2** (ground-floor retail with residential above) is the most common mixed occupancy for urban infill — triggers horizontal assembly separations per CBC Table 508.4 and often podium construction (Section 510.2).

## 5. Podium / Type III over I (CBC 510.2)

**"Podium" design** (also called **horizontal building separation**, §510.2):
- One or more stories of Type I-A construction as the "podium" (typically ground-floor retail/parking).
- Type III-A, V-A, or IV wood-frame residential (R-2) above.
- A **3-hour horizontal assembly** separates the lower Type I from the upper group, and the building above the separation is treated as a **separate building** for height/area purposes.
- Very common 5-over-1 and 5-over-2 mixed-use projects (III-A/V-A R-2 over I-A M/parking).

## 6. Fire Separation Distance & Exterior Opening Limits (CBC 705, Tables 705.5 & 705.8)

### Table 705.5 — Required fire-resistance of exterior walls (hours)

| Fire Separation Distance | Occupancy groups H | F-1, M, S-1, A, B, E, F-2, I, R, S-2, U |
|--------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| <5 ft | 3 | 1 (R, U, A, B, etc.) |
| 5 to <10 ft | 2 | 1 |
| 10 to <30 ft | 1 | 1 (Types IV/V) / 0 (I/II/III) for most |
| ≥30 ft | 0 | 0 |

### Table 705.8 — Maximum opening % based on fire separation distance (sprinklered values in parens for informational emphasis)

| Fire Separation Distance (ft) | Unprotected, No Sprinklers | Unprotected, Sprinklered | Protected |
|-------------------------------|----------------------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| 0 to <3 | Not Permitted | Not Permitted | Not Permitted |
| 3 to <5* | Not Permitted | 15% | 15% |
| 5 to <10 | 10% | 25% | 25% |
| 10 to <15 | 15% | 45% | 45% |
| 15 to <20 | 25% | 75% | 75% |
| 20 to <25 | 45% | No Limit | No Limit |
| 25 to <30 | 70% | No Limit | No Limit |
| ≥30 | No Limit | No Limit | No Limit |

\* R-3/CRC allows openings at 3 ft separation with sprinklers. CRC Table R302.1(1)/(2) governs one- and two-family dwellings and townhouses.

**CRC R302.1** (one- and two-family): exterior walls within **5 ft** of lot line require 1-hr rated walls; **projections** (eaves) within **2 ft** are not permitted; openings **<3 ft**: not permitted; **3 to <5 ft**: limited to 25% of wall area (not sprinklered) or 25% with residential sprinklers.

## 7. Sprinkler Triggers — NFPA 13 / 13R / 13D

| Standard | Scope | Water duration | Sprinkler in attic/garage? |
|----------|-------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **NFPA 13** | Full commercial/industrial; R-2 >4 stories or >60 ft | 30–60 min | Yes |
| **NFPA 13R** | R-2 **up to 4 stories and ≤60 ft** | 30 min | Attic combustible only; garages yes |
| **NFPA 13D** | 1- and 2-family dwellings, townhouses ≤3 stories | 10 min | Attic/garage exempt (CA amended to require garage sprinklers in many jurisdictions) |

### California Triggers
- **CRC R313** — requires NFPA 13D (or per R313) in **all new** one- and two-family dwellings and townhouses (effective in CA since 2011).
- **CBC 903.2.8** — R-2 occupancies require NFPA 13 or 13R throughout.
- **Local amendments** frequently require full 13 instead of 13R (e.g., increased hazard areas).
- **ADUs** — treated as dwellings; CRC R313 applies to the ADU. Many jurisdictions waive sprinklers when the primary dwelling is unsprinklered (Gov Code 65852.2(a)(1)(D)(vii)).

## 8. Quick Decision Tree for Small Residential Projects

```
Is project 1–2 dwellings (incl. ADU) and ≤3 stories?
  └─ YES → CRC applies → R-3 → CRC R313 13D sprinklers
            (exterior walls: CRC Table R302.1; height: CRC R301.1)
  └─ NO  → CBC applies
            ├─ 3+ dwellings? → R-2 → 13R (≤4 stories, ≤60 ft) or 13
            ├─ Mixed-use M/B over R? → Check 508.3/508.4 separation, 510.2 podium
            └─ Construction type → pick per Table 506.2 allowable area
```
