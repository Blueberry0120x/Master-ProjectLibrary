# CALGreen Compliance Checklist — Residential New Construction

Reference: 2022 CALGreen (Title 24 Part 11, Chapter 4 — Low-Rise Residential).
Applies to all new low-rise residential buildings (1–3 stories) in California.

---

## Chapter 4.1 — Planning and Design

### 4.106 — Storm Water Pollution Prevention

- [ ] **4.106.2** Erosion and sedimentation controls implemented during construction
  - Plans include: perimeter silt fence, inlet protection, concrete washout area
  - Stabilization of all disturbed areas before rain events

---

## Chapter 4.2 — Energy Efficiency

### 4.201 — Compliance

- [ ] **4.201.1** Building complies with Title 24 Part 6 (California Energy Code)
  - CF1R attached to plans
  - All mandatory energy measures noted on plans
  - HERS registration plan confirmed (if HERS measures used)

---

- [ ] Fixture schedule on plans matches the above limits
- [ ] Manufacturer model numbers or "CALGreen §4.303.1 compliant" noted on fixture schedule

### 4.303 — Outdoor Water Use

- [ ] **4.303.2** Outdoor landscaping complies with MWELO (Model Water Efficient Landscape
  Ordinance) if landscape area > 500 sf
  - Hydrozone plan prepared
  - Irrigation controller is "smart" (weather-based) per MWELO

---

## Chapter 4.4 — Material Conservation and Resource Efficiency

### 4.408 — Construction Waste Reduction

- [ ] **4.408.1** Construction Waste Management Plan submitted to building department
  - Plan identifies: materials to be diverted, recycling/disposal facilities, projected tonnage
  - Projected diversion rate ≥ **65%** by weight

- [ ] **4.408.2** Waste management documentation maintained on site during construction
  - Hauler receipts collected from recycling facilities
  - Completion form submitted to jurisdiction at project close

- [ ] **4.408.4** (if using this compliance path) Construction waste not to exceed
  3.4 lbs/sf (implies ≥ 65% diversion for standard new residential at ~4.0 lbs/sf base)

> **NOTE:** 4.0 lbs/sf base factor and × 1.04 stucco/2-story modifier source is
> unconfirmed. Do not cite these specific values on submitted forms without verifying
> with the jurisdiction's CALGreen desk. See `report/factual-sweep.md`.

- [ ] **4.410.1** Excavated soil and land clearing debris: 100% diverted from landfill
  (reuse on-site, export to another project, or compost)

---

## Chapter 4.5 — Environmental Quality

### 4.503 — Fireplaces

- [ ] **4.503.1** If gas fireplace: direct-vent with sealed combustion chamber,
  OR no continuous natural gas pilot light (electronic ignition only)
- [ ] If no fireplace: N/A

### 4.504 — Pollutant Control

- [ ] **4.504.1** Adhesives, sealants, caulks: VOC content complies with SCAQMD
  Rule 1168 or Green Seal Standard
  - Note on plans: "All adhesives and sealants shall comply with CALGreen §4.504.1 VOC limits"

- [ ] **4.504.2** Composite wood products (cabinets, subfloor, sheathing): no added
  urea-formaldehyde (NAUF) or ultra-low emitting formaldehyde (ULEF) per CARB ATCMs
  - Note on plans: "All composite wood shall be NAUF/ULEF per CALGreen §4.504.2"

- [ ] **4.504.3** Resilient flooring: VOC emissions per CDPH Standard Method v1.2
  (if resilient flooring specified)

- [ ] **4.504.4** Thermal insulation: formaldehyde-free or CDPH certified
  (if fiberglass batt or blown-in)

### 4.505 — Interior Moisture Control

- [ ] **4.505.1** Vapor retarder installed under slab:
  - Minimum 10-mil polyethylene sheet (or equal) directly under concrete slab
  - Joints lapped 6 in and sealed, or overlapping by 12 in
  - Note on foundation plan: "Vapor retarder: 10-mil poly per CALGreen §4.505.1"

### 4.506 — Indoor Air Quality

- [ ] **4.506.1** Exhaust fans installed in all bathrooms and kitchen:
  - Bathroom fans: sized per ASHRAE 62.2 (minimum 50 CFM continuous or 100 CFM intermittent)
  - Ducted to exterior — no attic discharge
  - Note on plans: "Bath exhaust fans: 50 CFM (cont.) / 100 CFM (int.), ducted to exterior per CALGreen §4.506.1"

### 4.507 — Environmental Comfort

- [ ] **4.507.2** Heating and cooling systems designed per ACCA Manual J (load calcs)
  and Manual S (equipment selection)
  - Load calculations performed; equipment sized to loads (not rule-of-thumb)
  - Note on plans: "HVAC system sized per ACCA Manual J load analysis per CALGreen §4.507.2"

## Notes on Tier 1 / Tier 2

- This checklist covers **mandatory measures only** (base compliance, no local tier).
- If the jurisdiction has adopted Tier 1 or Tier 2, additional measures are required.
- Verify Tier adoption with the local Building Division at submittal.
- **San Diego City:** No confirmed Tier adoption as of 2025.
- **Chula Vista:** CAP reach code functions as an energy-focused "reach" above state baseline.
