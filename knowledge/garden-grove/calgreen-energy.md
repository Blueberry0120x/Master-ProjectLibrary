## 2. Construction & Demolition Debris Management Plan (CDDMP)

Garden Grove uses a **CDDMP** (not San Diego's WMF) to comply with CALGreen 4.408.

### CDDMP Requirements

- **Required for:** All new construction, additions > 500 sf, demolition projects.
- **Diversion goal:** ≥ 65% by weight of total C&D debris.
- **Submit with:** Building permit application.
- **Completion form:** Submit after project completion with hauler receipts.

### What the CDDMP Must Include

1. Project address, APN, permit number.
2. Estimated total C&D tonnage (use CalRecycle factors).
3. Material breakdown: concrete, wood framing, drywall, metals, asphalt, other.
4. Planned diversion method: recycling facility, reuse, salvage.
5. Name and address of recycling/disposal facilities.
6. Projected diversion percentage (must be ≥ 65%).

### Tonnage Calculation (Residential New Construction)

| Project Type | Base Factor |
|-------------|-------------|
| New wood-frame residential | 4.0 lbs/sf |
| Stucco modifier (2-story) | × 1.04 |
| Calculation | sf × factor × modifier ÷ 2,000 = total tons |

Example — 1,200 sf, 2-story, stucco:
`1,200 × 4.0 × 1.04 / 2,000 = 2.50 tons`

## 3. Title 24 Part 6 — Energy Compliance

Garden Grove enforces 2022 Title 24 Part 6 (California Energy Code) per CEC standards.
No local reach code has been confirmed — **verify with Building Division.**

### Compliance Path (Residential New Construction)

- **Performance approach:** CF1R generated by CEC-approved software (EnergyPro, CBECC-Res).
- **Prescriptive approach:** Limited applicability — performance almost always required for new SFD/ADU.
- CF1R must be attached to permit application and plan set.

### Mandatory Measures (All Residential)

| Section | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| §110.0–110.12 | Certificate requirements, fenestration, HVAC |
| §150.0(a) | Insulation — walls, floors, ceilings per climate zone |
| §150.0(c) | Fenestration U-factor and SHGC per climate zone |
| §150.0(d) | Vapor retarder under slab |
| §150.0(m)(1) | HVAC duct sealing and insulation |
| §150.0(n) | Water heating — solar-ready or heat pump WH per CEC |
| §150.1(c)7 | EV charging — conduit rough-in for SFD |
| §150.1(c)14 | Solar-ready zone for PV |

### Climate Zone

Garden Grove is in **CEC Climate Zone 8** (coastal OC / greater LA basin).

Key Zone 8 characteristics:
- Moderate heating and cooling loads.
- Higher internal gain assumptions.
- Lower fenestration SHGC requirement than inland zones.

### HERS-Verified Measures

The following commonly trigger HERS verification in Zone 8:
- Central air conditioning systems (duct leakage test)
- Mini-split / ductless systems (if credited in compliance)
- High-efficiency water heaters (heat pump WH)
- Verified insulation (blown-in or spray foam)

HERS raters: CalCERTS (calcerts.com), CHEERS (cheers.net), or PG&E/SCE-approved raters.

## 4. Local Energy Reach Codes

As of 2024, Garden Grove had **not adopted a local energy reach code** beyond the state
2022 Part 6 baseline. Confirm with Building Division before submittal whether a 2025
reach code has been adopted effective Jan 1, 2026.

## 5. Solar & Battery Storage

- **Solar-ready zone** required for all new SFD per §150.1(c)14.
- **PV system installation:** Streamlined permit under SB 379 — no discretionary review,
  OTC plan check for qualifying systems.
- **Battery storage (ESS):** Separate electrical permit; may require fire department
  review for large systems (> 20 kWh).
