# Garden Grove — Fire Code & GGFD Requirements

Source: Garden Grove Municipal Code Title 9 (local CFC amendments) + 2022 California
Fire Code (CFC, Title 24 Part 9). Garden Grove Fire Department (GGFD): (714) 741-5550.

---

## 1. Applicable Codes

| Code | Applicability |
|------|--------------|
| 2022 California Fire Code (CFC) | Statewide baseline; effective Jan 1, 2023 |
| GGMC Title 9 — Fire Code amendments | Local amendments to CFC; verify current adoption |
| NFPA 13D (2019 CA ed.) | Residential fire sprinklers — one- and two-family dwellings |
| NFPA 13R | Residential sprinklers — up to 4-story multifamily |
| NFPA 72 | Fire alarm and detection systems |

---

## 2. Fire Sprinkler Requirements

### Single-Family Dwellings (New Construction)

Garden Grove has adopted a **local amendment requiring NFPA 13D fire sprinklers
in all new one- and two-family dwellings** (SFD) per GGMC Title 9.
- **Trigger:** New SFD construction.
- **ADU:** Confirm with GGFD whether a new detached ADU triggers sprinkler
  requirement independently or only when the primary dwelling is sprinklered.
  Call GGFD at (714) 741-5550 before designing the system.
- **Additions:** Generally sprinklers not required in an addition unless the
  addition triggers a threshold (e.g., > 50% of existing value) — verify with GGFD.

> **CAUTION:** Sprinkler requirements in CA cities vary significantly. Always
> confirm the current local threshold with GGFD before designing or submitting.

### Multifamily (R-2, 3+ units)

- NFPA 13R required for R-2 occupancies up to 4 stories per CRC R313 + CFC.
- NFPA 13 (full) required for R-2 buildings > 4 stories.
- Deferred submittal: sprinkler plans submitted to GGFD after building permit issuance.

### Deferred Submittal

Sprinkler and fire alarm plans are typically a **deferred submittal** — the building
permit is approved first with a note "Fire sprinkler system — deferred submittal," then
sprinkler contractor submits plans separately to GGFD for review and approval.

## 3. Fire Alarm & Detection

| Occupancy | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| SFD (new) | Smoke alarms per CRC R314 — hardwired with battery backup, interconnected |
| SFD with sprinklers | Smoke alarms still required; sprinklers do not substitute |
| ADU | Smoke alarms per CRC R314; CO detector if gas appliances or attached garage |
| R-2 (multifamily) | Full fire alarm per NFPA 72 if above threshold; detectors in all units |

### Smoke Alarm Locations (CRC R314)

- Inside each sleeping room.
- Outside each sleeping area (hallway).
- On each floor level including basement.
- On the ceiling or high on the wall (top 12 inches).

### Carbon Monoxide Detector (CRC R315)

Required in dwellings with fuel-burning appliances, attached garage, or fireplace.
Location: outside each sleeping area; on every level.

## 4. Fire Access & Addressing

### Street Address

- Numbers visible from street, minimum 4-inch high characters, contrasting color.
- ADU must have a separate address or unit number displayed.

### Emergency Vehicle Access

- Fire apparatus access road: minimum 20 ft clear width, 13 ft 6 in vertical clearance.
- Dead-end roads > 150 ft require a turnaround (cul-de-sac or hammer-head).
- Gates across access roads require Knox Box or Knox key switch.

### Fire Hydrant Proximity

- New SFD: fire hydrant within 600 ft of main structure (measured along road).
- Flow requirement: 1,000 gpm minimum for SFD.

## 5. Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI)

Garden Grove is largely urban infill with minimal wildland exposure. **Most parcels
are NOT in a State Responsibility Area (SRA) or VHFHSZ.** Always verify on the
CAL FIRE FHSZ map for the specific parcel before assuming no WUI requirements.

If parcel IS in a VHFHSZ:
- Chapter 7A CBC ignition-resistant construction materials apply.
- Defensible space (100 ft or to property line) is required.

| Zone | Distance | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Zone 1 ("Lean, Clean, and Green") | 0–30 ft | Remove dead/dry vegetation; no combustible mulch within 3 ft of structure |
| Zone 2 ("Reduced Fuel Zone") | 30–100 ft | Space trees and shrubs; remove ladder fuels |

CAL FIRE FHSZ map: https://www.fire.ca.gov/what-we-do/fire-prevention/fire-hazard-severity-zones

## 6. GGFD Plan Review Process

1. Building Division routes plans to GGFD concurrent with building plan check.
2. GGFD reviews fire access, hydrants, sprinkler notes, alarm notes.
3. GGFD issues corrections as part of consolidated plan check report.
4. Sprinkler/alarm plans: separate deferred submittal to GGFD after building permit issued.
5. Final fire inspection required before Certificate of Occupancy.

**GGFD contact:** (714) 741-5550 | https://www.gardengrove.org/fire
