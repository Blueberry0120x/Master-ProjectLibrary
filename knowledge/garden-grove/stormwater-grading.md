# Garden Grove — Stormwater & Grading

Source: OC NPDES Drainage Area Management Plan (DAMP) + City of Garden Grove
Municipal Code Title 13 (Public Services) + OC Low Impact Development Standards.

---

## 1. Regulatory Framework

Garden Grove is in the **Orange County MS4 NPDES permit** area (San Diego Regional
Water Quality Control Board jurisdiction for southern OC).

| Authority | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Santa Ana Regional Water Quality Control Board (SARWQCB) | MS4 permit, NPDES enforcement for most of OC including Garden Grove |
| OC Public Works — Watershed Programs | DAMP administration, technical guidance |
| City of Garden Grove Public Works | Local grading, drainage, encroachment permits |
| City of Garden Grove Building Division | Stormwater review at plan check |

**Primary documents:**
- OC NPDES Drainage Area Management Plan (DAMP) — most recent edition
- OC Low Impact Development (LID) Standards Manual
- GGMC Title 13 — Public Services (grading, drainage)

## 2. Stormwater Requirements — New Construction

### Project Tier Determination

| Condition | Tier |
|-----------|------|
| New construction creating ≥ 10,000 sf impervious surface | **Priority Project** — LID/WQMP required |
| New construction < 10,000 sf impervious | **Standard Project** — basic BMPs required |
| Single-family residential ≤ 1 acre | Standard Project (typically) |
| Redevelopment replacing ≥ 5,000 sf impervious in priority categories | May be Priority Project |

**ADUs and residential additions typically fall under Standard Projects** (< 10,000 sf).
Verify with Building Division at submittal.

### Standard Project Requirements

For projects below Priority Project threshold:
1. **Site Design BMPs** — direct runoff to pervious areas, disconnect impervious from
   drains, use permeable pavers where feasible.
2. **Source Control BMPs** — proper trash enclosure, material storage away from drains,
   landscape with low-fertilizer plants.
3. **Construction BMPs** — erosion control, sediment control, concrete washout area,
   materials management.

Submit a **Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)** or **Best Management
Practices (BMP) Site Plan** per OC DAMP requirements. Verify current form with
Building Division.

### Priority Project (LID/WQMP) Requirements

If project meets Priority Project criteria:
- **Water Quality Management Plan (WQMP)** required — prepared by licensed civil engineer.
- LID treatment control BMPs (bioretention, infiltration trenches, green roofs, etc.).
- Hydromodification management if project increases peak runoff.
- Submit WQMP with building permit application; reviewed by city and/or OC Public Works.

## 3. Grading Permits

| Condition | Permit Required |
|-----------|----------------|
| Cut or fill > 50 cubic yards | Grading permit from Public Works |
| Cut or fill creating slopes > 3:1 | Grading permit |
| Any grading in a floodplain | Grading permit + floodplain development permit |
| Grading within 50 ft of a watercourse | Additional review |
| ADU with minimal grading | May be exempt — verify with Public Works (714) 741-5181 |

### Grading Plan Requirements

- Prepared by licensed Civil Engineer (CA PE).
- Show existing and proposed contours, cut/fill quantities, drainage patterns.
- Erosion and sediment control plan.
- Soils report if cuts > 5 ft or fills > 3 ft.
- Drainage study for sites with drainage issues.

## 4. Flood Zone Compliance

Garden Grove parcels near the Santa Ana River or tributary channels may be
in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas (SFHA — Zone AE).

Check the FEMA Flood Map Service Center (MSC) for your parcel:
https://msc.fema.gov/portal/home

If in Zone AE:
- Finished floor elevation must be at or above Base Flood Elevation (BFE) + 1 ft freeboard.
- No fill in floodway without compensatory storage.
- Floodplain development permit from City of Garden Grove required.
- LOMA (Letter of Map Amendment) may be needed if site is inadvertently included in SFHA.

## 5. Construction-Phase NPDES (CGP)

For **land disturbance ≥ 1 acre**, the **State Construction General Permit (CGP)**
applies (separate from local SWPPP):
- File **Notice of Intent (NOI)** with SWRCB SMARTS system before grading.
- Prepare and implement SWPPP.
- File **Notice of Termination (NOT)** after final stabilization.

Most SFD/ADU projects are < 1 acre and do not trigger CGP. Larger infill or
subdivision projects may cross the threshold.

CGP: https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/water_issues/programs/stormwater/construction.html

---

## 6. Post-Construction Maintenance

For Priority Projects with structural BMPs (bioretention, detention basin, etc.):
- **Operation & Maintenance (O&M) Agreement** recorded against the property.
- Owner responsible for perpetual maintenance of all BMPs.
- Inspection required annually; records kept on site.
