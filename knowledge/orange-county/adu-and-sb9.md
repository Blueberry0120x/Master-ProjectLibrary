# Orange County — ADU & SB 9 (Unincorporated)

**Jurisdiction:** This reference covers **unincorporated Orange County only**.
Each of the 34 incorporated OC cities has its own adopted ADU ordinance and SB
9 ordinance; check the city's Municipal Code for parcels inside city limits.

---

## State Framework (Applies Everywhere in CA)

### ADU Statutes (Gov. Code § 65852.2 and § 65852.22)

Recent key bills that established current baseline (already codified):
- **AB 68 (2019), AB 881 (2019), AB 670 (2019), AB 587 (2019)** — core reforms
- **SB 13 (2019)** — impact fee waivers, 2-unit allowance
- **AB 3182 (2020)** — sale of ADU allowed in certain conditions
- **AB 345 (2021), AB 2221 (2022), AB 916 (2022)** — clarifications, height
- **AB 1033 (2023)** — allows sale of ADUs as condos if city opts in
- **AB 1332 (2023)** — pre-approved ADU plan program (city must adopt by 2025)
- **AB 976 (2023)** — prohibits owner-occupancy requirement (permanent)
- **AB 2533 (2024)** — unpermitted ADU legalization through 2030
- **AB 2335 (2024)** — streamlining, no minimum lot size

### SB 9 (2021) — Gov. Code § 65852.21 and § 66411.7

- Permits **up to 2 dwelling units** on a single-family lot (urban lot split
  allows 2 lots × 2 units = 4 total in urbanized areas).
- By-right (ministerial) on qualifying parcels in urbanized / urban-cluster
  single-family zones.
- Excludes: historic districts, coastal hazard zones, farmland, high fire areas
  not addressed, unit restrictions if demolition/alteration of protected housing.

## Unincorporated OC ADU Ordinance

**Code location:** OC Codified Ordinance **Title 7, Division 7, § 7-9-146** (or
current ADU section — County has updated multiple times to conform with state
amendments). Check Municode for current numbering.

### Permitted Types

| Type | Description | Max Size |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Detached ADU** | Separate structure | **1,200 sf** max (state baseline) |
| **Attached ADU** | Added to primary SFD | 50% of primary or **1,200 sf** max |
| **Interior conversion ADU** | Within existing SFD or accessory structure | Within existing envelope |
| **Junior ADU (JADU)** | Within existing SFD, efficiency kitchen, up to **500 sf** | Owner-occupancy OK for JADU |
| **Statewide exempt ADU** (Gov. Code § 65852.2(e)) | Up to **800 sf**, 16 ft height, 4 ft setbacks | Must be ministerially approved |

### Development Standards (State-Floor — County Cannot Be More Restrictive)

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Setbacks (detached)** | Min **4 ft** side and rear; 0 ft allowed for conversion of existing structure |
| **Front setback** | Same as primary residence per base zone (but cannot block state-exempt 800 sf ADU) |
| **Height — detached ADU** | **16 ft** by right; **18 ft** with 1 story on lot served by major transit or in multifamily zone; **25 ft** or 2 stories for attached ADU matching primary |
| **Lot coverage** | Cannot be used to preclude 800 sf ADU |
| **Parking** | 1 space per ADU OR per bedroom; **WAIVED** if within ½ mi walking of transit, in historic district, on same lot as SFD, in permit-parking area, or for car-share |
| **Passageway / separate entrance** | Required |
| **Owner occupancy** | **Prohibited** for ADUs (permanent per AB 976); allowed for JADU |
| **Fire sprinklers** | Only if required for primary SFD; new ADU gets 13D per CRC R313 |
| **Separate utility meter** | Not required by state; water/sewer connection fees capped for ADU < 750 sf (fee = $0) |
| **Separate sale** | Generally prohibited except condominium process under AB 1033 (requires local opt-in — verify OC adoption status) |

### Processing

- **Ministerial** (no CEQA, no hearing) — **60-day** decision (state law).
- Permit application via OCDS Accela portal.
- Concurrent plan check with building.

### Pre-Approved ADU Plans (AB 1332)

State law requires jurisdictions to offer a **pre-approved plan program** by
**Jan 1, 2025**. Confirm current status of OC unincorporated program via OCDS
Planning. Benefits: reduced plan check fee/time, faster permit.

## ADUs in the Coastal Zone

- ADUs in the Coastal Zone require a **Coastal Development Permit (CDP)** in
  addition to building permit, unless exempt or categorically excluded.
- Certified LCP (or CCC if no LCP) governs.
- ADU standards cannot violate LCP protections (public access, ESHA, coastal
  hazards).
- **CCC guidance:** ADUs are generally supported as lower-cost housing, but
  siting must respect coastal resources.

## SB 9 Applicability in Unincorporated OC

### Qualifying Parcels

SB 9 applies to parcels that meet **all**:
- Zoned single-family residential (R1 in OC)
- Located in an **urbanized area or urban cluster** (per US Census)
- Not historic, not prime farmland, not high/very-high fire hazard without
  mitigation, not coastal wetlands, not in flood plain without mitigation
- Not demolishing > 25% of existing exterior walls (unless specific conditions)
- Not displacing tenants in place for past 3 years
- Not subject to recorded rent/moderate-income deed restriction

In OC, **much of the unincorporated coastal-adjacent R1** would not qualify due
to VHFHSZ/coastal overlays; most **unincorporated pockets** (Orange Park Acres,
Rossmoor, North Tustin, Midway City, Covenant Hills if applicable) need to be
checked against SB 9 exclusion criteria.

### SB 9 — 2 Units on a Lot

- Up to 2 primary units on a single-family lot (duplex or 2 SFDs).
- 4 ft side/rear setbacks max.
- Each unit up to **800 sf** protected by state statute.
- Ministerial review, no hearing, no CEQA.

### SB 9 — Urban Lot Split

- Lot split into 2 parcels, each min **1,200 sf** (or 40% of original, whichever
  greater).
- Parcelator must owner-occupy one unit for **3 years** after split (affidavit).
- Not applicable to lots created through prior SB 9 split.
- Not applicable if applicant, or person acting in concert, has split an
  adjacent lot under SB 9.

### County Implementation

OC has adopted a local SB 9 ordinance (check Title 7 Div 7 current section —
typically § 7-9-146 or § 7-9-150 equivalent). Local objective standards may
include:
- Objective design standards (materials, roof pitch, massing)
- Trash enclosure requirements
- Driveway/curb cut standards
- Tree protection

## Pre-Approved Plan Program

Per AB 1332, required statewide by Jan 1, 2025. OC unincorporated program
status — verify at OCDS Planning counter. Where implemented, benefits:
- Plan check fee reduction (often 50%+)
- Expedited issuance (target 30 days)
- Pre-stamped structural / T24 / CALGreen sheets

---

## Short-Term Rental / STR Restriction

- AB 587: ADU **cannot be rented short-term (< 30 days)** — permanent state
  rule in most cases.
- County STR ordinance may impose additional restrictions (verify current OC
  STR moratorium / permit program).
