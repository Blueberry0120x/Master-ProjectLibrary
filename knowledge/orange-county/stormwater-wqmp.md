# Orange County — Stormwater, WQMP & Hydromodification

**Authority:** OC Public Works (OCPW) — Environmental Resources / Watersheds
Program + OC Flood Control District for regional drainage.
**Permittees:** County of Orange + 34 incorporated cities (co-permittees under
MS4 Permits).

---

## MS4 Permit Framework

Orange County is split between two Regional Water Quality Control Boards:

| Region | Cities / Area | Regional Board | Permit |
|--------|---------------|----------------|--------|
| **North OC** | County area draining to SA River and coastal drainage north of Newport Bay; most of N/Central OC cities | **Santa Ana RWQCB (Region 8)** | **Order R8-2009-0030 (NPDES CAS618030)** as amended — "North OC MS4" |
| **South OC** | Aliso, Salt, San Juan, San Mateo watersheds; South OC cities (Laguna Beach, Mission Viejo, San Clemente, Rancho Santa Margarita, etc.) | **San Diego RWQCB (Region 9)** | **Order R9-2013-0001 as amended by R9-2015-0001, R9-2015-0100** — South OC MS4 (part of Regional MS4 Permit) |

**Be aware which watershed applies** — WQMP triggers, Priority Project categories,
and hydromodification requirements differ between Region 8 and Region 9 sides.

## Countywide Drainage Area Management Plan (DAMP)

The **Orange County DAMP** (North OC) and **South OC Model WQMP** implement MS4
obligations. Current editions:
- **DAMP** (Region 8) — maintained by OC Public Works; last major update ~2011,
  amended periodically.
- **Model WQMP + Technical Guidance Document (TGD)** — latest 2011/2013 with
  updates.
- **South Orange County Hydromodification Management Plan (HMP)** applies in
  Region 9.

Download from: https://ocflood.com / https://ocstormwater.com

## Priority Projects (Require Project WQMP)

A project is a **Priority Project** if it meets any of the following (Region 8
DAMP Section 7 — Categories A–I approximately; Region 9 Model WQMP similar):

| Category | Trigger |
|----------|---------|
| **A** | Significant redevelopment ≥ 5,000 sf — replacing ≥ 50% impervious triggers full WQMP |
| **B** | New development ≥ 10,000 sf of impervious surface |
| **C** | Commercial / industrial ≥ 100,000 sf |
| **D** | Automotive repair shops |
| **E** | Restaurants (SIC 5812) ≥ 5,000 sf |
| **F** | Hillside development ≥ 5,000 sf with natural slopes ≥ 25% |
| **G** | Environmentally Sensitive Area (ESA) — any project adjacent to ESA / 303(d) water / ASBS |
| **H** | Parking lots ≥ 5,000 sf (or ≥ 15 spaces) exposed to rainfall |
| **I** | Retail gasoline outlets ≥ 5,000 sf and ≥ 100 ADT |

Also: single-family hillside residential in Category F triggers WQMP even though
small.

### Non-Priority Projects

Smaller projects still require **site-design BMPs** and construction ESCP, but
not a full Project WQMP.

## Project WQMP Required Contents

1. **Project narrative** — location, size, land use
2. **Pollutants of concern (POC)** — by land use, impaired water receiving
3. **Site design BMPs (SD-xx)** — minimize imperviousness, preserve natural areas,
   disperse runoff
4. **Source control BMPs (SC-xx)** — trash enclosures, storm drain stenciling,
   landscape BMPs
5. **Treatment Control BMPs (TC-xx)** — sized via LID hierarchy (see below)
6. **Hydromodification analysis** — if in HMP area
7. **Operation & Maintenance Plan** — who, how, frequency; 15-year maintenance
   funding
8. **Recorded WQMP** — covenant recorded against property for perpetual
   maintenance (OC form)

## LID Hierarchy (Required Sizing Order)

All Priority Projects must address the **Design Capture Volume (DCV)** — typically
the **85th percentile 24-hr storm** per Model WQMP (approx 0.6"–1.0" in OC).
Treatment must be designed following the hierarchy:

1. **Infiltration** — bioretention, infiltration basin, dry well, permeable
   pavement. Preferred where feasible (soils K ≥ 0.3 in/hr typical; geotech
   confirmation; clearance to groundwater, structures).
2. **Harvest & Use** — cisterns, rainwater capture for irrigation/toilets if
   demand exists.
3. **Biotreatment** — bioretention with underdrain, flow-through planters,
   proprietary media filters (only if 1 & 2 infeasible, with feasibility
   showing).
4. **Offsite mitigation / in-lieu fee** — last resort per MS4 provisions.

**Infeasibility findings** must be documented with geotech data
(infiltration rate, groundwater, expansive soils, contamination, slope).

## Hydromodification Management (HMP)

Applies in designated watersheds to prevent erosion in receiving streams:

- **South OC (Region 9):** South Orange County HMP, mandatory for projects
  adding ≥ 10,000 sf impervious in designated HMP areas (Aliso, Salt Cr,
  San Juan, San Mateo watersheds).
- **North OC (Region 8):** Hydrologic Conditions of Concern (HCOC) analysis;
  HMP applies per DAMP Section 7.II-3 where channels are erodible.

**Performance standard:** Post-project flow-duration curve must match pre-
project (or baseline unpaved condition) for 10%–Q10 range. Typically requires
**extended detention + infiltration** or an engineered HMP treatment train.

**Exempt:** Projects draining to tidal waters, concrete channels, or sumps;
infiltrating 100% of DCV.

## Construction-Phase Requirements

### Erosion & Sediment Control Plan (ESCP) / WPCP

Required for all grading and construction activities:
- Silt fence, fiber rolls, stabilized construction entrance
- Concrete washout area
- Dewatering controls
- Stockpile management
- Inlet protection
- Portable toilets min 5 ft from drain inlets

### Construction General Permit (CGP) — SWRCB Order 2022-0057-DWQ

Required for projects **≥ 1 acre** of disturbed soil (or part of common plan
of development ≥ 1 acre):
- File **Notice of Intent (NOI)** via SMARTS.
- **SWPPP** prepared by QSD (Qualified SWPPP Developer).
- QSP (Qualified SWPPP Practitioner) implements.
- Risk Level 1/2/3 determined by site hazard / receiving water.
- NOT submittal.

### Dewatering

- NPDES Dewatering General Permit (Region 8: R8-2015-0004; Region 9: R9-2015-
  0013 or similar current order) for dewatering discharges to MS4 or surface
  water.

## OC Local Drainage Manual (OC Flood Control District)

**OC Hydrology Manual** (1986, amended) + **OC Hydraulics Manual** — basis for
all drainage analysis in unincorporated areas and many OC cities:
- Rational Method for areas < 640 ac
- Hydrograph methods (e.g., AES-2005) for larger basins
- Design storms: 10-yr local, 25-yr collector, 100-yr regional
- Manning's n, HEC-RAS, HEC-HMS guidance

Specific product: **Local Drainage Manual** governs on-site and tributary
drainage design for development permits.

**OC Flood Control Channel encroachment:** Separate permit from OCFCD for any
work within channel ROW or adjacent to regional flood facility.

---

## Trash Amendments (SWRCB 2015)

Full trash capture required:
- Priority Land Uses (PLUs): high-density residential, industrial, commercial,
  mixed-use, public transit stations
- Certified full-capture devices (CPS, connector pipe screens, trash nets, large
  inline CDS units) OR land-use-based compliance via institutional controls
- OC cities implement via MS4 via certified trash capture inventory.
