# Orange County Fire Authority (OCFA) — Fire Code & Submittals

**Agency:** Orange County Fire Authority — JPA serving 24 member cities + all
unincorporated Orange County (~71% of county population).
**Division for plan review:** Community Risk Reduction → **Planning & Development
Services (P&DS)**.
**Portal:** https://ocfa.org → Community Risk Reduction → Planning & Development
**HQ:** 1 Fire Authority Rd, Irvine, CA 92602 — main (714) 573-6000 / P&DS
(714) 573-6100 (verify).

---

## Fire Code Adoption

OCFA adopts the **2022 California Fire Code (CFC)** with **OCFA local amendments**
via ordinance in each member jurisdiction. Core documents:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **2022 CFC + OCFA Amendments** | Base fire code |
| **OCFA Guidelines (C-##, B-##, etc.)** | Technical standards & submittal requirements |

Amendment highlights (2022 OCFA):
- Residential sprinklers required per CRC § R313 (state standard).
- Extended fire flow / hydrant spacing in VHFHSZ.
- Fuel modification requirements per **Guideline C-05 / B-09**.
- Defensible space under PRC § 4291 (state) + OCFA enforcement.

**Next cycle:** 2025 CFC effective **Jan 1, 2026**. OCFA typically publishes
amendment comparison document Q4 2025.

## Fire Master Plan (Guideline C-01)

Required at **tentative map / site development permit** stage for:
- Subdivisions (5+ lots / any lot ≥ 1 acre in VHFHSZ)
- Multifamily ≥ 4 units
- Commercial / industrial projects > 5,000 sf
- Any project in VHFHSZ or SRA

Must show:
- All fire apparatus access roads with widths, grades, turnaround
- Hydrant locations and spacing with fire flow targets
- Fire department connections (FDC)
- Knox box / gate locations
- Fuel modification zones (if applicable)
- Building addresses and emergency access routes

Approval of Fire Master Plan is a prerequisite to issuance of building permits.

## Fire Apparatus Access (Guideline C-03 / CFC § 503)

| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Clear width (unobstructed)** | **20 ft** minimum; 26 ft adjacent to hydrants (for aerial where required) |
| **Vertical clearance** | **13 ft 6 in** minimum |
| **Grade** | **10% max** standard; up to **15%** with OCFA approval and design verification |
| **Turning radius** | 28 ft inside / 48 ft outside (OCFA standard truck template); verify current apparatus standard |
| **Dead-end length** | **150 ft max** before approved turnaround required |
| **Turnaround types** | 96 ft Ø cul-de-sac, 120 ft hammerhead (T-type), or approved Y-turn |
| **Surface** | All-weather, H-20 load rated (paved or engineered) |
| **Gates** | Knox key switch + minimum 20 ft clear through gate |
| **Fire lane marking** | Red curb + "NO PARKING FIRE LANE" signage per C-03 |

**Triggering when access required:**
- Any portion of building > 150 ft from public way (CFC § 503.1.1).
- Buildings > 3 stories or > 30 ft high.
- All commercial / multifamily structures.

## Water Supply & Hydrants (Guideline C-04)

Fire flow per **CFC Appendix B** (adopted with OCFA amendments):

| Building Size (sf, CT III-B) | Req Fire Flow (gpm) | Duration |
|------------------------------|---------------------|----------|
| ≤ 3,600 (SFD) | 1,000 | 1 hr |
| 3,601 – 4,800 | 1,500 | 1 hr |
| 4,801 – 6,200 | 1,750 | 1 hr |
| > 6,200 | per table | 2+ hr |

Sprinklered buildings: reduction per CFC Appendix B (typically 50% reduction
for NFPA 13, not for 13D).

**Hydrant spacing (CFC Appendix C w/ OCFA amendment):**
- SFD subdivision: max **600 ft** between hydrants, max **300 ft** to farthest
  point of any lot.
- Commercial: max **300 ft** between hydrants along access road.
- Hydrant within **150 ft** of FDC connection.

## Sprinklers (Guideline B-01)

Required per CRC / CBC / CFC and OCFA amendments:
- **All new one- and two-family dwellings + ADUs** — NFPA 13D (state mandate CRC § R313).
- **All new multifamily R-2** — NFPA 13R (≤ 4 stories) or NFPA 13.
- **New commercial** by CBC § 903.2 occupancy-specific thresholds (generally
  ≥ 5,000 sf A/B/M/E/F/S; lower for A-1, H, I).
- **Existing buildings:** triggers at changes of occupancy or substantial
  additions per CBC Ch 34 / CEBC.

Deferred submittal: shop drawings + hydraulic calcs + cut sheets to OCFA.

## Fuel Modification (Guidelines B-09 / C-05)

**Required when:**
- Project is in or adjacent to a **Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zone (VHFHSZ)**
  or **State Responsibility Area (SRA)**, OR
- **Any wildland fuel within 200 ft** of proposed structures, OR
- Site is in a foothill / canyon Specific Plan area (Foothill/Trabuco, Ladera,
  Coto de Caza, RMV, etc.).

### FMP Zones

| Zone | Typical Width | Treatment |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| **Zone A — Wet Zone (Setback Zone)** | **50 ft** from structure (minimum 30 ft) | Irrigated, permanent landscape, fire-resistive plant list, no flammable fuels; structural hardscape allowed |
| **Zone B — Thinning Zone** | **120 ft** combined with Zone A in high-hazard (typical total 170 ft from structure; varies) | Native vegetation thinned, spacing per OCFA spacing chart, irrigation optional |
| **Zone C — Native / Transition Zone** | Remainder / interface | Selective removal of dead fuel, retention of native cover |

**Documentation:** FMP must be prepared by OCFA-approved consultant, show all
vegetation, zones, irrigation, HOA maintenance commitment, and recorded CC&Rs
obligating long-term maintenance.

**Timing:** FMP conceptual approval at SDP/tract stage; final approval and
installation inspection prior to combustible materials on site.

### Interaction with PRC § 4291 (Defensible Space)

- PRC § 4291: 100 ft of defensible space around structures in SRA.
- Zone 0 (0–5 ft): ember-resistant (PRC 4291(a)(1), updated 2024 regs).
- Zone 1 (5–30 ft): lean / clean / green.
- Zone 2 (30–100 ft): reduced fuel.
- OCFA's FMP zones usually equal or exceed 4291 requirements for VHFHSZ.

## WUI Construction (CBC Chapter 7A / CRC § R337)

Statewide requirements in VHFHSZ/SRA — OCFA enforces through plan review:
- Ignition-resistant cladding, siding, eaves, soffits (Class A roof, ember-
  resistant vents per SFM listings).
- Windows: dual pane, tempered outer pane.
- Vents: 1/8" mesh ember-resistant (WUI-listed).
- Decks: ignition-resistant per § 7A.4 / R337.7.
- Gutters with noncombustible leaf guard recommended.

**Zone 0 structural hardening per AB 3074 (2024 regs effective).**

## Knox Box & Gates (CFC § 506)

- Required on all new commercial buildings, multifamily ≥ 3 units, and gated
  communities.
- Purchased from Knox Co. with OCFA access code.
- Mounted 5–6 ft AFG, visible from fire lane.
- Gate Knox switch + manual override with OCFA key.

## In-Building Radio Communications (ERRCS — Guideline C-06)

Required for buildings where radio signal is insufficient. Triggers:
- Buildings > 50,000 sf
- Buildings > 3 stories
- Below-grade occupied space
- Designated essential services buildings

NFPA 1221 / CFC § 510 design, commissioned by FCC-licensed GROL technician.

## OCFA Plan Review Process

1. **Applicant** submits via member jurisdiction's permit system OR directly to
   OCFA for unincorporated projects.
2. OCFA logs, assigns to reviewer (10–20 business days for 1st review typical).
3. Correction letter issued via member jurisdiction portal.
4. Resubmit response + revised plans.
5. Stamped approval → construction.
6. **Inspections** by OCFA Community Risk Reduction field staff at:
   - Underground hydraulic
   - Rough sprinkler / alarm
   - Final sprinkler / alarm (with hydrostatic / trip test)
   - FMP installation
   - Final occupancy
