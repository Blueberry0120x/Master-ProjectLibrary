# Orange County — Development & Permit Reference

## Scope

Orange County, CA is a County of **34 incorporated cities** plus **unincorporated
areas** administered directly by the County. Approximately **85%** of the county
population lives in incorporated cities; each city runs its own Building, Planning,
and (in most cases) Fire departments with local amendments to the California codes.

This reference library primarily covers the **unincorporated areas** plus a quick
index to how incorporated cities fit into the regulatory picture.

> **Note:** Separate reference folders exist for **Garden Grove**
> (`knowledge/garden-grove/`) and **Santa Ana** (`knowledge/santa-ana/`). For other
> cities, start at the city's own Community Development / Building & Safety portal.

## Authority Split (Unincorporated OC)

| Domain | Lead Agency | Scope |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Building permits, zoning, planning | **OC Development Services (OCDS)** — part of OC Community Resources / OC Public Works | Ministerial building permits, discretionary land use, Site Development Permits |
| Grading, drainage, encroachment, hydrology | **OC Public Works (OCPW) — Infrastructure & Development Services** | Grading permits, encroachment permits, hydrology approvals |
| Fire & life safety | **Orange County Fire Authority (OCFA)** | Fire Code, fuel modification, fire sprinklers, access |
| Private sewage / wells / food / pools | **OC Health Care Agency — Environmental Health Division** | OWTS (septic), wells, pool plan check |
| Regional flood control | **OC Flood Control District (OCFCD)** | Channels, regional facilities, Local Drainage Manual |
| Coastal Zone | **California Coastal Commission** + County (per certified LCP) | Coastal Development Permits |
| Wastewater (regional) | **OC Sanitation District (OC San)** | Trunk sewers, FOG, industrial discharge |

## Fire Service — Who Protects Whom?

**Orange County Fire Authority (OCFA)** is a Joint Powers Authority serving
**24 member cities + unincorporated OC** — approximately **71%** of county
population. Member cities include Aliso Viejo, Buena Park, Cypress, Dana Point,
Irvine, Laguna Hills, Laguna Niguel, Laguna Woods, Lake Forest, La Palma,
Los Alamitos, Mission Viejo, Placentia, Rancho Santa Margarita, San Clemente,
San Juan Capistrano, Santa Ana, Seal Beach, Stanton, Tustin, Villa Park,
Westminster, Yorba Linda, Garden Grove *(contracted in; verify with OCFA)*, plus
unincorporated County.

**Cities with their own fire departments (non-OCFA):**
- Anaheim Fire & Rescue
- Brea Fire Department
- Costa Mesa Fire & Rescue
- Fountain Valley Fire Department
- Fullerton Fire Department
- Huntington Beach Fire Department
- Laguna Beach Fire Department
- Newport Beach Fire Department
- Orange Fire Department

*(Garden Grove historically had its own FD; verify current membership — OCFA has
absorbed several cities in recent years.)*

**Coverage matters for:** fire code amendments, sprinkler thresholds, fuel
modification, hydrant spacing — confirm which AHJ has jurisdiction before
starting plan check.

## Code Cycle Notes

- **Current effective:** 2022 California Building Standards Code (CBC, CRC, CFC,
  CEC, CGBSC, CPC, CMC, CEnC) — effective January 1, 2023.
- **Next cycle:** 2025 CBSC — **effective January 1, 2026**. OCFA and OCDS
  typically publish local amendment updates Q4 2025. Verify before submittal.
- OC unincorporated adopts CBC/CRC/CFC/CEC/CGBSC by reference in **Title 3 of the
  OC Codified Ordinance** with local amendments for climate, wildland, and soils.
