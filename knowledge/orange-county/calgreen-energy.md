**Scope:** Unincorporated Orange County + notes on OC city reach codes.

## Code Adoption

Unincorporated OC adopts the California Building Standards Code by reference
via **Title 3 (Buildings) of the OC Codified Ordinance**, with local amendments
for climate, seismicity, wildland, and soils.

## CALGreen — Mandatory Measures (Residential Low-Rise, CRC-governed)

All new residential construction in unincorporated OC must comply with CALGreen
Part 11, Chapter 4 (Residential Mandatory Measures) + applicable Tier content
only if adopted locally.

### Selected Mandatory Measures (2022 CALGreen)

| Section | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **4.106.2** | Site management — storm water drainage/retention for sites ≥ 1 acre (SWPPP); < 1 acre use ESCP best practice |
| **4.106.3** | Site development — minimize cut/fill disturbance, protect trees |
| **4.106.4** | EV charging — **new one- and two-family dwellings: 1 EV-ready space (40A 208/240V raceway + panel capacity + outlet)** |
| **4.106.4.1** | Multifamily: EVCS / EV-Ready / EV-Capable mix per § 4.106.4.2 table (e.g., 10% EVCS + 25% EV-Ready + 10% EV-Capable for multifamily depending on use) |
| **4.201.1 / 4.203** | Water use — WaterSense plumbing fixtures: lavatory 1.2 gpm, kitchen 1.8 gpm, showerhead 1.8 gpm, toilet 1.28 gpf, urinal 0.125 gpf |
| **4.303.1** | Water-efficient landscape per **MWELO** (if landscape area ≥ 500 sf new or 2,500 sf rehab) |
| **4.303.2** | Weather- or soil-moisture-based irrigation controllers |
| **4.408** | **Construction waste diversion — minimum 65% diversion** by weight (recycle/reuse); submit WMP + final report |
| **4.410** | Operation & Maintenance Manual for owner at occupancy |
| **4.504** | Pollutant control — low-VOC adhesives, paints, flooring; moisture protection |
| **4.506** | Indoor air — bathroom exhaust per ASHRAE 62.2; whole-house ventilation |
| **4.507** | Envelope / duct sealing per Title 24 |

### 65% C&D Diversion (§ 4.408)

- Applies to all new construction and most demolition/renovation in
  unincorporated OC.
- Submit **Waste Management Plan (WMP)** at permit issuance.
- Final compliance report (weight tickets) prior to final inspection.
- Hauler receipts from certified C&D facility or self-haul to mixed-C&D MRF.

### Tier 1 / Tier 2 Voluntary

Not mandatorily adopted countywide in OC unincorporated (as of current
confirmation). Some **OC cities** have adopted Tier 1 as mandatory (verify
individual city reach codes).

## CALGreen — Nonresidential (CBC-governed)

CALGreen Chapter 5 mandatory measures for nonresidential new construction,
additions ≥ 1,000 sf, or alterations with > $200,000 permit valuation in
existing buildings:
- 20% potable water reduction (baseline IECC/CBC)
- Commissioning required for projects ≥ 10,000 sf nonresidential
- EV infrastructure per 5.106.5.3 (20% EV-capable spaces in most occupancies)
- Bicycle parking
- 65% C&D diversion
- Low-VOC materials
- Daylighting / skylights in large warehouses (§ 5.507.1)

## Title 24 Part 6 — Energy Code

### Climate Zones in Orange County

| Climate Zone | Typical Areas |
|--------------|---------------|
| **CZ 6** | **Coastal OC** — Seal Beach, Huntington Beach, Newport Beach, Laguna Beach, Dana Point, San Clemente, and near-coastal unincorporated |
| **CZ 8** | **Coastal-Inland OC** — Santa Ana, Garden Grove, Costa Mesa, Fountain Valley, Irvine, Tustin, inland Huntington Beach |
| **CZ 9** | **Inland OC** — Anaheim, Orange, Fullerton, Brea, La Habra, inland Santa Ana plain |
| **CZ 10** | **Foothill OC / Inland Empire edge** — Yorba Linda, Rancho Santa Margarita, Coto de Caza, Trabuco Canyon, eastern foothill unincorporated |
| **CZ 16** | Mountain peaks (Santiago, Silverado high elevations — rare) |

> Caltech/CEC climate zone map is definitive. Many OC sites are at the CZ 8 / 9
> boundary — check lat/long against CEC Climate Zone Tool.

### 2022 T24 Part 6 Residential Key Features

- **Heat pump preference** — HVAC and domestic hot water trade-off advantages.
- **PV mandate** (continued from 2019 code) for most new low-rise residential,
  sized to annual load (kWAC) per CF1R.
- **Battery storage incentive** — compliance credit for paired PV+battery.
- **Enhanced envelope** — QII incentive, attic/wall insulation, window U/SHGC.
- **HERS verification** — duct leakage, fan watt draw, refrigerant charge,
  whole-building air tightness (where required).

### 2022 T24 Part 6 Nonresidential

- Lighting power density tightened
- Heat pump SWH for commercial kitchens / restaurants
- Receptacle controls, daylight controls
- Commissioning for large buildings

Bay Area cities on all-electric mandates (several cities paused or rescinded
after the Berkeley/CRA 9th Circuit decision on natural gas bans). Current
status (verify city by city):

| City | Reach Code / Electrification Ordinance |
|------|----------------------------------------|
| **Costa Mesa** | Adopted Tier 1 CALGreen / electrification consideration — verify current |
| **Laguna Beach** | Climate Action Plan references, partial reach code — verify |
| **Irvine** | Climate Action/Adaptation Plan; reach code under consideration — verify |
| **Fullerton** | — |
| **Newport Beach** | — |
| **Huntington Beach** | — |
| **Anaheim** | — |
| **San Clemente** | — |

> **Most OC cities have rescinded or never adopted natural gas bans after the
> April 2023 California Restaurant Assn. v. City of Berkeley decision.** Several
> cities now allow gas service per state code baseline.
> Confirm each city's current reach code status at time of permit — the
> Statewide Codes & Standards reach code tracker

> list.

### OC Unincorporated

No confirmed countywide reach code beyond state baseline; Title 24 Part 6
mandatory measures only. Verify with OCDS at time of submittal.

## C&D Diversion (65%) — OC Unincorporated

- CALGreen § 4.408 (residential) and § 5.408 (nonresidential) mandate.
- Certified C&D facilities serving OC:
  - **OC Waste & Recycling** — Olinda Alpha (Brea), Prima Deshecha (San Juan
    Capistrano), Frank R. Bowerman (Irvine) landfills with C&D capture.
  - **CR&R** — Stanton Sorting Facility, Perris C&D MRF.
  - **Republic Services**, **Rainbow Environmental Services**, **Waste Management**
    all operate C&D MRFs serving OC.
- Franchise hauler list: each city has exclusive hauler; unincorporated OC
  has franchise agreements by area — confirm via OC Waste & Recycling.

## MWELO — Landscape

- State Model Water Efficient Landscape Ordinance (DWR, § 490 et seq.) adopted
  by reference.
- Triggers: new construction ≥ 500 sf landscape; rehab ≥ 2,500 sf; permits
  that include landscape.
- MAWA (Maximum Applied Water Allowance) calculated; landscape/irrigation
  designer signs certification; HERS-like verification at final.
