## 2. Residential Development Standards (Single-Family)

### Setbacks — SF Zone (typical)

| Setback | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Front yard | 15–20 ft (varies by street classification) |
| Side yard (interior) | 5 ft minimum |
| Side yard (street / corner) | 10 ft |
| Rear yard | 20 ft |

**Verify exact setbacks for your zone in SAMC Ch 41 — standards vary by zone and
Specific Plan overlay.**

### Building Height

| Zone | Maximum Height |
|------|---------------|
| SF / SF-A | 35 ft; 2 stories |
| MF-1 | 35 ft |
| MF-2 | 45 ft |
| MF-3 | 65 ft |

### Lot Coverage

| Zone | Max Lot Coverage |
|------|-----------------|
| SF | 50% |
| SF-A | 55% |
| TF | 55% |
| MF zones | Varies — check SAMC |

### Floor Area Ratio (FAR)

Santa Ana applies FAR to some commercial zones and Specific Plan areas.
For base SF zone residential, development intensity is primarily controlled by
lot coverage, setbacks, and height rather than FAR.

## 3. Parking Requirements

### Residential Parking

| Use | Required Spaces |
|-----|----------------|
| SFD (2–4 bedrooms) | 2 spaces (1 covered) |
| SFD (5+ bedrooms) | 3 spaces |
| ADU (studio/1BR ≤ 500 sf) | 0 (state law) |
| ADU (other) | 1 space |
| TF / Duplex | 2 spaces per unit |
| MF-1 / MF-2 | 1.5 spaces per unit + 0.25 guest |
| MF-3 | 1 space per unit + 0.1 guest |

**State ADU law overrides local parking minimums — see `adu-and-sb9.md`.**

### Garage Dimensions

- Minimum clear interior: 20 ft × 20 ft (2-car); 10 ft × 20 ft (1-car).

## 4. Overlay Districts & Specific Plans

Santa Ana has numerous overlay districts and specific plan areas that supersede
or supplement base zone standards:

### Specific Plans

| Specific Plan | Area | Key Features |
|---------------|------|-------------|
| Downtown Santa Ana Specific Plan (DSASP) | Historic downtown core | Mixed-use, historic preservation, design review |
| South Main Corridor Specific Plan | South Main St | Commercial/residential mixed-use transition |
| Harbor Mixed-Use Transit Corridor (HMUTC) | Harbor Blvd | Transit-oriented, density bonuses |
| Pacific Electric / Bristol Transit Corridor | Bristol St | TOD overlay, reduced parking |
| West Santa Ana Branch (WSAB) TOD | Future rail alignment | In planning |

### Overlay Zones

- **Flood Plain Overlay (FP):** Areas in FEMA SFHA — additional elevation, freeboard, and
  floodplain development permit required.
- **Historic District Overlay:** Properties in the Heninger Park, French Park, or other
  historic districts require design review; demolition is discretionary.
- **Scenic Corridor Overlay:** Enhanced landscape and screening standards along key corridors.

## 5. Density Bonus (State Law — Gov Code 65915)

Santa Ana must grant density bonuses per state law. Key tiers:
- 8% VLI or 15% LI → 22.5% bonus
- 24% VLI → 50% bonus (maximum for standard projects)
- 100% affordable → 80% bonus (AB 2334, 2022)

Santa Ana has an active affordable housing program — contact Planning at (714) 647-5804
for current incentive/concession menu.

## 7. Environmental Review (CEQA)

- Ministerial ADU permits: categorically exempt from CEQA (Class 3).
- Discretionary approvals (CUP, variance, Specific Plan amendment): CEQA Initial Study required.
- Historic districts and floodplain areas may require additional analysis.
