# Santa Ana — Stormwater & Grading

Source: OC NPDES DAMP + Santa Ana Municipal Code Title II Ch 18 (Grading) +
SARWQCB MS4 permit. Santa Ana Public Works Engineering: (714) 647-3380.

---

## 1. Regulatory Framework

| Authority | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Santa Ana Regional Water Quality Control Board (SARWQCB) | MS4 permit, NPDES enforcement |
| OC Public Works — Watershed Programs | DAMP administration, technical guidance |
| City of Santa Ana Public Works — Engineering | Local grading, drainage, encroachment, floodplain |
| City of Santa Ana Building Safety | Stormwater review at plan check |

**Primary documents:**
- OC NPDES Drainage Area Management Plan (DAMP) — most recent edition
- OC Low Impact Development (LID) Standards Manual

## 2. Stormwater Requirements — New Construction

### Project Tier Determination

| Condition | Tier |
|-----------|------|
| New construction ≥ 10,000 sf impervious | Priority Project — LID/WQMP required |
| New construction < 10,000 sf impervious | Standard Project — basic BMPs |
| Single-family residential ≤ 1 acre | Standard Project (typically) |

ADUs and SFD additions are typically Standard Projects. Verify with Building Safety.

### Standard Project Requirements

1. **Site Design BMPs** — direct runoff to pervious areas, disconnect impervious surfaces.
2. **Source Control BMPs** — trash enclosure design, material storage away from drains.
3. **Construction BMPs** — erosion/sediment control, concrete washout area.

Submit a **BMP Site Plan** with permit application showing BMP locations.

### Priority Project (LID/WQMP)

If threshold met:
- **Water Quality Management Plan (WQMP)** by licensed civil engineer.
- LID treatment BMPs: bioretention, infiltration, vegetated swales.
- Hydromodification analysis if increased peak runoff.
- O&M Agreement recorded against property for perpetual BMP maintenance.

## 3. Grading Permits

| Condition | Permit Required |
|-----------|----------------|
| Cut or fill > 50 cubic yards | Grading permit from Engineering |
| Slopes > 3:1 created | Grading permit |
| Grading in floodplain | Grading + floodplain development permit |
| Grading within 50 ft of watercourse | Additional review |

Grading plan prepared by licensed Civil Engineer — show existing/proposed contours,
cut/fill quantities, drainage, erosion control plan.

---

## 4. Flood Zone Compliance

Santa Ana has portions of the city within FEMA SFHA Zone AE (Santa Ana River
and its tributaries).

- Finished floor elevation: at or above BFE + 1 ft freeboard.
- No fill in floodway without compensatory storage.
- Floodplain development permit from Engineering required.
- Check FEMA MSC: https://msc.fema.gov/portal/home

---

## 5. Construction General Permit (≥ 1 acre)

Disturbances ≥ 1 acre require SWRCB CGP coverage:
- File NOI in SMARTS before grading.
- Prepare and implement SWPPP.
- File NOT after final stabilization.

Most SFD/ADU projects are under 1 acre. Subdivisions and larger infill projects may trigger.
