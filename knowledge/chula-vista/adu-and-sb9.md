# Chula Vista — ADU / JADU & SB 9

Chula Vista's Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) and Senate Bill 9 (SB 9) implementation
follow the state-mandated framework with local procedural rules. **State law (Gov Code
§65852.2 and §66411.7) preempts most local restrictions.**

## ADU Ordinance

**Primary citation:** **CVMC Chapter 19.58** (Accessory uses) — the ADU standards are
typically in **CVMC 19.58.022** or an updated ordinance. Chula Vista amended its ADU
ordinance to conform to AB 976, AB 1033, AB 2221, SB 897 (2023–2024 legislation) and
must conform to current state law. **Verify current adopted version** on Municode.

### ADU Types (State-Preempted)

| Type | Max Size | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Detached ADU** | Up to 1,200 sf (local), **850 sf 1BR / 1,000 sf 2BR statutory minimum** | Any residential lot |
| **Attached ADU** | 50% of primary dwelling up to 1,200 sf | Attached to primary SFD |
| **Conversion ADU** | Existing area of primary dwelling / accessory structure | Within existing footprint |
| **JADU** | ≤ 500 sf | Within walls of existing/proposed SFD; owner-occupancy required |

### State-Mandated "By-Right" ADU (§65852.2(e))

Cities MUST permit:
- One ADU + one JADU on any single-family lot
- Up to two detached ADUs on multifamily lots (new construction) OR
  Conversion of 25% of existing multifamily units to ADUs (min 1)
- An 800-sf ADU up to 16 ft (18 ft single-story on some lots per AB 976) with 4-ft setbacks
  is by-right regardless of local standards
- AB 1033 — local option to allow ADU **condo sale** separately; Chula Vista adoption status — verify

### Development Standards (local, subordinate to state floor)

| Standard | Value |
|---|---|
| Setbacks (side / rear) | **4 ft** (state minimum; no setback for conversions) |
| Front setback | Same as underlying zone; state allows reduction if infeasible |
| Height — detached ADU | **16 ft** (base), **18 ft** (within ½ mi major transit or multifamily), **25 ft** (attached to multi-story SFD, 2 stories) |
| Lot coverage | Cannot be used to preclude by-right 800-sf ADU |
| FAR | Cannot be used to preclude by-right 800-sf ADU |
| Parking | **1 space per ADU OR per bedroom** — **WAIVED** if ≤½ mi transit, historic district, within permit-parking area, car-share ½ mi, or conversion ADU |
| Owner-occupancy (ADU) | **Prohibited** per state law (through 1/1/2025 for ADUs permitted before then); JADU owner-occupancy still required |
| Passageway | Cannot be required |
| Replacement parking | **Not required** when garage converted to ADU |
| Fire sprinklers | Required only if primary dwelling has them (CRC R313.2) |

### Review Timeline

- **60 days** from complete application — ministerial (no CEQA, no hearing)
- Pre-approved ADU plans — Chula Vista offers pre-approved / ADU Accelerator plans (verify current catalog)
- Solar: PV required if new ADU, per Title 24 Part 6 (exemption for conversion ADUs)

### Fees

- **Impact Fees WAIVED** for ADUs **< 750 sf** (Gov Code §65852.2(f)(3))
- ADUs ≥ 750 sf — impact fees proportional to primary dwelling sf (pro-rata)
- Building permit + plan check fees still apply (reduced schedule for ADUs typical)
- School fees — per SB 13 (ADUs <500 sf exempt; larger at discretion of district)

### Utilities

- ADU may be served by primary's water/sewer connection (no new meter required for
  conversion ADUs)
- Separate meter may be required for new construction ADU (varies Otay / Sweetwater)
- Sewer capacity fee waived for ADU <750 sf

## SB 9 — Urban Lot Split & 2-Unit Housing Development

**Gov Code §66411.7 (urban lot split) + §65852.21 (2-unit development)** — effective
Jan 1, 2022. Chula Vista adopted conforming implementation in CVMC (Chapter 18 /
19.58 — **verify exact section**).

### Eligibility

Parcel must:
- Be zoned **single-family residential** (R-1, R-E, R-1-5)
- Be in an **"urbanized area"** per 2010 Census (most of Chula Vista qualifies)
- NOT be in: prime farmland, wetlands, high fire hazard (unless state-standards compliant),
  earthquake fault zone, flood hazard, conservation easement, habitat for protected species,
  historic district / landmark
- NOT have been rent-controlled / Ellis-withdrawn in prior 15 years
- NOT have been an owner-occupant displacement in prior 3 years (tenant protections)

### SB 9 Urban Lot Split (§66411.7)

| Standard | Value |
|---|---|
| Original lot size | ≥ **2,400 sf** |
| Resulting lots | 40/60 split min (each ≥ 1,200 sf) |
| Number of units per lot | Up to **2** (plus possible ADU — see below) |
| Review | **Ministerial** — no CEQA, no hearing |
| Owner affidavit | Must intend to occupy one unit for 3 years |
| Demolition | Cannot demolish rent-controlled / low-income / recent tenant-occupied housing |
| Parcel maps | Processed ministerially (not a Subdivision Map Act tentative) |

### SB 9 Two-Unit Development (§65852.21)

- Allows **2 units** on an SFD lot (duplex, two detached, or addition) **by right**
- Can be combined with urban lot split → up to **4 units total across 2 lots**
- Max unit size limitation: cannot require <800 sf per unit
- Setbacks: 4 ft side/rear (same as state ADU)
- Parking: max 1 space/unit; waived within ½ mi transit or car-share

### Not Allowed Under SB 9

- Short-term rentals (units must be ≥ 30-day rental)
- Additional ADUs beyond 2 total units on a split lot (interpretation — a non-splitting
  SB 9 lot may still have ADU + JADU)

## Common Compliance Issues (ADU)

- Parking attempted in front setback or over sidewalk (not allowed)
- ADU in VHFHSZ — must meet **Ch 7A / R327** WUI material requirements
- Septic systems — Otay WD / Sweetwater sewer, or septic per DEH (rare)
- Failure to provide separate address / mail delivery
- Encroachments in easements (drainage, sewer)
