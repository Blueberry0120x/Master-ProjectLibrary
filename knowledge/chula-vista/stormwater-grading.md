# Chula Vista — Stormwater & Grading

Chula Vista is a **Copermittee** under the **San Diego Regional MS4 Permit** (Order
R9-2013-0001 as amended — Regional Water Quality Control Board, Region 9). Stormwater
compliance is enforced by **Engineering / Land Development** within DSD.

## Governing Documents

| Document | Scope |
|---|---|
| **CVMC Chapter 14.20** (Grading Ordinance) | Grading permits, erosion control |
| **CVMC Chapter 14.16** (Drainage) | Drainage facilities, floodplain |
| **CVMC Chapter 14.04** / **Title 14** | Stormwater Management |
| **Chula Vista Storm Water Management Manual (SWMM)** | BMP design, PDP requirements |
| **Chula Vista Subdivision Manual** | Public improvements / drainage |
| **BMP Design Manual** (region-wide reference) | San Diego Region MS4 standards |
| **Jurisdictional Runoff Management Program (JRMP)** | Copermittee program plan |

Stormwater is regulated under **NPDES MS4 Phase I** for San Diego Region (Order R9).

## Watershed Management Areas (WMA)

Chula Vista lies within two WMAs:

| WMA | Coverage |
|---|---|
| **San Diego Bay WMA** | Western / central Chula Vista, Sweetwater River, Bayfront — drains to San Diego Bay |
| **Otay WMA** | Eastern / southern Chula Vista, Otay River, Otay Reservoir — drains to Otay River / south San Diego Bay |

Each WMA has a **Water Quality Improvement Plan (WQIP)** with priority conditions,
numeric goals, and monitoring.

## Grading Ordinance (CVMC Title 14 / Ch 14.20)

### Permit Thresholds

| Trigger | Required |
|---|---|
| Cut or fill ≥ 50 cubic yards | Grading permit |
| Cut or fill ≥ 200 cy OR slope > 5 ft | Engineered grading (soils report + stamped plans) |
| Fill slope ≥ 3 ft vertical | Engineered |
| Cut slope ≥ 5 ft | Engineered |
| Any work in floodplain | Permit + FEMA compliance |
| Any work in MSCP Preserve | Biology approval + permit |

### Exemptions

- <50 cy on single parcel
- Agricultural operations outside floodplain / resource areas
- Excavation for footings/basements within building footprint

### Construction Requirements

- Erosion & sediment control plan (ESCP / WPCP)
- Active construction BMPs per CASQA Construction Handbook
- Dust control (SDAPCD Rule 55)
- Off-site haul route approval
- Seasonal provisions Oct 1 – Apr 30 (rainy season enhanced BMPs)

## Priority Development Project (PDP) Thresholds

Per the regional MS4 permit and Chula Vista BMP Design Manual, a project is a **PDP**
if it creates or replaces ≥ specified impervious area:

| PDP Category | Threshold |
|---|---|
| New development | **≥ 10,000 sf** of impervious surface |
| Redevelopment | ≥ 10,000 sf and ≥ 50% alteration/replacement (else treat only new area) |
| Restaurant | ≥ 5,000 sf impervious |
| Auto repair / gas station | any size (specific land use) |
| Retail gasoline | any size |
| Parking lot | ≥ 5,000 sf / ≥ 15 spaces |
| Street / road | ≥ 5,000 sf contiguous impervious |
| Detached SFD (single) | Typically NOT PDP unless on hillside/slope ≥25% |
| Hillside development | ≥ 5,000 sf on slopes ≥ 25% |

**Non-PDP** projects still require **Standard Project** BMPs: source control + site
design + construction BMPs.

## PDP Requirements

1. **Storm Water Quality Management Plan (SWQMP)** — preliminary at entitlement, final at permit
2. **Pollutant Control BMPs** — capture / treat **Design Capture Volume (DCV)**:
   DCV = 0.85-inch 24-hr storm × impervious area × runoff coefficient
3. **Hydromodification Management (HMP)** — if project drains to a non-exempt waterbody,
   post-project runoff rates/durations must mimic pre-development (0.1Q2 – Q10 range)
4. **Preferred BMP hierarchy:**
   - Infiltration (first priority)
   - Biofiltration / harvest-and-use (if infiltration infeasible)
   - Flow-through / media filters (last resort)
5. **Verification** — O&M plan, covenant, annual self-certification

## Standard BMPs (all projects)

### Site Design

- Minimize impervious
- Disperse runoff to landscape
- Preserve native vegetation
- Use permeable surfaces where feasible

### Source Control

- Trash enclosures covered / bermed
- Storm drain stenciling
- Vehicle / equipment wash areas → sanitary sewer
- Landscape: efficient irrigation, native/low-maintenance

### Treatment Control

- Bioretention / rain gardens
- Vegetated swales
- Infiltration trenches / chambers (where geotech permits)
- Permeable pavement
- Proprietary media filters (tertiary)

## Hydromodification Management (HMP)

Applies to PDP draining to natural streams. Exempt if:
- Drains to exempt waterbody (tidal, concrete channel to ocean)
- Creates <1 acre impervious
- Meets performance standard via LID / flow-duration control

## Floodplain Management (CVMC 14.18 / FEMA)

- FEMA FIRM enforcement — SFHA zones AE, A, X
- 1 ft freeboard above BFE for habitable finished floor
- NFIP compliance; substantial improvement rule (50% value trigger)
- Otay River, Sweetwater River, Telegraph Canyon Creek are key watercourses

## MSCP / Biology

Chula Vista is a participating jurisdiction in the **Multiple Species Conservation
Program (MSCP)** with an approved **Subarea Plan**. Development in or adjacent to the
Preserve requires biology survey + resource management plan.

## Construction SWPPP

- **Statewide Construction General Permit** (SWRCB Order 2022-0057-DWQ) for sites ≥ 1 acre
- QSD-prepared SWPPP, QSP during construction
- Risk Level 1/2/3 determination
- SMARTS filing; NOI + NOT
