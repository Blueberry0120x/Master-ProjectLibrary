# Chula Vista — CALGreen, C&D, Title 24 Energy & Climate Action Plan

Chula Vista adopts the **2022 California Building Standards Code** via **CVMC Title 15**,
including **CALGreen (Part 11)** and **Title 24 Part 6 Energy**. The **2025 code cycle**
becomes effective **January 1, 2026**. Chula Vista is a **climate leader** — adopting
one of San Diego County's first building-electrification reach codes and operating under
a comprehensive **Climate Action Plan (CAP)**.

## CALGreen (Title 24 Part 11)

### Mandatory Residential Measures

| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| Water efficiency: indoor fixtures | 20% below CPC baseline |
| Kitchen faucets | ≤ 1.8 gpm |
| Showerheads | ≤ 1.8 gpm @ 80 psi (more restrictive than state with CEC appliance rule) |
| Toilets | ≤ 1.28 gpf |
| Irrigation controllers | WaterSense smart, ET-based |
| EV charging | 1 EV-capable raceway (SFD), Level 2 for new multifamily per tables |
| Moisture content of lumber | ≤ 19% |
| Job site recycling | 65% of C&D debris diverted |
| Air filtration | MERV 13 at HVAC |
| Weather-resistive barrier | Per §4.406 |
| Bathroom exhaust | ENERGY STAR, humidity-controlled |

### Tier 1 / Tier 2 Voluntary Measures

Chula Vista does not mandate CALGreen Tiers generally, but may require them via
Design Review for commercial or as part of CAP measures (e.g., LEED equivalency).

## Construction & Demolition (C&D) Debris Recycling

**CVMC Chapter 8.25** — Construction and Demolition Debris Recycling Ordinance. Chula
Vista meets or exceeds the state CALGreen baseline.

| Project Type | Diversion Required |
|---|---|
| New residential | **65%** (CALGreen §4.408) — Chula Vista aligned |
| Commercial / TI | **65%** (CALGreen §5.408) |
| Demolition-only (>500 sf) | 65% diversion required |

### Process

1. Submit **C&D Debris Management Plan (WMP)** with building permit application
2. Deposit (refundable) — per fee schedule, held until diversion verified
3. Use a **franchised hauler** or approved facility
4. Submit **receipts / weight tickets** from certified facility within specified window
5. Deposit refunded upon achieving ≥ 65% diversion; forfeited if not

### Designated Haulers

- **Republic Services / Allied Waste** — Chula Vista franchise (western)
- **EDCO** — Chula Vista franchise (specific service areas)

Verify current franchise holder at chulavistaca.gov/departments/public-works.

### Materials Tracked

Concrete, asphalt, wood, metal, drywall, cardboard, landscape debris, mixed C&D.

## Title 24 Part 6 — Energy

### Climate Zones

California Energy Commission climate zones per CBECC-based map:

| Area | Climate Zone |
|---|---|
| **Coastal / western Chula Vista** (west of I-805, Bayfront) | **CZ 7** |
| **Mesa / central** (along I-805 corridor) | CZ 7 |
| **Eastern / inland** (Otay Ranch, EastLake, east of SR-125) | **CZ 10** |

Climate zone affects prescriptive insulation, glazing U-factor/SHGC, cooling sizing.
**Most of Chula Vista building stock is CZ 7**; eastern PCs often CZ 10.

### 2022 Energy Code Highlights (effective 2023–2025)

- **Heat pump baseline** for space and water heating in residential
- **Solar PV required** on all new low-rise residential (SFD, duplex, townhouse)
- **Battery-ready or battery-included** for new multifamily (dwellings) and nonresidential
- **EV charging** requirements in CALGreen + Energy
- **Ventilation** ASHRAE 62.2 compliance
- **Envelope** prescriptive U-values by CZ

### 2025 Energy Code (effective Jan 1, 2026)

- Expanded heat pump requirements (multifamily, nonresidential)
- Stricter envelope for CZ 10+
- Enhanced solar + storage for multifamily and commercial
- Verify updated prescriptive tables via CBECC 2025

## Chula Vista Climate Action Plan (CAP)

The **Chula Vista CAP** (adopted 2017, updated 2021 "2017 CAP Update"; and further
revisions in progress) sets a trajectory to:
- **55% GHG reduction by 2030** (below 2005 baseline)
- **Carbon neutrality by 2045** (aligned with state SB 100 / AB 1279)

### CAP Implementation Measures (building-related)

| Measure | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Energy Efficiency | Reach code, retrofit programs |
| 100% Renewable Electricity | Via SDCP (San Diego Community Power) — Chula Vista is a member |
| Zero-Carbon New Buildings | Reach code (see below) |
| Water Conservation | MWELO, recycled water expansion |
| Zero Waste | 75% diversion goal |
| Clean Mobility | EV infrastructure, transit, active transport |

## Chula Vista Building Electrification Reach Code

Chula Vista adopted a **Building Electrification Reach Code** in **2022/2023** (effective
with the 2022 code cycle). The reach code established that all new low-rise residential
construction must either:

- Be **all-electric**, OR
- Install **additional solar PV + battery storage** sufficient to offset mixed-fuel usage
  (prescriptive compliance path with cost-effective credit)

**Important:** Following the April 2023 **9th Circuit decision in** *California Restaurant
Assn. v. Berkeley*, many CA cities (including Chula Vista) have paused or revised
outright gas-ban reach codes because EPCA preempts ordinances that "ban natural gas
appliances." Chula Vista's reach code has been restructured to rely on **energy
performance (Title 24 Part 6 plus amendments)** rather than a direct appliance ban —
**verify currently adopted version** (CVMC Title 15 amendments, CEC-filed package).

### Scope (typical)

- **New residential** — SFD, ADU (new construction), duplex, townhouse, low-rise multi
- **New nonresidential** — specified occupancies
- **Not** generally retroactive to existing building replacements

### Compliance Paths

1. **All-electric** (heat pump space + water heating, induction cooking / resistance, no gas service) — simplest
2. **Mixed-fuel with enhanced PV/battery + envelope/efficiency** — meet Total EDR / Source EDR targets per the city's amendment

Reach code filed with CEC per Pub Res Code §25402.1(h)(2). Expiration aligned with
2022 cycle; **re-adoption for 2025 cycle required by Jan 1, 2026** — confirm status.

## Sustainable Landscape / MWELO

CCR Title 23 §490 et seq. (MWELO) enforced by City Landscape:
- Applies to landscape areas ≥ 500 sf (new) or ≥ 2,500 sf (rehab)
- ETwu ≤ MAWA (maximum applied water allowance)
- Dedicated meter for >5,000 sf non-res landscape
- Hydrozones, smart controllers, drip irrigation preferred
- Mulch ≥ 3 in depth in all non-turf areas
- Turf restrictions (cannot exceed 25% of landscape area in non-residential)

## Recycled Water

  in served areas

  common areas
