## 2. Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) — HIGH PRIORITY IN ESCONDIDO

Unlike urban-core cities, **Escondido has significant WUI exposure.** Many
parcels in north, east, and hillside areas are in a **Very High Fire Hazard
Severity Zone (VHFHSZ)** or State Responsibility Area (SRA).

**Always check the CAL FIRE FHSZ map before designing any Escondido project.**

CAL FIRE FHSZ viewer: https://www.fire.ca.gov/what-we-do/fire-prevention/fire-hazard-severity-zones

### If Parcel is in VHFHSZ

**Chapter 7A — Ignition-Resistant Construction Materials (CBC):**

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Exterior walls | Ignition-resistant material (Class 1 or noncombustible) per CBC 707A |
| Roof assembly | Class A fire-rated roof covering (CBC 705A) |
| Eaves and soffits | Enclosed or ignition-resistant material (CBC 706A) |
| Vents | Listed ember-resistant vent covers (CBC 708A) |
| Windows/skylights | Multi-pane, dual-glazed with tempered outer pane (CBC 710A) |
| Doors (exterior) | Solid wood/steel or tested assembly (CBC 711A) |
| Decks/porches | Ignition-resistant decking or heavy timber (CBC 709A) |
| Attached garages | Ignition-resistant wall at garage-dwelling interface |

**Defensible Space (PRC §4291):**

| Zone | Distance | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Zone 1 | 0–30 ft | Lean, clean, and green; no combustible mulch within 3 ft |
| Zone 2 | 30–100 ft | Reduced fuel; vertical/horizontal spacing |

EFD may inspect defensible space before issuing final certificate of occupancy.

### Chapter 7A Cost Impact

Chapter 7A requirements add approximately 3–8% to construction cost for a typical
SFD in a WUI zone. Flag this early in project design — it affects roofing, exterior
cladding, window selection, and vent specification.

## 3. Fire Sprinkler Requirements

### Single-Family Dwellings

EFD requires **NFPA 13D fire sprinklers for all new SFD construction** per local
amendment to CRC R313.

- **ADU (attached):** Typically triggers sprinklers if primary is sprinklered.
- **ADU (detached):** Confirm with EFD at (760) 839-4600.
- **WUI parcels:** Sprinkler system design must account for hillside water pressure
  and flow — work with Vallecitos Water District early.

### Multifamily

- NFPA 13R for R-2 up to 4 stories.
- Deferred submittal to EFD after building permit.

## 4. Fire Alarm & Detection

| Occupancy | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| SFD (new) | Smoke alarms per CRC R314 — hardwired, battery backup, interconnected |
| ADU | Smoke alarms per CRC R314; CO detector if gas or garage |
| R-2 multifamily | NFPA 72 fire alarm |

## 5. Fire Access & Addressing

- Address numbers: 4 in minimum, visible from street, contrasting color.
- ADU requires separate address or unit number posted.
- Fire apparatus access: 20 ft clear width, 13 ft 6 in vertical clearance.
- Dead ends > 150 ft: approved turnaround.
- Hillside private driveways: EFD may require additional width or turnouts
  for fire apparatus — confirm early for any hillside project.
- Gates: Knox key switch or Knox Box.

### Fire Hydrant

- New SFD: hydrant within 600 ft along road.
- WUI hillside: may require shorter spacing or on-site water storage — confirm with EFD.
- Flow requirement: 1,000 gpm minimum.

---

## 6. EFD Plan Review Process

1. Building Division routes plans to EFD concurrent with plan check.
2. EFD reviews access, hydrants, WUI compliance, sprinkler/alarm notes.
3. WUI/Chapter 7A projects: additional materials specifications review.
4. Corrections as part of consolidated report.
5. Sprinkler/alarm: deferred submittal to EFD.
6. Defensible space inspection: may be required before final C/O.
7. Final fire inspection.
