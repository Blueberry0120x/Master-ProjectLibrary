# ADU & SB 9 — County of San Diego (Unincorporated)

**Authority:** Planning & Development Services (PDS). County Zoning Ordinance §6156 (ADU), General Plan housing element.
**State Laws applied:** Gov Code §§65852.2 & 65852.22 (ADU/JADU), §§65852.21 & 66411.7 (SB 9).

> **Differs from City of San Diego:** City has expansive companion-unit bonus and pre-approved plan program. The County has adopted a straightforward §6156 ADU ordinance that generally tracks state minimums; fewer bonus/incentive layers. **SB 9 applicability is sharply limited** in the unincorporated County because most areas are not within an "urbanized area" or "urban cluster" as required by Gov Code §65852.21(a)(2).

---

## 1. Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) — County Zoning Ord §6156

### Where Allowed
- In **any residential zone** where a single-family or multi-family dwelling is allowed or exists.
- One ADU per existing or proposed SFD lot (by state law floor).
- One JADU (junior ADU) in addition to one ADU, when attached within the primary SFD (state floor).
- On multifamily lots: up to 25% of existing units as conversion ADUs + up to 2 detached ADUs (state law).

### Unit Types Recognized
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Detached ADU** | Standalone structure |
| **Attached ADU** | Addition sharing one wall w/ primary dwelling |
| **Conversion ADU** | Converted space inside existing dwelling, garage, or accessory |
| **JADU** | ≤ 500 SF, within walls of primary SFD, efficiency kitchen, may share bath w/ primary, owner-occupancy required |

### Size Limits (state floor / County application)
- **Detached:** min 150 SF (state mandate), max **1,200 SF** (state cap), **County allows up to 1,200 SF**.
- **Attached:** max 50% of primary dwelling SF **or** 1,200 SF, whichever is less; but state law forces min **850 SF (1-BR)** / **1,000 SF (2-BR+)**.
- **Conversion:** no size cap if entirely within existing structure envelope + permitted 150 SF expansion for ingress/egress.

### Setbacks
- **4 ft side and rear** (state floor — Gov Code §65852.2(a)(1)(D)(vii)).
- **Front setback: primary zone standard** — but County must allow an 800 SF / 16-ft high / 4-ft setback ADU on ANY lot (state "ministerial 800 SF default").
- **No setback required** for conversion of existing structure to ADU (including garage conversion).

### Height
- **16 ft** default state floor.
- **18 ft** if within ½ mi of major transit stop or for attached ADU on multifamily parcel.
- **25 ft** for attached ADU matching primary height (on SFD lot).
- County max typically matches zone's height designator otherwise.

### Parking
- **No parking required** for ADUs when any of these apply (Gov Code §65852.2(d)):
  - Within ½ mi walking of public transit.
  - Within architecturally/historically significant district.
  - Conversion of existing garage/structure.
  - Within permit parking area with no overnight residential permit available.
  - Within 1 block of car-share.
- Otherwise: **1 space per unit or per bedroom**, may be tandem, may be uncovered in setback. In rural County areas, parking often feasible on-site regardless.

### Utilities
- ADUs may share utility connections w/ primary; **separate meter optional** (not required by state law for existing-home attached/conversion ADUs; required typical for detached new connection).
- **No impact fees** under 750 SF (state law).
- Over 750 SF: impact fees proportional to primary dwelling SF ratio.
- **School fees:** only on new ADUs > 500 SF (per state school-fee law adjustments).

### Fire Sprinklers
- ADU requires **NFPA 13D** sprinklers ONLY if primary dwelling has (or is required to have) sprinklers.
- Most pre-2011 primary homes were NOT required to sprinkler — new ADU attached/detached on those lots generally does NOT need sprinklers. (Gov Code §65852.2(a)(1)(D)(ix).)
- **WUI note:** even without sprinklers, Chapter 7A applies to new ADUs in FHSZ — roof, windows, siding, vents, eaves. See fire-and-wildfire.md.

### Owner-Occupancy
- **No owner-occupancy requirement** for ADUs permitted between Jan 1, 2020 and Jan 1, 2025 (per AB 881).
- AB 1033 (2024) reimposed owner-occupancy option per local election; County has **not** required owner-occupancy as of writing — verify current ordinance.
- **JADUs: owner-occupancy IS required** (state law).

### Short-Term Rental
- ADUs **cannot** be used for rentals < 30 days per state law.
- JADUs same.

### Rural / Well & Septic Considerations (big one in County)
- **Well capacity** must be demonstrated adequate (often a well-yield test).
- **OWTS capacity:** existing septic sized per CBC Table 422.1 / DEH LAMP. Adding ADU bedrooms typically triggers:
  - Tank expansion (1,000 → 1,200 gal, or 1,200 → 1,500 gal, depending on total BR count).
  - Leach field extension or verified reserve.
  - DEHQ approval required BEFORE building permit issuance.

### Ministerial Review
- **60-day state clock** (Gov Code §65852.2(b)(2)). County must approve/deny complete application within 60 days.
- No discretionary review; no CEQA (statutorily exempt).
- Admin appeal available if denied.

## 2. Pre-Approved / Standard Plans Program

- County PDS has **standard ADU plans** available per AB 434 mandate.
- Check PDS webpage for current list — small, mid, and large options + floor plans.
- **Fee reductions** may apply when using standard plans.

## 3. Junior ADU (JADU) — §65852.22

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Max size | 500 SF |
| Location | Within walls of **existing or proposed** primary SFD |
| Bath | Own or shared w/ primary |
| Kitchen | Efficiency kitchen (cooktop, sink, countertop, storage) |
| Entry | Separate exterior entry OR interior access from primary |
| Owner-occupancy | **Required** (primary OR JADU) |
| Deed restriction | Owner-occupancy + no-severance recorded |

## 4. SB 9 — Urban Lot Split & 2-Unit Ministerial

**Applies only in urbanized areas / urban clusters** per US Census + Gov Code §65852.21(a)(2). Much of the unincorporated County does **NOT qualify**.

### Applicability Check
Before processing any SB 9 application:
1. Confirm parcel is in an **"urbanized area" or "urban cluster"** per US Census.
2. Confirm zoning allows single-family (SB 9 applies to parcels zoned single-family residential only).
3. Exclusions:
   - Prime farmland / farmland of statewide importance
   - Wetlands
   - Very High FHSZ (unless meets state mitigation exception per Gov Code §4291 compliance etc. — complicated; often disqualifies County rural lots)
   - Hazardous waste site
   - Delineated earthquake fault zone
   - Special Flood Hazard Area (100-yr floodplain)
   - Regulatory floodway
   - Conservation easement / habitat for protected species
   - Historic site
4. **Not allowed to demolish/alter**:
   - Housing rented in past 3 years
   - Deed-restricted affordable housing
   - Housing subject to rent control

### Two Parts to SB 9

| Part | What it does | Result |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **§65852.21 — Two-Unit Dev** | Allows up to 2 units ministerially on any qualifying SFR parcel | Duplex / SFD + ADU in separate structure |
| **§66411.7 — Urban Lot Split** | Ministerial split into 2 lots (each min 1,200 SF, typ 40/60 or 50/50) | Two smaller parcels |

Combined: up to **4 units on original parcel** (2 per lot × 2 lots). State law floor; County typically permits 2 ADUs on each resulting lot but local ordinance may pare back to 2 units per lot total.

### Key Standards
- **Owner-occupancy for Lot Split:** applicant must sign sworn statement of intent to occupy one of the units for **3 years** (§66411.7(g)(1)).
- **Setbacks:** 4 ft side/rear (same as ADU); existing walls preserved.
- **Objective standards only** — County cannot apply subjective design review.
- **Parking:** ≤ 1 space/unit, **zero** if within ½ mi transit or car-share block.
- **No short-term rentals** (<30 days) per §66411.7(g)(2).
- **Minimum lot size per split piece:** 1,200 SF (or 40% of original, whichever larger).

### Why SB 9 Mostly Doesn't Apply in Unincorporated County
- Most unincorporated land is **not** in an urbanized area/cluster per Census.
- Many rural parcels are in **Very High FHSZ**.
- Many have **Prime Farmland** designation.
- RPO steep slopes, floodplain, habitat further exclude.
- Practically, SB 9 applies only in a handful of Village-category areas contiguous with incorporated cities (e.g., parts of Spring Valley, Casa de Oro, Lakeside, Bostonia).

## 5. Fees — ADUs & SB 9

| Item | ADU <750 SF | ADU ≥750 SF | SB 9 |
|------|-------------|-------------|------|
| Traffic Impact Fee (TIF) | Waived | Proportional | Per unit |
| PLDO (parks) | Waived | Proportional | Per unit |
| RTCIP (SANDAG) | Waived <750 SF | Proportional or floor | Per unit |
| School fees | Waived <500 SF | Full per SF ≥500 | Per unit |
| Fire (SDCF/FPD) | Plan check | Plan check | Plan check |
| DEH (OWTS/well) | If applicable | If applicable | If applicable |

## 6. Required Sheets — ADU Permit (typical)

1. Site plan (1"=10' typical) — property lines, existing SFD, proposed ADU, setbacks, driveway, well/septic, Zone 0/1/2.
2. Floor plan.
3. Elevations.
4. Sections.
5. Structural (foundation/framing, lateral).
6. T24 CF1R (Energy).
7. CALGreen residential checklist.
8. Fire access / defensible space plan.
9. OWTS modification plan (if septic).
10. Well yield test (if well).
11. Utility connection details.

## 8. Differences from City of San Diego

| Topic | County | City |
|-------|--------|------|
| ADU ordinance cite | §6156 | LDC §141.0302 (ADU regs) |
| Bonus ADU program | No (just state floor) | Yes — Companion Unit / Bonus ADU program (now adjusted post-2024) |
| Pre-approved plans | Yes (§6156 program) | Yes (robust pre-approved program) |
| SB 9 practical scope | Very limited (rural) | Broader (much of City is urbanized) |
| Rural septic/well coord | Central concern | Rarely a factor |
