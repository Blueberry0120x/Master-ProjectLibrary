# Zoning & Land Use — County of San Diego (Unincorporated)

**Authority:** County Zoning Ordinance, Title 6 of the San Diego County Code of Regulatory Ordinances (last comprehensive codification 1978; amended through present).
**Enforcement:** Planning & Development Services (PDS). Counter at 5510 Overland Ave, Kearny Mesa.

> **Differs from City of San Diego:** City uses a use-based zone string (e.g., `RS-1-7`). County uses a **designator system** — 6-7 letter/number codes that each modify a separate attribute (use, lot size, height, coverage, yard, animal regs). A parcel's zoning on a plat may read `RR` + `1` + `H` + `D` + `P` + `A`.

---

## 1. The Designator System (§1000 et seq.)

Every zoned parcel in the unincorporated County carries a string of **designators** applied in this fixed order. Each designator is a separate chapter/section of the Zoning Ord with its own table.

| Position | Designator | Controls | Section | Example |
|----------|-----------|----------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Use Regulation** | Allowed/conditional uses | §1100 (base) | RR (Rural Res), RS (SFD), RM (Multifamily), A70 (Limited Ag), A72 (General Ag), C (Commercial), M (Industrial), S (Special Purpose) |
| 2 | **Animal Regulations** | Livestock/large/small animal density | §3000 | G (none), J (minor), K (light ag), L (rural), M (intensive ag), N (exclusive) |
| 3 | **Density/Lot Size** | Min lot area or DU/acre | §4000 | A = 40 ac, B = 20 ac, C = 8 ac, D = 4 ac, E = 2 ac, F = 1 ac, G = 20,000 SF, H = 10,000 SF, I = 8,000 SF, J = 7,200 SF, K = 6,000 SF, L = 5,000 SF, M = 4,000 SF, then multifamily densities |
| 4 | **Building Type** | Allowed structure forms | §4600 | A = any, B = single detached, C = single attached, D = group, E = high rise, F = mobile home, G = accessory only |
| 5 | **Max Building Height** | Height in ft | §4700 | Common: 35 ft default, 30 ft hillside overlay, 45 ft RM, varies |
| 6 | **Lot Coverage** | Max % lot covered | §4800 | Letters mapped to % (e.g., R = 40%, S = 45%, T = 50%) |
| 7 | **Front/Side/Rear Yard (Setbacks)** | Min yards | §4900 | Letter codes for setback packages |
| 8 | **Special Area Regulations** (when present) | Overlays | §5000+ | Biological Resource Core (BRC), Scenic, Hillside, etc. |

## 2. Base Use Regulations (§1100–§2900)

### Residential
- **RR — Rural Residential** (§1140): SFD, agricultural accessory, limited home occupations.
- **RS — Single-Family Residential** (§1130): SFD primary; ADU allowed; limited accessory uses.
- **RV — Variable Family Residential** (§1135): mix SFD + limited duplex by density designator.
- **RM — Multi-Family Residential** (§1136): apartments, condos, townhouses per density designator.
- **RU — Urban Residential** (§1137): higher density (village regional category).
- **RD — Multiple Use Residential** (§1138): residential + limited office/commercial.
- **RMH — Residential Mobile Home** (§1139).

### Agricultural
- **A70 — Limited Agriculture** (§1110): farming + SFD; commercial ag prep limited.
- **A72 — General Agriculture** (§1120): broader commercial ag; min lots can reach 80 ac.
- **S92 — General Rural** (§2990): very low density rural; often 40-80 ac min.

### Commercial / Industrial / Other
- **C30-C40** Commercial (Neighborhood, General, Heavy).
- **M50-M58** Industrial (M50 Light → M58 Extractive).
- **S-series** Special Purpose: S80 (Open Space), S81 (Ecological Resource), S82 (Ext. Impact Industrial), S86 (Parking), S87 (Local Recreation), S88 (Specific Planning), S90 (Holding).

### Use Matrix
The Use Regulations chapter lists hundreds of specific uses (each numbered) and tags each zone as:
- **Permitted (P)** — by right, ministerial.
- **Limited (L)** — subject to stated standards.
- **Conditional Use Permit — Major (M) or Minor (m)** — requires MUP approval.
- **Not allowed (blank).**

## 4. Setback (Yard) Designators (§4900)

Each letter code maps to a **package** of front/side/rear minimum yards. No single table; values vary by zone. Common packages applied in residential RR/RS:

| Yard | Typical (RR/RS with "H" package) | Typical Small-Lot ("V"/"W") |
|------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Front | 20 ft (from road ROW or road easement) | 10–15 ft |
| Side | 10 ft (one side can be 5 ft if other is 15 ft) | 5 ft |
| Rear | 15 ft | 10 ft |

**Corner lots:** both frontages treated as front yards for sight-triangle; exterior side yard typically 10 ft.

**Check Sheet approach:** always read the parcel's exact designator string from the Zoning Map and cross-reference §4900 tables. ZoningPdf from Accela is authoritative.

## 5. Building Height (§4700)

Default max height is tied to a letter designator. Typical:
- **"H"** = 35 ft (measured per §4705 from lowest point of natural grade within 5 ft of structure, to highest pt of roof excluding chimneys).
- **"P" / "Q"** (Hillside Overlay) = 30 ft + 15 ft stepping limits on slopes >15%.
- **RM zones** often allow 40–45 ft (3 stories).
- Cupolas, chimneys, mechanical up to 10 ft above permitted height per §6152.

## 6. Lot Coverage (§4800)

Expressed by letter code. Typical residential zones cap coverage at **40–50%** (R=40, S=45, T=50). Coverage includes all structures with roofs + covered patios >30 inches above grade.

> **Differs from City:** County generally does NOT use FAR (floor-area ratio). Bulk is controlled by coverage + height + yards. FAR only appears in a few Village overlay and Specific Plan areas.

## 7. Community Planning Areas (CPAs) & Overlays

Every parcel sits in **one of 28 adopted Community Plans**. The Community Plan adds land-use designations and may tighten (never loosen) zoning. Notable CPAs:

- Alpine, Bonsall, Crest-Dehesa, Central Mountain (Julian), Desert (Borrego), Fallbrook, Jamul-Dulzura, Lakeside, Mountain Empire, North County Metro, Otay, Pala-Pauma, Pendleton-De Luz, Pepper Drive/Bostonia, Ramona, Rainbow, San Dieguito, Spring Valley, Sweetwater, Valle de Oro, Valley Center, Julian, Rancho Santa Fe.

**Community Plan Designation** (e.g., "Rural Lands 20", "Semi-Rural 2") sets a **General Plan density cap**. The Zoning Ord designator must be equal-or-more-restrictive. If they conflict, a General Plan Amendment (GPA) is required to rezone.

## 8. Special Area Regulations (Overlays, §5000+)

| Overlay | Key Effect |
|---------|-----------|
| **Biological Resource Core (BRC)** | Triggered in MSCP / PAMA areas; development footprint restricted; biological survey required |
| **Hillside Overlay** | Slopes ≥15%; step-back height rule; grading minimization |
| **Scenic Highway Overlay** | Along SR-67, SR-78, SR-79, S-1, etc.; screening, sign controls |
| **Airport Influence Area** | AIA 1–5 around Gillespie, Ramona, Fallbrook, McClellan-Palomar; height + use restrictions |
| **Dark Sky (Light Pollution)** | Countywide but stricter within ~15 mi of Palomar & Mt Laguna observatories; fully shielded fixtures, lamp color K limits |
| **Flood Plain (FP)** | See grading-drainage-stormwater.md |
| **Resource Protection Ordinance (RPO)** | Wetlands, floodplain, steep slopes (>25%), sensitive habitat, significant prehistoric sites |
| **MSCP — Multiple Species Conservation Program** | South County Subarea Plan adopted; North County Plan in development |
| **Transit Zoning** (post-2020) | Select Village Boundaries; higher density for housing element |

## 9. Regional Categories (General Plan 2011)

The General Plan classifies land into three **Regional Categories**:

1. **Village** — urban form, infrastructure present, higher density (RS/RU/RM).
2. **Semi-Rural** — rural residential, clustered lots (RR + 0.5 to 10 ac).
3. **Rural Lands** — very low density (20–80 ac), conservation, limited ag.

Community Plans then assign specific **Land Use Designations** within these categories.

## 10. Parking Standards (§6750+)

| Use | Required Parking |
|-----|-----------------|
| SFD | 2 spaces (1 covered per garage/carport) |
| ADU (County ordinance) | See adu-and-sb9.md — often waived per state law |
| Multi-family | 1 space/studio, 1.5/1-BR, 2/2-BR+ (guest 0.25–0.5) |
| Retail | 1 per 250 SF GFA |
| Office | 1 per 300 SF |
| Restaurant | 1 per 100 SF public area |
| Medical | 1 per 200 SF |

Stall size: 9×18 standard; 8×16 compact (max 25%); tandem allowed in residential. EV charging per CALGreen.

## 11. Accessory Structure Standards (§6156 + §6150)

- **Detached accessory** (shed, garage, pool house, workshop):
  - Must sit in rear/side yard behind front setback line.
  - May reduce side/rear yard to **5 ft** if ≤16 ft high and <1,000 SF.
  - Must be ≥6 ft from primary dwelling unless fire-separated per CRC R302.
  - One-story detached ≤120 SF may be exempt from building permit (still needs zoning compliance).
- **Attached**: counts toward coverage, meets primary setbacks.
- **Home Occupation** (§6157): by right with limits on employees (0 non-resident), signage, vehicular activity.
- **Temporary Uses** (§6158): permits up to 7 days (commercial) / 180 days (residential — e.g., onsite mobile during construction).

## 13. Quick Reference — Differences from City of San Diego

| Topic | County | City |
|-------|--------|------|
| Zone string format | Designator system (6-7 codes) | Combined string (RS-1-7) |
| Primary bulk control | Coverage % + height + yards | FAR + height + yards |
| Density units | Min lot area (sq ft or ac) | DU/ac often |
| Community Plan layer | 28 CPAs | ~52 Community Plans (City) |
| ADU ordinance | §6156 | Chapter 14, Article 1, Division 10 (LDC) |
| Wildfire applicability | Most unincorp is WUI/FHSZ | Coastal/canyons only |
| OWTS/septic common? | Yes — most lots | Rare — sewer is default |
| Fire authority | SDCF/CAL FIRE contract + districts | SDFD (city dept) |
