# Fire & Wildfire — County of San Diego (Unincorporated)

**Authority (review / inspection):**
- **San Diego County Fire (SDCF)** — a County department that contracts with **CAL FIRE** for operations; covers ~1.5M acres (most of unincorporated County). sandiegocounty.gov/content/sdc/sdcfa
- **Independent Fire Protection Districts (FPDs):** Rancho Santa Fe FPD, Valley Center FPD, Alpine FPD, North County FPD, Deer Springs FPD, Julian-Cuyamaca FPD (re-formed post-dissolution), Borrego Springs FPD, Lakeside FPD, and others.
- **CAL FIRE (State)** — Enforces PRC §4291 defensible space in the **State Responsibility Area (SRA)**.

**Codes:** 2022 California Fire Code (CFC) + 2022 CBC Chapter 7A + County Fire Code (Title 9, Div 6) amendments. 2025 CFC effective **Jan 1, 2026**.

> **Differs from City of San Diego:** City has its own Fire-Rescue Department with City-specific CFC amendments. County layers SDCF + ~15 separate FPDs + state CAL FIRE, and **virtually all unincorporated parcels are in a Fire Hazard Severity Zone (FHSZ)** — so CBC Chapter 7A applies countywide. This contrasts with the City, where only canyons/fringe lots are WUI.

## 1. Responsibility Areas (SRA / LRA / FRA)

| Area | Who responds | Who reviews plans | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| **State Responsibility Area (SRA)** | CAL FIRE / contract | CAL FIRE / SDCF + County | Most of unincorporated |
| **Local Responsibility Area (LRA)** | SDCF or FPD | Same | Parcels within FPD boundaries or near incorporated cities |
| **Federal Responsibility Area (FRA)** | USFS, BLM, NPS | Federal agency | Cleveland NF, Anza-Borrego Desert State Park (not FRA technically but state park), etc. |

## 2. Fire Hazard Severity Zones (FHSZ)

CAL FIRE classifies lands as **Moderate**, **High**, or **Very High**. Nearly all unincorporated County parcels are in H or VH.

- **LRA FHSZ maps** re-adopted by **Board of Supervisors** after CAL FIRE's recommendation.
- **Triggers:** CBC Chapter 7A (WUI building materials), PRC §4291 defensible space, stricter access/water supply.

## 3. CBC Chapter 7A — WUI Ignition-Resistant Construction

Applies to new construction (and, per County amendments, substantial remodels) in any FHSZ in SRA **or** designated WUI in LRA.

### Materials Summary

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Roof** | Class A assembly (CRC R902 / CBC 705A). No Class B or combustible wood shake. Gaps at ridge/eave sealed or use listed ember-resistant ridge/eave vent. |
| **Vents (attic, crawl, under-eave)** | Listed to ASTM E2886 / SFM 12-7A-1; ≤1/8-in mesh noncombustible. No vents within 10 ft of property line without 7A listing. |
| **Eaves & Soffits** | Noncombustible (stucco, fiber cement, ½" Type X gyp) or ignition-resistant; boxed eaves preferred; open rafter tails permitted only if non-combustible sheathed. |
| **Exterior Walls** | Noncombustible, ignition-resistant (IR), heavy-timber, log, or Class A-rated. Common solutions: stucco, fiber-cement, ≥5/8" IR-rated exterior wall assembly. |
| **Windows** | Dual-pane with at least one tempered pane (exterior pane typically tempered); 20-min fire-rated also accepted. |
| **Exterior Doors** | Solid-core ≥1-3/8", 20-min, or noncombustible. |
| **Decking** | Ignition-resistant deck board (Exterior Fire-X, composite that meets SFM 12-7A-4); OR enclose underside; OR maintain 10 ft clear noncombustible zone under. |
| **Rain Gutters** | Noncombustible with leaf-screens. |
| **Appendages (trellises, fences)** | Noncombustible within 5 ft of the structure per Zone 0 ember-resistant rules (new CAL FIRE rule phasing in). |
| **Address signage** | Reflective, 4-in min, contrasting bg, visible from road (SDCF requires 6-in in some districts). |

## 4. PRC §4291 Defensible Space (100-ft)

State law, enforced by CAL FIRE in SRA and by SDCF/FPDs in LRA. County may adopt stricter.

| Zone | Distance | Treatment |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Zone 0 — Ember-Resistant** | 0–5 ft from structure | No combustible material: mulch, wood fences touching house, stored wood/combustibles, plants under windows, wood chip mulch. Use noncombustibles (gravel, pavers, stone, hardscape). (AB 3074, rulemaking in progress — enforcement ramping.) |
| **Zone 1 — Lean, Clean, Green** | 5–30 ft | Remove dead plants/grass/weeds; irrigated, well-spaced plants; no firewood storage within 30 ft of house (move to Zone 2); tree limbs 10 ft from chimney; canopy separation 10 ft. |
| **Zone 2 — Reduced Fuel** | 30–100 ft | Grass cut to 4 in max; horizontal canopy spacing per slope; vertical spacing 3× understory height; ladder fuels removed. |

Inspections: by CAL FIRE LE-100 inspectors or FPD.

## 5. Access Roads & Driveways (CFC Ch 5 + County amendments)

| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Two-way road width** | 24 ft clear |
| **One-way / hammerhead / driveway** | 16 ft clear (some FPDs: 20 ft with 14-ft vertical) |
| **Vertical clearance** | 13 ft 6 in minimum, often 15 ft trimmed |
| **Maximum grade (paved)** | 20% (some districts stricter — 15%) |
| **Max grade (unpaved)** | 15% with approved road base |
| **Turning radius** | 28 ft inside, 48 ft outside (fire apparatus) |
| **Turnarounds on dead-end drives** | Required when length >150 ft (measured from road centerline); hammerhead (min 60 ft leg each way) or 40-ft radius cul-de-sac |
| **Bridges/culverts** | HS-20 (70,000 lb) Type I fire apparatus loading; Caltrans Bridge Design Specifications |
| **Gate width** | Same as road width; knox box or approved opener; 30-ft setback from road edge for queue |
| **Surface** | All-weather paved or approved compacted aggregate (WU recommendations) |

## 6. Water Supply (CFC Appendix B/C + County)

### With Public Hydrants
- Hydrant spacing on looped mains: **500 ft** (single family on rural lots); **300 ft** in village zones.
- **Hydrant flow:** SFD with sprinklers — **500 GPM @ 20 psi for 30 min**; without sprinklers or >3,600 SF — **1,500 GPM @ 20 psi for 2 hrs** (Table B105.1(2)).
- Hydrant within **400 ft** of structure (measured along approved access path, rural: may extend).

### No Hydrants / Remote Sites (most unincorporated)
- **Onsite water storage required:** commonly **2,500–5,000 gallons** dedicated fire use.
- Cistern/tank with:
  - **4-in NH fire department connection** (some districts 2½-in gated Y).
  - Located in an accessible spot with apparatus pull-up on approved surface.
  - Gravity draft or powered — if pump, must have emergency power.
- For buildings >3,600 SF or multiple structures, volume typically scales to 10,000+ gal.
- Private pond, pool, or stream **only with approved dry hydrant** and year-round confirmed volume.

### Sprinklers (NFPA 13D)
- Required by 2022 CRC R313 for all new SFDs statewide (including County), regardless of water supply.
- **Acceptable designs:** Multi-purpose (domestic + fire), stand-alone 13D.
- **Standby volume:** 2 heads flowing for 10 min (typ 375–500 gal).
- Tank-fed 13D systems permitted where public water unavailable.

## 7. Address Identification (CFC §505)

- **Numeric address posted** at the road entrance (not just front of house) when driveway >50 ft.
- **Numerals:** 4" min height, ½" stroke, reflective, contrasting bg. Some FPDs require 6".
- **Post setback:** max 6 ft back from edge of travel lane.

## 8. Fuel Modification / Landscape Plans

For new SFDs in high-hazard areas, County Fire may require a **Fuel Modification Plan** showing:
- Zone 0/1/2 boundaries with dimensions.
- Plant species list (no "pyrophytic" species: eucalyptus, juniper, Italian cypress near structure, etc.).
- Vertical/horizontal canopy separation.
- Slopes and slope-modified setbacks.
- Maintenance responsibility.

## 9. Special Hazards & Permits

- **Hazardous materials** (any qty > CFC Table 5003): Hazardous Materials Business Plan (CERS, DEH).
- **LPG propane tank** setbacks: 10 ft from structure/property line for ≤500 gal; 25 ft for >500-2000; 50 ft for >2000; and 10 ft from ignition sources/vents.
- **Solar PV**: PV rapid shutdown (CFC §1205 / NEC 690.12); roof access pathways per CFC §1204.
- **Energy storage systems (ESS)**: CFC Chapter 12 / 1207; setback from property line; listed to UL 9540.

## 10. Fire Sprinklers in Accessory Structures

- Per 2022 CRC R313.1 / County amendment: NFPA 13D required in new SFDs.
- **ADUs**: sprinklers required only if the primary dwelling has them (Gov Code §65852.2(a)(1)(D)(ix)). If primary was built pre-2011 (when sprinklers became mandatory), the new ADU generally does NOT need to be sprinklered. County applies the state floor.
- **Detached accessory buildings** (garage, shed): generally no sprinklers if not a dwelling unit and <1,000 SF.

## 11. Quick Reference: SFD in Unincorporated County WUI

| Item | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Roof | Class A, 7A-compliant |
| Vents | 1/8" listed ember-resistant |
| Eaves | Boxed/non-combustible |
| Exterior walls | IR-rated (stucco/fiber-cement typical) |
| Windows | Dual-pane, tempered outer |
| Decks | IR lumber or enclosed underside |
| Driveway | 16 ft clear, ≤20% grade, 150-ft turnaround |
| Water | 2,500+ gal tank w/ FDC, or hydrant ≤400 ft |

| Defensible space | PRC 4291 Z0/Z1/Z2 |
| Sprinklers | NFPA 13D required |

## 12. Differences from City of San Diego

| Topic | County | City |
|-------|--------|------|
| Review agency | SDCF or one of ~15 FPDs | SDFD (one dept) |
| Scope of WUI | Effectively countywide | Canyon fringes + FHSZ only |
| Water supply default | Often onsite tank | Hydrant grid |
| Access road length to turnaround | 150 ft | 150 ft (SDFD) |
| Sprinkler pre-2011 retrofit | Same state floor | Same state floor |
