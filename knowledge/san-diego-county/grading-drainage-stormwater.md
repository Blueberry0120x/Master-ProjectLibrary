# Grading, Drainage & Stormwater — County of San Diego (Unincorporated)

**Authority:** Department of Public Works (DPW) Land Development Engineering; PDS (Floodplain, RPO); DEHQ (water quality where applicable); Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB — MS4 permit).
**Codes/Ords:**
- **County Grading Ordinance** — Title 8, Div 7, Ch 2 (§87.201 et seq.)
- **Watershed Protection Ordinance (WPO)** — Title 6, Div 8, Ch 7 (§67.801 et seq.) (adopted 2020, replaced former Storm Water Ordinance)
- **BMP Design Manual** (2020 w/ updates)
- **Resource Protection Ordinance (RPO)** — §86.601 et seq.
- **Flood Plain Ordinance** — §811.101 et seq. / FEMA NFIP

> **Differs from City of San Diego:** City has its own Grading Ord (LDC Ch 14, Art 2, Div 1) and BMP Design Manual. Thresholds and PDP triggers are similar but not identical. County has a much larger share of steep-slope and RPO-sensitive land because so much of it is undeveloped.

## 1. County Grading Ordinance (§87.201+)

### Permit Triggers

| Activity | Threshold for Grading Permit |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Cut or fill volume | **≥ 200 cubic yards** combined |
| Disturbed area (clearing/grubbing) | **≥ 2,500 SF** |
| Cut > 5 ft deep or fill > 3 ft deep | Regardless of volume |
| Any grading in **floodplain**, **RPO wetlands**, **steep slopes**, **MSCP PAMA** | Always requires permit |
| Stockpiling > 100 CY | Stockpile permit |

**Below-threshold work** still must comply with:
- Erosion control (WPO)
- Setback from property lines (5 ft typical at toe of fill, 2 ft from top of cut; refer to §87.415)
- Drainage (positive slope away from buildings per CBC R401.3)

### Permit Types

1. **Land Development Grading Permit (Major Grading)** — discretionary or large site improvements, engineered plans stamped by Licensed Civil Engineer, geotech report required.
2. **Minor Grading Permit** — typically <5,000 CY on residential lots, simpler review.
3. **Improvement Plans** — required with subdivision/TM/TPM.
4. **Clearing Permit** — vegetation removal above thresholds without cut/fill.
5. **Stockpile Permit**.

### Submittals for Grading Permit
- Grading plans (stamped CE) 1"=40' or better; 2-ft contours existing + proposed.
- Cut/fill quantity calculations; balance statement.
- Geotechnical report (slope stability, expansive soils, liquefaction where applicable).
- **Erosion & Sediment Control Plan (ESCP)** per WPO — site-specific BMPs, inspection schedule.
- Drainage study (hydrology/hydraulics) if >1 ac disturbance or downstream impacts possible.
- Haul route plan if >500 CY offhaul.

### Cut/Fill Slopes
- Max slope: **2:1 (H:V)** without special geotech; steeper requires engineer.
- Setbacks from property lines: **2 ft** top of cut, **5 ft** toe of fill (per §87.415, increases with height).
- **Benching** every 30 ft vertical on slopes >30 ft high.
- Terrace drains required at bench.
- **Slope stabilization / landscaping** within 30 days of completion (per WPO).

## 2. Watershed Protection Ordinance (WPO §67.801)

Adopted 2020; replaced legacy Storm Water Ordinance; implements MS4 NPDES permit. All development/redevelopment must comply.

### Categories

| Category | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **Non-PDP Standard Projects** | Any land disturbance — source-control BMPs + site design BMPs |
| **Priority Development Project (PDP)** | Projects above thresholds — require full structural treatment BMPs, hydromodification if applicable, long-term maintenance agreement |

### PDP Thresholds (Per BMP Design Manual Table 1-2)

| Project Type | PDP Threshold |
|--------------|--------------|
| New development (most categories) | **≥ 10,000 SF new impervious** |
| Single Family Hillside Home | ≥ 10,000 SF impervious **on slopes ≥ 25%** |
| Restaurants / Auto Repair / Retail Gas / Parking Lots | ≥ 5,000 SF new impervious (lower threshold — pollutant generating) |
| Redevelopment | If adds/replaces ≥ 50% of pre-project impervious and total impervious ≥ threshold |

### PDP Structural BMP Hierarchy
Must demonstrate ability to satisfy (in order of priority per Municipal Permit):

1. **Infiltration (INF)** BMPs — e.g., bioretention w/o liner, infiltration basin, permeable pavement w/ infiltration. Preferred.
2. **Harvest & Use (HAR)** — cisterns for irrigation, greywater, etc.
3. **Biofiltration (BIO)** — bioretention w/ underdrain, proprietary biofilters.
4. Flow-thru treatment (last resort, requires waiver).

**Design capture volume (DCV)**: 85th-percentile 24-hr storm × impervious area × coefficients.

### Pollutant Treatment Targets
- Sediment, nutrients, metals, hydrocarbons, bacteria (TMDL-dependent watershed).
- Watershed-specific "pollutants of concern" listed in BMP Manual Appx A.

## 3. Hydromodification Management (HMP)

Applies to PDPs within the **HMP Applicability Area** (Hydromod Map — essentially coastal & foothill watersheds; desert exempt).

### HMP Performance Standard
Post-project runoff duration and flow rates must match pre-project for range **0.1 × Q2 to Q10** (2-yr to 10-yr storms).

### Exemptions
- Project discharges directly to ocean, tidally influenced, or approved exempt reach.
- Sites where receiving channel is concrete-lined all the way to ocean/exempt reach.
- Interior sites with no connection to erosion-sensitive waters.

## 4. Construction Erosion & Sediment Control (ESCP)

Required on all permits (regardless of size). Must include:
- Site plan showing BMPs (silt fence, fiber roll, check dams, stabilized construction entrance).
- Schedule (rainy season Oct 1 – Apr 30 triggers enhanced BMPs).
- Inspection log — weekly + after every ≥ 0.25" rain event.
- **SWPPP (Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan)** required if **≥ 1 acre disturbance** (state Construction General Permit - WDID# filed with SMARTS).

## 5. Resource Protection Ordinance (RPO §86.601)

Protects sensitive resources even when not regulated by wetlands permit agencies (Corps §404, CDFW §1600, RWQCB §401).

### RPO-Regulated Features

| Feature | Definition / Standard |
|---------|----------------------|
| **RPO Wetlands** | Broader than Corps — includes seasonal drainages, vernal pools |
| **Floodplain (RPO)** | 100-yr floodplain — building prohibited unless exemption |
| **Floodway** | Central high-velocity channel — no new structures |
| **Steep Slopes** | Natural slopes **≥ 25%** more than 10 ft rise — limited encroachment |
| **Significant Prehistoric or Historic Sites** | Protected; survey required |
| **Sensitive Habitat Lands** | Coastal sage scrub, oak woodland, etc., per RPO definitions |

### Encroachment Limits on Steep Slopes
- **25%+ natural slope, >10 ft rise:** generally cannot exceed a formula-based encroachment area (§86.604).
- Grading allowed only for:
  - Approved development driveway/building pad with minimum cut/fill.
  - Brush management for fire safety.
  - Infrastructure needed for development of remaining lot.

## 6. Floodplain Management — County Ord §811 + FEMA NFIP

County is NFIP participant.

### FEMA Zones
- **Zone A / AE** — 1% annual chance (100-yr) floodplain; BFE (Base Flood Elevation) required for AE.
- **Zone AO** — shallow flooding; depth specified (typ 1–3 ft).
- **Zone V / VE** — coastal wave velocity (limited in County; more common City/Coronado).
- **Zone X (shaded)** — 0.2% annual (500-yr).
- **Floodway** — central high-velocity portion of Zone AE; no new structures.

### Elevation Requirements (§811.507)
- **Lowest floor ≥ BFE + 1 ft freeboard** (residential).
- **Non-residential** may use dry floodproofing in A zones.
- **Crawl space** opening requirements: ≥ 1 sq in / sq ft enclosed area, ≤ 1 ft above grade.
- **Elevation Certificate** required at FEMA-standard before foundation and at finish.

### Permits in Floodplain
- **Floodplain Development Permit** (PDS) required for ALL development in SFHA — even regrading, fill.
- **No-rise certification** required in floodway.

## 7. Drainage Design (DPW Design & Procedures Manual)

- Design storm: **100-yr** for building protection / onsite conveyance to boundary.
- **25-yr** for culverts and storm drains inlet capacity.
- **10-yr** for gutter/flowline.
- **Rational method** acceptable for small (<200 ac) watersheds; otherwise HEC-HMS.
- All runoff must leave property in **historic flow pattern or approved concentration**; concentrated flow onto neighboring property requires drainage easement.
- Pipes: RCP min Class III for public mains; HDPE allowed per DPW Standards.
- **Swales**: max 4% slope for vegetated, 8% with lining; min 0.5%.

## 8. Driveway & Road Drainage

- Cross-drainage culverts sized for 100-yr (on access roads serving residences).
- Driveway crossings of ephemeral creeks: culvert per DPW §DS-9 or dip-crossing (Arizona crossing) with approval.
- Permanent BMP at driveway outlet (velocity dissipater) if concentrated flow.

## 9. Stormwater Submittal Matrix

| Project Size | Submittal |
|--------------|-----------|
| <1 ac disturbance, below PDP threshold | **Water Pollution Control Plan (WPCP)** + site design + source control BMPs |
| ≥ 1 ac disturbance, below PDP threshold | WPCP + SWPPP (state CGP WDID) |
| PDP | **Storm Water Quality Management Plan (SWQMP)** + structural BMPs + O&M Plan + recorded BMP Maintenance Agreement |
| PDP + HMP | Above + Hydromod report & sized BMP |

## 10. Post-Construction BMP Maintenance

- Owner records **BMP Maintenance Agreement** with County.
- Annual inspection by owner; County may audit.
- Failure to maintain = code enforcement + fines under WPO.

## 11. Quick-Ref: SFD in Unincorporated County

| Project Size | Grading Permit? | WPO PDP? | HMP? | Floodplain? |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|------|--------------|
| 1,200 SF ADU on flat 1-ac lot in suburban CPA | No (if <200 CY) | No (if <10K SF impervious) | No | Only if in SFHA |
| 3,000 SF SFD on 1-ac hillside (25%+ slope) | Yes | Yes (PDP hillside trigger) | Yes (if in HMP area) | Check FIRM |
| 5,000 SF SFD on 5-ac rural flat | Usually yes (grading >200 CY) | Usually yes (>10K SF impervious w/ driveway) | Depends | Check FIRM |

## 12. Common Pitfalls

- **RPO steep-slope overlay** catches many hillside lots; plan driveway & pad carefully.
- **Driveway impervious** counts toward PDP threshold; a long rural driveway alone can exceed 10,000 SF.
- **Percolation + infiltration**: if OWTS on same lot as INF BMP — keep separated by 10+ ft and document non-interference.
- **Fire Zone 0 (0-5 ft)** ember-resistant rule limits bioretention at foundation — offset BMP from structure.

## 13. Differences from City of San Diego

| Topic | County | City |
|-------|--------|------|
| Grading permit trigger | 200 CY / 2,500 SF | 200 CY / 2,500 SF (similar) |
| BMP Manual | County 2020 BMP Manual | City 2018+ BMP Manual |
