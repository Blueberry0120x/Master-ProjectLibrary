## Item 8 — Re-Pipe Scope

**Scope:** Whole-house supply re-pipe to PEX-A (recommended over PEX-B for freeze resistance and expansion fittings).

| Zone | Lines | Est. Linear Ft |
|---|---|---|
| Main trunk (meter to manifold or water heater) | 1" cold main | ~30 ft |
| Water heater to manifold | ¾" hot trunk | ~15 ft |
| Master bath branch | ¾" → ½" at fixtures | ~40 ft |
| Kids bath branch | ¾" → ½" at fixtures | ~35 ft |
| Powder room branch (new) | ½" hot + cold | ~20–40 ft depending on location |
| Kitchen branch | ½" hot + cold to sink + DW | ~25 ft |
| Hose bibs (existing) | ½" cold | ~20 ft total |
| Garage hose bib (if any) | ½" cold | ~15 ft |
| **Total estimate** | | **~200–220 linear ft** |

**Existing material:** Unknown — field verify. Options:
- **Copper:** Cut and cap; PEX can tie in at manifold or at each branch
- **Galvanized:** Full replacement strongly recommended (corroded, restricted flow)
- **CPVC:** Replace fully — CPVC is brittle with age in CA

**PEX fitting recommendation:** Uponor/Wirsbo expansion fittings (PEX-A) preferred over crimp — more reliable at manifold, easier inspection.

**Manifold approach (recommended):** Install a home-run manifold near water heater with individual shutoffs per fixture — makes future maintenance easy and gives inspector clear system view.

## Item 9 — Water Heater Evaluation

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Electric tank (50 gal) | Lowest install cost; 240V/30A | Slower recovery; takes up space |
| Electric tankless (whole-house) | Space-saving; on-demand; rebate eligible | High amperage (150-200A); more expensive unit |
| Electric tankless (point-of-use at kitchen) | Cheaper electrical; instant hot at sink | Doesn't serve baths — still need main unit |

**Recommendation:** Single whole-house electric tankless unit if Permit 3 confirms 400A panel and electrician reserves capacity. Otherwise 50-gal tank electric is safe fallback.

**Location from floor plan:** Water heater appears in utility area between kitchen and bedrooms — visible on A02. Existing closet/alcove likely sufficient for tankless unit (wall-mount). Confirm dimensions in field.

## Plumbing Permit Plan Drawing Requirements Summary

Based on above evaluation, the plumbing plan set needs:

1. **Floor plan (proposed)** showing all fixture locations in all 4 rooms
2. **DWV isometric diagram** showing:
   - Existing main stack location
   - All branch connections (master, kids, powder, kitchen)
   - Pipe sizes: 4" main stack → 3" toilet branches → 2" shower/tub → 1.5" lav/sink
   - Vent routes: wet vent, AAV, or through-roof — designer to specify
   - Slopes: all horizontals at ¼" per foot minimum
3. **PEX supply diagram** showing:
   - Meter → main shutoff → pressure regulator → manifold → branches
   - Pipe sizes: 1" trunk → ¾" mains → ½" at fixtures
4. **Fixture schedule** (all 4 rooms)
5. **Wall-mount toilet carrier detail** (both baths) — Geberit or Kohler cut sheet
6. **Shower pan detail** (both baths) — waterproofing membrane, drain type
7. **Water heater detail** — T&P relief drain, expansion tank, location
8. **Low-flow compliance note** per SB 407
